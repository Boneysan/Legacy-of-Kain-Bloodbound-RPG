# Chapter 11: Realms, Terrain, and Arcane Power
In the shattered world of Nosgoth, the veil between the Material and Spectral Realms is thin, and the land itself pulses with ancient magic. This chapter details how your character’s Realm Affinity shapes their interaction with these realms, how Terrain Types influence movement and combat, and how Energy Foci amplify magical power. Together, these systems govern how you navigate the ruins, battlefields, and cursed places of Nosgoth, whether you’re a blood-hungry Vampire, a spectral Wraith, or a Glyph-marked wanderer caught between worlds.

## 11.1 Realm Affinity
### Overview
Every character in *Legacy of Kain: Bloodbound RPG* is tied to one of two realms—or, in rare cases, both. Your Realm Affinity determines how you perceive and interact with the world, from the tangible stone and blood of the Material Realm to the ethereal whispers and hidden pathways of the Spectral Realm. Affinity shapes your strengths, weaknesses, and tactical options, making it a cornerstone of your character’s identity.

### Affinity Types

**Material:** You are grounded in the physical world. You interact with objects and beings in the Material Realm normally, but cannot see or affect Spectral creatures or pathways without magic. Vampires, Sarafan warriors, and most mortals fall into this category.

**Spectral:** You exist primarily in the Spectral Realm. You can move through certain walls, see hidden truths, and interact with spirits—but struggle with physical objects and may require anchors (like glyphs or relics) to touch the Material world. Wraiths and certain undead are Spectral-affined.

**Hybrid:** You can shift between realms or interact with both to some extent. Hybrid characters (e.g., Wraiths with Glyph-marked traits or ancient vampires like Kain) can access both realms for brief periods, often gaining powerful tactical advantages. This affinity is rare and often comes with unique drawbacks.

> **Affinity by Lineage and Class:**
> - **Material:** Vampires, Humans, Hylden-Blooded.
> - **Spectral:** Wraiths (Levels 1–19), Revenants.
> - **Hybrid:** Unbound, Soul Reaver (class), Wraith Lord (Level 20 Paragon), and characters with specific Hybrid-granting perks or abilities.

### Mechanical Implications

- **Spectral Movement:** Spectral-affined characters can use abilities or stunts to ignore certain terrain (e.g., moving through walls or bypassing locks) at the GM’s discretion.
- **Targeting and Resistance:** Spells or damage types (e.g., **Spectral**, **Void**) affect characters differently based on affinity. For example, Spectral creatures take double damage from holy relics but may resist Material-based attacks.
- **Visibility:** Some enemies, artifacts, or pathways are only visible or interactable in one realm. A Material-affined character might walk past a Spectral glyph, unaware of its power, while a Spectral character could miss a Material trap.

### Shifting Realms
In Nosgoth, the ability to cross the veil is a powerful and dangerous gift.

- **Bonus Action Shift:** Characters with the **Hybrid** affinity or specific perks (like *Wraith Phasing*) can use a **Bonus Action** to shift their manifestation between realms. 
- **HP 0 Shift (Spectral entities only):** When a character with the **Spectral** or **Hybrid** affinity is reduced to 0 HP in the Material Realm, they do not fall **Unconscious**. Instead, they immediately shift into the Spectral Realm with 1 HP. While in this state, they cannot return to the Material Realm until they find a **Soul Portal**, **Arcane Nexus**, or use a powerful ritual.
- **Material Return:** Returning to the Material Realm requires a **Soul Portal** or a specific Energy Focus. A character must be at or above **50% of their maximum HP** (the Return Threshold) to attempt the crossing. See [Section 11.5.2](./11_Realms-Terrain-Arcane-Power.md#1152-crossing-the-veil) for full crossing mechanics.

## 11.2 Terrain Types
### Overview
Nosgoth’s landscapes are as deadly as its inhabitants. From blood-soaked battlegrounds to spectral mists, terrain shapes every encounter. Terrain Types affect movement, combat, and strategy, and their effects often depend on your Realm Affinity. Understanding terrain is key to survival, whether you’re navigating the ash-choked ruins of Meridian or the corrupted marshes of the Termogent Forest.

### Terrain Descriptions and Effects

| Terrain Type | Description & Effects |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Open Ground** | Standard terrain. No modifiers to movement or combat. Examples: clear roads, flat stone floors. |
| **Difficult Terrain** | Costs 2 movement per tile. Examples: large rubble, snow, shallow water, broken stairs. |
| **Hazardous Terrain** | May deal damage or require a Difficulty Rating (**DR**) check to cross. Examples: fire, acid pools, spectral thorns (DR 2–4, depending on severity). |
| **Elevated Terrain** | Higher ground grants +1 DR against ranged attacks and +1 success on certain **Observation** or combat rolls. Examples: hills, rooftops. |
| **Cover** | Half cover grants +1 DR, full cover grants +2 DR against ranged and spell attacks. Examples: walls, barrels, dense foliage. |
| **Obscuring Terrain** | Reduces line of sight; **Stealth** rolls gain +1 success when hiding. Examples: mist, darkness, ash clouds. |
| **Spectral Terrain** | Exists partially in the Spectral Realm. Non-Spectral entities need glyphs or special skills to interact with or move through it. Examples: ghostly bridges, spectral walls. |
| **Corrupted Ground** | At the end of each scene of prolonged exposure, causes 1 stress or minor corruption. Certain perks grant immunity. Examples: Hylden-tainted soil, cursed altars. |
| **Holy Ground** | Deals 1 damage at the start of each turn to undead or corrupted creatures. Boosts DR by 1 for holy casters or relics. Examples: consecrated shrines, Sarafan sanctums. |
| **Bloodsoaked Terrain** | Grants +1 success to **Blood Mages’** rolls; may allow one free Blood Point (**BP**) recovery each scene if fed upon. Examples: battlefields, ritual sites. |
| **Arcane Nexus** | Magical energy foci. Reduce spell costs by 1 for casters attuned to the Nexus. Rare and volatile. Examples: Pillar remnants, ancient glyphs. |

### Interaction with Affinity

- **Material Affinity:** Characters move and fight normally on Material-based terrain (e.g., Open Ground, Difficult Terrain) but may struggle with Spectral Terrain, requiring glyphs or Hybrid allies to interact.
- **Spectral Affinity:** Characters can bypass some physical obstacles (e.g., moving through walls on Spectral Terrain) but may be unable to interact with Material objects without anchors.
- **Hybrid Affinity:** Characters can adapt to both realms, gaining tactical advantages (e.g., using Spectral Terrain to ambush while retaining Material combat effectiveness).

Example: During a skirmish in the ruins of Avernus, a Blood Mage stands on **Bloodsoaked Terrain**, gaining +1 success on their spellcasting. A Spectral enemy uses **Obscuring Terrain** (mist) to hide, forcing the Mage to rely on a Hybrid ally to spot the foe.

## 11.3 Energy Foci
### Overview
An Energy Foci (plural: Foci) is a locus of concentrated magical power—a place, object, or formation where ambient arcane energy is naturally or supernaturally amplified. These Foci are often remnants of the Pillars of Nosgoth, spectral bleed-throughs, or Hylden constructs. Spellcasters can tap into Foci to enhance their magic, but doing so comes with risks.

### Mechanical Benefits

- **Reduced Costs:** Reduce spell **Soul Energy** or **Blood Point** cost by 1 (minimum 0) while casting from or within a Foci.
- **Enhanced Casting:** Gain +1 success on spellcasting rolls if attuned to the Foci.
- **Special Effects:** Some rare Foci allow forbidden or hybrid magic (e.g., spectral-blood casting) or serve as fast-travel nodes, ritual circles, or resurrection anchors in certain campaigns.

### Drawbacks or Risks

- **Instability:** Overuse can destabilize a Foci, causing magical surges, corruption waves, or temporary rifts to the Spectral Realm.
- **Contention:** Many Foci are contested by factions like the Pale Accord or Sarafan, or guarded by wraiths or spirits.
- **Corruption:** Casting forbidden spells at a Foci may trigger Corruption Threshold gains or attract ancient entities like the Elder God.

Example: **The Cradle of Glyphfire**
Deep within the ruins of Avernus lies a circular chamber inscribed with shifting runes. Those who channel magic within this Foci do so at half cost—but each casting whispers a fragment of their soul into the Spectral Realm, risking 1 stress per cast unless they succeed on a **Will** + **Ritualism** check (DR 3).

### How to Attune to an Energy Foci
To harness a Foci’s power, a character must attune to it through a ritual process.

#### Basic Method
1. Be within range of the Foci (typically 1–2 tiles).
2. Spend one full round focusing uninterrupted.
3. Make a **Will** + **Ritualism** or **Soul** + **Glyphcasting** skill check.

The Difficulty Rating (**DR**) depends on the Foci’s potency:
- **Common:** DR 2
- **Rare/Corrupted:** DR 3
- **Ancient/Forbidden:** DR 4+

#### Success Results

| Successes | Effect |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Pass DR** | Gain reduced spell cost while attuned. |
| **+1 Success** | Also gain +1 success on spell rolls. |
| **+2 Successes** | Additional bonus: e.g., restore 1 resource (**Soul Energy** or **Blood Point**) per cast or unlock a hidden Foci property. |

#### Failure & Consequences
Failing the attunement roll may:
- Inflict 1 corruption or stress.
- Overload the Foci, making it unstable (unusable for 1 scene).
- Attract attention from Spectral entities or nearby factions (e.g., Sarafan patrols).

You can retry attunement after a **long rest** or by spending a **Corruption Die**.

**Optional Rule: One Foci at a Time**
Characters may only be attuned to one Foci at a time, unless they possess a rare Foci Conduit Relic or a perk like:
*“Anchor of the Arcane”* – You may maintain two concurrent Foci attunements without backlash.

## 11.4 Integrating Realms, Terrain, and Foci
### Synergies
The interplay of Realm Affinity, Terrain Types, and Energy Foci creates dynamic tactical opportunities:

- A Hybrid character attunes to an Arcane Nexus on Spectral Terrain, casting a powerful spectral-blood spell to banish a wraith.
- A Material-affined Vampire navigates Corrupted Ground to reach a Foci, relying on a Spectral ally to bypass a ghostly barrier.
- A Blood Mage uses Bloodsoaked Terrain to recover Blood Points, then channels an Arcane Nexus to unleash a devastating spell.

### GM Advice
When designing encounters, consider:

- **Terrain Challenges:** Place Foci in Hazardous or Spectral Terrain to create puzzles or risks. For example, a Foci guarded by spectral thorns requires a Spectral or Hybrid character to access safely.
- **Faction Conflicts:** Make Foci contested objectives, with Sarafan or Pale Accord forces vying for control, forcing players to fight or sneak to attune.
- **Narrative Stakes:** Tie Foci to Nosgoth’s lore—e.g., a Foci tied to a fallen Pillar might destabilize if overused, triggering a rift that advances the campaign’s plot.

### Quick Reference Table

| Element | Material Affinity | Spectral Affinity | Hybrid Affinity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Open Ground** | Normal movement | Normal movement | Normal movement |
| **Spectral Terrain** | Cannot interact without glyphs | Can move through freely | Can interact with limits |
| **Arcane Nexus** | Can attune with effort | Easier attunement (DR -1) | Can attune and shift realms |
| **Corrupted Ground** | Takes stress/corruption | Immune to corruption | Takes reduced stress |

## 11.5 The Spectral Realm — Full Rules

The Spectral Realm is not an afterlife. It is the decayed, lightless mirror of the world you know — Nosgoth as it truly is, stripped of the living world's noise and warmth. Walls that stand solid in the Material Realm crumble here into flooded black ruins. Fire does not exist. Blood lies still and dark. The only things that move with purpose are the dead, the cursed, and those who have crossed the veil by will or by wound.

These rules govern everything that happens once a character steps — or is thrown — across that boundary. They are designed to create consistent, table-ready answers to the core questions of Spectral play: How long can I survive here? How do I get back? Can I hit that? What can I do?

> **Cross-References:** The basic Affinity types (Material, Spectral, Hybrid) are defined in [Section 11.1](#111-realm-affinity). The Wraith lineage's *Wraith Phasing* trait is defined in [Chapter 2, Section 2.3](./02_Lineages-and-Race.md#23-wraiths). The HP 0 shift rule for Spectral and Hybrid characters is in [Section 11.1](#111-realm-affinity). Spectral damage type interactions are in [Chapter 9: Combat](./09_Combat.md) and [Chapter 5: Spellcasting and Magic](./05_Spellcasting-and-Magic.md).

### 11.5.1 The Soul Economy

Existing in the Spectral Realm is not passive. Your form dissolves by degrees, and only the concentrated life-force of consumed souls keeps you coherent. This creates the core pressure of Spectral play: you must hunt to survive, and you must survive long enough to return.

#### Soul Bleed
At the **start of each of your turns** while fully manifested in the Spectral Realm, you lose **1 HP**. This loss cannot be reduced, prevented, or healed by Armor, Resistance, or abilities unless a specific rule says otherwise. It is the cost of existing in a realm that was not made for the living.

**Wraith Exception:** Wraith lineage characters have a natural anchor between realms. Their Soul Bleed rate is **1 HP per 3 rounds** instead of every turn. They belong to the Spectral Realm more than any other lineage — they simply cannot stay there indefinitely.

**Revenant Exception:** Revenants are partially anchored to the Spectral Realm by their undead nature. Their Soul Bleed rate is **1 HP per 3 rounds**, the same as the Wraith lineage. They belong to the threshold between worlds — they cannot survive the Spectral Realm indefinitely, but they dissolve more slowly than the fully living.

**Hybrid Exception:** Characters with Hybrid Affinity who shift voluntarily (via class ability or perk) do not suffer Soul Bleed for the **first 3 rounds** after crossing. After that, standard Soul Bleed applies.

**Native Spectral Exception:** Creatures with **Affinity: Spectral** printed in their Monster Manual stat block do not suffer Soul Bleed while in the Spectral Realm. The Spectral Realm is their natural state of existence — they do not dissolve there. Soul Bleed applies only to visitors: Material and Hybrid characters who have crossed the veil, and PC lineages (including Wraiths and Revenants) whose Spectral Affinity reflects a nature caught between worlds rather than true native habitation. If a native Spectral creature is somehow forced into the Material Realm and then returns to the Spectral Realm, it does not suffer Soul Bleed on that return.

#### Soul Consumption
When you reduce a creature to 0 HP while in the Spectral Realm, you may immediately consume its soul as a **Free Action**, restoring HP equal to **half the creature's TV, rounded up (minimum 1)**. You must be within 5 feet of the creature at the moment of death. You cannot consume the soul of a creature that died in a previous round.

Free-floating souls — scattered remnants the GM may place in Spectral terrain — restore **1 HP** and require a **Bonus Action** to consume. A character may consume only one free-floating soul per turn.

#### The Return Threshold
You cannot return to the Material Realm until your current HP is at or above **50% of your maximum HP**. Below that threshold, you do not have enough life-force to hold a physical form. Soul Bleed creates the pressure; soul consumption provides the means; the threshold creates the goal. You are always working toward it.

### 11.5.2 Crossing the Veil

There are two ways to cross between realms: through a **Soul Portal**, or through a **Voluntary Shift** (for those with the ability). There is also a third way — being thrown across against your will — covered in [Section 11.5.5](#1155-forced-realm-shifts).

#### Soul Portals
Soul Portals are fixed crossing points — ancient Pillar remnants, Arcane Nexuses, ritual circles, or places where the veil has worn thin from centuries of death and magic. The GM places **one to three Soul Portals** per major location. They are always visible in both realms: a faint luminescence in the Material world, a pillar of cold light in the Spectral.

**Using a Soul Portal to return to the Material Realm:**

1. You must be at or above the **Return Threshold** (50% maximum HP).
2. Spend **1 SE** and use your **Action**. This requires concentration — if you take damage before the start of your next turn, make a **DR 2 Will** save or the crossing fails and the SE is lost.
3. Roll **Will + Soul** vs **DR 2**. On a success, you materialize in the Material Realm in the space occupied by the Soul Portal (or the nearest unoccupied space). On a failure, you remain in the Spectral Realm — you do not lose the SE again; retry on a subsequent turn.

**Critical Failure on the Crossing Roll:** If you pushed the roll and the Corruption Die shows a 1 with 0 successes, the crossing partially succeeds — you materialize but are immediately **Stunned** for 1 round, disoriented by the violence of re-entry. Gain 1 Corruption.

#### Voluntary Shift (Hybrid Affinity)
Characters with Hybrid Affinity or specific abilities that allow voluntary realm-shifting use the following rules instead of the Soul Portal process.

- **Shifting into the Spectral Realm:** Costs **1 SE**, uses a **Bonus Action**. No check required unless the character is under a compulsion, **Stunned**, or **Paralyzed**.
- **Shifting back to the Material Realm:** Costs **1 SE**, uses a **Bonus Action**, and requires the Return Threshold (50% HP). No portal needed. If below the Return Threshold, the character may spend an additional **2 SE** to force the crossing — this represents burning soul-energy to compensate for physical insufficiency. On a forced crossing, roll **Will + Soul vs DR 3**; on failure, the character partially manifests and immediately suffers the Partial Shift state (see [Section 11.5.5](#1155-forced-realm-shifts)).

#### Wraith Phasing — Relation to These Rules
*Wraith Phasing* (the Wraith lineage trait) is a **brief, controlled dip** into the Spectral Realm — it lasts one turn and the Wraith snaps back automatically. It does not trigger Soul Bleed. It does not require a Soul Portal to end. It is not a full crossing.

A **full Spectral crossing** — whether from HP 0, a voluntary full shift, or a forced shift — uses the Soul Portal and Voluntary Shift rules above. When in doubt: if the shift lasts longer than the current turn, it is a full crossing.

### 11.5.3 Combat Across Realms

The veil between realms is not equally permeable to all forces. Physical matter does not cross cleanly. Soul-touching energies do. The table below is the complete authority on cross-realm combat — no GM ruling required.

#### Damage Type Interaction Table

| Damage Type | Spectral attacker vs **Material** target | Material attacker vs **Spectral** target |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Physical** (Bludgeoning, Piercing, Slashing) | Half damage, bypasses Armor | No effect — passes through |
| **Spectral** | Full damage, bypasses Armor | Full damage, bypasses Armor |
| **Entropic** | Full damage | Full damage |
| **Force** | Full damage | Full damage |
| **Fire / Cold / Lightning** | No effect — passes through | No effect — passes through |
| **Poison / Disease** | No effect | No effect |
| **Radiant / Holy** | Full damage | Full damage, +2 bonus damage vs undead |

**Reading the table:** Elemental and biological effects (fire, poison, cold) are phenomena of the living world — they do not exist on the other side. Soul-touching forces (Spectral, Entropic, Force, Radiant) cross freely in both directions. Physical damage partially crosses — a blow thrown from the Spectral side half-manifests against the living — but cannot be fully landed, and Material weapons pass through Spectral forms entirely.

> **Clarification — Incorporeal Trait vs This Table:** The table above governs true cross-realm combat: one combatant in the Spectral Realm, the other in the Material Realm. When a Spectral creature *manifests* in the Material Realm to fight — as they do in all standard Monster Manual encounters — they have partially crossed over and their printed **Incorporeal** trait applies instead. Incorporeal ("immune to nonmagical physical attacks; magical weapons affect normally") describes a partially-present Spectral creature's defense, not a cross-realm targeting rule. The two systems cover different scenarios and do not conflict. If a Spectral creature is attacked by a Material character while both are in the same realm, use the Incorporeal trait. If the attacker and target are in different realms, use this table.

#### Targeting Rules

- A **Material** character cannot see, hear, or target a Spectral creature without a Spectral trait, active glyph, or a successful **Focus + Observation** check (DR 3). Without perception, they cannot target. Area-of-effect abilities that do not require targeting still resolve normally but deal no damage to Spectral creatures unless the damage type crosses the veil.
- A **Spectral** character can always perceive Material creatures. The living world is visible from the Spectral side — dimly, as if seen through deep water — but always present.
- **Glyphs cross both realms.** A placed glyph affects creatures in both the Material and Spectral Realm simultaneously unless the glyph's text specifies otherwise. This makes Glyphwright characters uniquely dangerous to Spectral threats.
- **The Soul Reaver Blade** and any ability explicitly tagged *crosses realms* deal full damage regardless of the attacker's or target's realm, and count as both Physical and Spectral for this table.

#### Conditions Across Realms
Conditions applied by a cross-realm attack function normally if the damage type crosses the veil. Conditions applied by a damage type that does not cross (e.g., a fire trap's **Burning** condition) cannot be applied to a creature in the other realm, even if the damage partially crosses.

### 11.5.4 Environmental Mirroring

The Spectral Realm is not a different place — it is the same place, aged and decayed without the living world's maintenance. What stands in one realm often does not stand in the other. The GM should define two states for any room or location that matters: the Material version and its Spectral mirror.

#### The Mirror Rule

| Material State | Spectral Mirror |
| :--- | :--- |
| Locked door | Open doorway — locks are living mechanisms; they do not exist in Spectral |
| Intact stone floor | Crumbled ledge or flooded basin (Difficult Terrain) |
| Sealed or blocked passage | Open ruin — collapsed in life, intact in death, or vice versa |
| Living guard or patrol | Spectral echo or bound remnant, aware of both realms |
| Hidden or dormant glyph | Visible and glowing — all glyphs are revealed in the Spectral Realm |
| Flowing river or running water | Black, motionless water — Difficult Terrain for Spectral creatures, impassable for most |
| Torch or fire light source | Absent — fire does not exist in Spectral |
| Locked chest or sealed vault | Ghostly outline only — contents remain in the Material world |

#### Scouting Between Realms
A Spectral character can read the Material world's layout from the Spectral side to gather intelligence before crossing back. Make a **Focus + Observation** check:

| DR | Information Gained |
| :--- | :--- |
| DR 1 | General layout: room size, number of exits, approximate creature count |
| DR 2 | Specific details: guard positions, visible traps, locked doors |
| DR 3 | Hidden features: secret passages, concealed enemies, dormant glyphs, structural weaknesses |

This check takes a **Bonus Action** and requires the character to be stationary for that turn.

#### Darkness in the Spectral Realm
Fire does not exist in the Spectral Realm. Torches, lanterns, and any light source carried from the Material world goes dark. Spectral and Hybrid characters automatically gain **Soul Sight** — a cold perception of their surroundings that functions in complete darkness, treating all Spectral terrain as dimly lit. Material characters forcibly shifted into the Spectral Realm do not have Soul Sight and are immediately **Blinded** until they spend a **Bonus Action** attuning their senses (no check required — it simply takes a moment).

#### Time and Decay
The Spectral Realm shows Nosgoth at its truest — ancient, wounded, and rotting. Structures that were built centuries ago may stand here in full ruin, their original form still visible as skeletal memory. GMs are encouraged to use this for atmosphere and for puzzle design: the Material path is blocked, but the Spectral mirror reveals how it was built, where the hidden mechanism lies, or what the room was truly used for.

### 11.5.5 Forced Realm Shifts

Some effects hurl characters across the veil against their will. These are violent transitions — the body or soul is not ready, and the crossing costs something.

#### Forced Shift Triggers

| Trigger | Affects | Save |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Prolonged direct sunlight | Vampires and undead | DR 3 Will — failure = forced shift to Spectral |
| Holy Ground (end of each round of exposure) | Undead, Corrupted | DR 2 Will — failure = forced shift to Spectral |
| Elder God ability or Void pulse | Any creature | DR 4 Will — failure = forced shift to Spectral |
| Glyph detonation (caught in blast radius) | Any creature | DR 2 Will — failure = forced shift to Spectral for 1 round only |
| Wraith Anchor destroyed | Wraith lineage character | DR 2 Will — failure = forced shift to Spectral, gain 1 Corruption |
| Entropic Critical Hit vs a Spectral creature | The Spectral creature struck | DR 3 Blood — failure = forced into Material Realm, disoriented |

#### Consequences of Forced Shifts

**Forced into the Spectral Realm (Material or Hybrid creature):**
- Immediately lose **1d6 HP** — the shock of crossing unprepared.
- If this drops you below the Return Threshold (50% HP), you cannot return until you consume enough souls to recover. You are trapped until the economy is satisfied.
- Soul Bleed begins immediately on your next turn.
- You do not need to have previously spent SE — the shift was involuntary. No SE cost is applied.

**Forced into the Material Realm (Spectral creature):**
- Take **1d6 Radiant damage** bypassing Armor — the pain of physical form reasserting itself.
- If you have no Hybrid trait or ability that permits Material presence, you are **Stunned** until the end of your next turn.
- If you have no way to voluntarily return to the Spectral Realm, you remain in the Material Realm until another forced shift, a Soul Portal, or the end of the scene — whichever comes first.

#### The Partial Shift State
Some effects — a failed forced crossing, a disrupted voluntary shift, or certain abilities — place a character in a **Partial Shift**: one foot in each realm, belonging fully to neither.

While Partially Shifted:
- You are visible and targetable by creatures in **both** realms simultaneously.
- All incoming damage is applied normally regardless of type — you have no realm-based defense.
- You cannot use abilities tagged *Spectral only* or *Material only*.
- Soul Bleed applies at the standard rate (1 HP per turn, no exceptions).
- You may spend a **Bonus Action** and **1 SE** to stabilize into one realm of your choice, ending the Partial Shift state. If you cannot (no SE remaining), the Partial Shift persists until the start of your next turn, at which point you snap fully into whichever realm you currently have more HP-capacity to sustain (GM discretion, typically Spectral if below 50% HP, Material if above).

### 11.5.6 Spectral Action Restrictions

The following defines precisely what is and is not possible while fully manifested in the Spectral Realm. These rules apply to any character — Wraith, shifted Vampire, or forcibly crossed Human — unless a specific class ability or trait explicitly overrides them.

#### What You Can Do
- Move, take Actions, Bonus Actions, and Reactions as normal.
- Attack and be attacked by other Spectral creatures without restriction.
- Perceive all glyphs, Soul Portals, free-floating souls, and Spectral terrain features automatically — no check required.
- Consume souls (Free Action on kill; Bonus Action for free-floating).
- Use any ability tagged *Spectral* or *crosses realms*.
- Speak to other Spectral creatures normally.
- Move through barriers, walls, and obstacles up to 1 foot thick (Wraith lineage or specific abilities) or freely through Spectral Terrain as defined in [Section 11.2](#112-terrain-types).
- Use **Soul Sight** to perceive in complete darkness (see [Section 11.5.4](#1154-environmental-mirroring)).
- Scout the Material world from the Spectral side (see [Section 11.5.4](#1154-environmental-mirroring)).

#### What You Cannot Do
- Pick up, move, open, or otherwise interact with Material objects — doors, weapons, items, levers, chests.
- Use abilities requiring physical contact with Material creatures unless the ability is tagged *crosses realms*.
- Be affected by elemental environmental hazards — fire traps, acid pools, poison gas, falling debris. These phenomena do not exist in the Spectral Realm and pass through you harmlessly.
- Use **Blood Points (BP)**. BP is a physical resource tied to living blood. While Spectral, all BP costs are suspended; abilities requiring BP cannot be activated. BP is not lost — it simply cannot be spent. Your pool is intact when you return.
- Benefit from Material light sources. See [Section 11.5.4](#1154-environmental-mirroring) for Soul Sight.

#### What Requires a Check

| Action | Check | On Success | On Failure |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Partial Manifest — touch one Material object | DR 3, Will + Possession | Interact with it for 1 round | Hand passes through; no effect |
| Speak audibly to Material creatures | DR 2, Will + Possession | They hear your voice clearly, direction unknown | They hear only a faint unintelligible sound |
| Identify a hidden feature in the Material world | DR 1–3, Focus + Observation | See [Section 11.5.4](#1154-environmental-mirroring) scouting table | No information gained |
| Force a Material creature to perceive you clearly | DR 3, Soul + Possession | They can see and hear you as if you were Material | They remain unaware of your presence |

**Critical Failure on Partial Manifest:** If you pushed the roll and the Corruption Die shows a 1 with 0 successes, you over-extended across the boundary — gain **1 Corruption** as the effort tears at your coherence.
