# 03 — What the Machine Shows

The Chronoplast does not lie. It shows two or more things that are all true, simultaneously, and asks which one the party is willing to treat as real enough to act on. This is the arc's central epistemological wound: not whether the future exists, but whether the party can be honest about which future they choose to believe because it is convenient.

---

## Scene Info

- **Arc / Session:** Arc III — Session 3
- **Trigger:** The party has gained entry to the Chronoplast through paradox key, Accord mathematics, or the approach clue from Session 1. The Chronal Shard is visible, reachable, and already showing reflections.
- **Scene Type:** `revelation`
- **Est. Duration:** 5–8 min
- **Tags This Scene Can Change:** `chronoplast_access`, `chronal_shard_fate`, `wheel_exposure`

---

## Cast

| NPC | Role in This Scene |
|-----|--------------------|
| Iriane Quell | Describing the machine's structure as it runs. She knows what the inputs are. She cannot tell the party what the outputs mean. |
| Silk-Without-Moon | Present at the Chronoplast margins. They came for different reasons than the party and they are watching the reflections with open appetite. |
| Curator Thess | Present, somewhere in the deeper archive. They are here because some of what the machine shows has been curated — and the curation shows. |

---

## Opening Narration

> The Chronoplast hangs in hidden ruin like a crystal engine built to wound sequence itself. Its mirrors turn on futures no one has earned yet, and the light inside them is neither warm nor cold but exactly the temperature of consequence. The Chronal Shard sits at the focal point of the array, and the reflections it is already generating show scenes the party has not lived — except that several of them match things the party has already done in forms slightly too accurate to be guesswork. The machine has been watching longer than anyone entered here.

---

## NPC Opening Line

> **Iriane Quell**, reading instruments she has carried since Arc II: *"Both futures are fully coherent. Neither depends on a logical error or a manipulation. They cannot both be real — or rather, they cannot both be real after a decision is made. Before a decision, the machine can hold them simultaneously."* She looks up. *"I need you to see them before I tell you which one I believe was manufactured. The order matters."*

---

## Player Choices

### A — View Both Futures and Make the Choice Together

> No hierarchy. Every party member sees both and the group decides together which one is operative.

**NPC Response (Iriane):**

> *"This is the correct process and the one most likely to produce a durable answer."* She opens the full array. Both futures resolve — the desired one and the feared one — in full narrative sequence, personalized to party composition. When the reflections close: *"The one you felt less certain about is the one I believe is accurate. I'm sorry."*

**Outcome:** The party sees both futures unedited. The one that felt uncertain is Iriane's best read of the true timeline. The party must now choose whether to act on the truth or act on what they want to be true. Curator Thess's presence in the deeper chamber becomes a problem here: they have already altered at least one sequence in what the machine holds.

**Tags:** `chronoplast_access: full`, `chronal_shard_fate: recovered`

---

### B — Demand to See the Manipulated Prophecy First

> Before seeing futures, identify the external interference. Who touched this machine and what did they change?

**NPC Response (Iriane):**

> *"Moebius's modification is in the third mirror-stack. He changed the sequencing of a specific campaign event to make one faction appear to have acted earlier than they did. The practical effect is — the faction you believed acted first may have been responding, not initiating."* She pauses. *"I need you to decide whether that changes anything about an obligation you formed based on that sequence."*

**Outcome:** The party now has confirmed proof of Moebian prophecy manipulation. One prior campaign assumption — likely about who struck first in a key Arc I or II conflict — is structurally false. `wheel_exposure` advances because the Wheel was benefiting from the manipulated sequence. Curator Thess retreats deeper into the archive; they are running.

**Tags:** `chronoplast_access: full`, `wheel_exposure: broken` *(the manipulation is provably Moebius-adjacent, which exposes Wheel doctrinal dependence on fabricated inevitability)*

---

### C — Contact the Chronal Shard Before Seeing Either Future

> Skip the reflected images. Touch the Shard directly and let it show you what it decides you need to see.

**NPC Response (Iriane):**

> *"I strongly advise—"* She stops. *"You've already decided. DR 3 Will test. I recommend whoever has the fewest false memories already."*

**Outcome:** Direct contact. The Shard shows one PC a highly specific and personalized future — not a faction outcome but a personal one: what they become, what they do with power when they believe no one is watching. One Corruption. The vision is private unless the PC chooses to share it. Silk-Without-Moon watches the contact sequence with visible interest and afterward tries to get close enough to ask what was seen.

**Tags:** `chronoplast_access: partial`, `chronal_shard_fate: recovered`

---

### D — Refuse to Let the Machine Decide What Is True

> The Chronoplast shows futures. It does not adjudicate them. You will not accept its authority over which version of history is real.

**NPC Response (Iriane):**

> *"That is philosophically sound."* Very long pause. *"The problem is you still need a framework for distinguishing acted-on memory from manipulated memory absent the machine. I have notes if you want them."* She hands over an annotated reference she has been carrying since Session 1. *"This is everything I trust from my own memory since the approach. It is seven pages."*

**Outcome:** The party resolves the contradiction through Iriane's notes rather than the Shard's reflection. This is slower and produces a less certain answer — but it is an answer built on evidence rather than the machine. The Shard remains unused and its future-visions are not activated. Silk-Without-Moon has no leverage from this choice and withdraws. Curator Thess emerges from the archive, looking less alarmed.

**Tags:** `chronoplast_access: partial`, `chronal_shard_fate: divided` *(Shard knowledge is distributed across Iriane's notes and party memory rather than held in one place)*

---

## If No Clear Choice Is Made

> If the party cannot agree, the Chronoplast begins defaulting to its longest-running active query — which was set by someone who used it before the party arrived. That query belongs to Curator Thess, and its findings are the ones that most sustain the Wheel's account of what is inevitable. Default to the manipulated-first-sequence outcome without the party catching the manipulation.

**Default Tag:** `chronoplast_access: partial`, `wheel_exposure: concealed`

## GM Notes

- Iriane's "the one you felt less certain about is the accurate one" is the scene's central wound. It should be delivered quietly, not dramatically.
- Silk-Without-Moon is present and wants something, but does not force the issue here. They are evaluating, not acting. Their goal is one of the futures the machine showed — specifically, the one where a boundary collapses and they consider that liberation. Let them be visible and not immediately threatening.
- Curator Thess's retreat into the deeper archive signals that they know the party is about to find the manipulation. If the party follows Thess immediately instead of engaging with a choice, this vignette pivots into a chase for Vignette 04.
- The personal vision in Choice C should be the most honest thing the Shard does. It should be uncomfortable not because it shows evil, but because it shows something the PC would do and might not be proud of when the moment of power arrives.
