# 02 — What the Wheel Offers

The Cult of the Wheel's argument is not stupid. It is emotionally true in ways the party has earned the right to feel. This scene is a philosophy confrontation, not a cult exposure — the Wheel makes its clearest possible case for surrender, and the party must answer with more than refusal.

- **Arc / Session:** Arc III — Session 2
- **Trigger:** The party enters a Wheel-influenced settlement, shrine, or waystation where fear has been converted into a strange, genuine peace. Vicar Oren Bale presents the Wheel's case in the manner of a competent counselor rather than a recruiter.
- **Scene Type:** `confrontation`
- **Est. Duration:** 5–7 min
- **Tags This Scene Can Change:** `wheel_exposure`, `betrayal_resolved`

## Cast

| NPC | Role in This Scene |
|-----|--------------------|
| Vicar Oren Bale | Calm, intelligent, and dangerous by being sincere. He believes every word he says. |
| A defector, mourner, or refugee under Wheel care | Present as evidence, not argument. They are visibly better than they were. The party may recognize the type of person who gets to this point. |
| An ally who is tempted | One party-adjacent NPC (or PC) who has had enough of impossible decisions and finds Bale's logic more credible than they want to admit. |

---

## Opening Narration

> In the settlement, mourners sit in undisturbed calm while masked officiants speak in low voices about the relief of ending obligation. There are candles, warm bread, clean bandages, and a pointed silence where weapons would have been. Vicar Oren Bale meets the party at the door with the patience of someone who has presided over the arrival of a great many armed people and watched them reconsider, and he receives them with what appears to be genuine pleasure rather than carefully managed relief. He offers the party a seat before asking why they've come. He already knows.

---

## NPC Opening Line

> **Vicar Oren Bale**, pouring something warm without being asked: *"You are going to the machine. We know the machine. We know what it shows people."* He sits down across from the party. *"Most of them come back with one of two states. Purpose, which is rare and tends to break. Or despair, which is common and tends to ask for the same thing we offer: the end of the obligation to know what cannot be known and still choose correctly. We are simply here for when that moment arrives."*

---

## Player Choices

### A — Reject the Premise

> Knowing the uncertain future is still better than surrendering to the certain one. The Wheel's peace is a forfeit.

**NPC Response (Bale):**

> *"I don't disagree. Forfeit is accurate. What I would ask you to consider is whether the forfeit was already made before you arrived here — whether the world you are trying to save contains what you are protecting, or merely the obligation. Those are different things and they cost different amounts."* He is not rattled. He finishes his cup. *"We will still be here when you return."*

**Outcome:** The Wheel's argument is declined. But Bale's last line about "obligation vs. the thing worth protecting" should sit in the room. The party has defined themselves against surrender but not necessarily against the emotional truth that drove his argument. No tag change from the rejection itself — but if no one in the party had a visible moment of recognition, Bale notes the party's unusual resilience and treats them as a longer-term project.

**Tags:** `wheel_exposure: concealed` *(no exposure yet; Bale revealed nothing operational)*

---

### B — Ask How They Know the Machine Shows Bad Things

> If the Cult of the Wheel is stationed here specifically because of the Chronoplast, they have foreknowledge. Probe it.

**NPC Response (Bale):**

> He considers the question carefully. *"We have counseled observers for sixty years. Occasionally they return and choose to remain with us rather than return to the obligations that brought them. Occasionally they return and do not choose, but describe what they saw. The machine shows everything that matters — which means everything that could break."* He pauses. *"I would not be accurate if I told you we had no interest in what you find there. We have every interest. We would prefer you find nothing useful — and if you do find something useful, we would prefer you bring it here rather than use it."*

**Outcome:** Bale has just confirmed that the Wheel wants the Chronal Shard's intelligence — specifically, they want it buried rather than acted on. This is the first operational tell. The party now knows the Wheel is running a long-term intelligence operation around the Chronoplast. `wheel_exposure` can advance.

**Tags:** `wheel_exposure: named`

---

### C — Identify the Ally Who Is Tempted and Address Them Directly

> Pull the conversation out of philosophy and into loyalty. The ally — NPC or PC — is sitting too quietly.

**NPC Response (the tempted ally):**

> They look at whoever addresses them for a moment too long. *"I'm not agreeing with him. I just don't think he's wrong about the cost."* They mean the cost of the Chronoplast. They mean the cost of the next arc. They are not wrong about that either.

**Outcome:** The ally's doubts are surfaced and named. If the party responds with understanding, the doubt is manageable — it becomes a storyline, not a rupture. If the party responds with pressure or dismissal, the Wheel now has a lead toward the party's weakest point. Bale watches this exchange with professional interest and makes no comment. He does not need to.

**Tags:** `betrayal_resolved: unresolved` *(the ally's doubt is now a named condition — track it)*

---

### D — Accept the Wheel's Hospitality Fully, Without Reservation

> Rest here. Let the party be taken care of. You've earned it and you're not agreeing to anything.

**NPC Response (Bale):**

> *"Of course."* A genuinely warm smile. *"We only ever ask for consent to counsel. We never coerce. Rest, eat. If you want to talk about what brought you here, I am available. If you want to say nothing about anything, that is equally respected."*

**Outcome:** The party rests and recovers. Bale spends the evening circulating, making quiet conversation with each party member separately without applying pressure. By morning, at least one party member has told Bale something they considered private. The Wheel now has a small intelligence gain — nothing operational, but personal. The defector who has been cared for here watches the party in the morning with a mixture of pity and recognition.

**Tags:** `wheel_exposure: concealed`, `betrayal_resolved: unresolved`

---

## If No Clear Choice Is Made

> If the party can't commit to a stance in Bale's presence, he simply keeps talking, calmly and at length, until one of them either engages or walks out. Default to Choice A if the party exits; default to Choice D if the party stays past the natural break point.

**Default Tag:** `wheel_exposure: concealed`

## GM Notes

- Bale must feel credibly good. The scene fails if he seems sinister throughout — the emotional truth of his offer must land before the party's refusal means anything.
- The defector figure is not bait or a setup. They genuinely found peace here. The world would have to be very different for that to have been a bad choice for them personally. Let the party see that.
- Bale's confirmation in Choice B that the Wheel wants Shard intelligence buried rather than acted on is the scene's most useful discovery. It is also the one Bale offers almost willingly — because admitting the Wheel's interest is a softer reveal than being exposed through investigation, and he would rather control the frame.
- The tempted ally is a held narrative variable. Don't resolve them here. Let the party manage or mismanage the relationship going into the Chronoplast.
- Vicar Oren Bale is not the Wheel's highest authority. He reports to **Sepulcher-Mother Elain**, who does not appear in Arc III. The GM should name Elain once — casually, not dramatically — during this scene or the session surrounding it, whether through Bale using her name in passing or through a piece of Wheel correspondence visible in the settlement. This establishes her before she appears at the Heath in Arc IV V05. Her arrival should not be the first time the party hears the name.
