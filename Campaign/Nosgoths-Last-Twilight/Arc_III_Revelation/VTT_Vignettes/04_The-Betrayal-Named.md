# 04 — The Name in the Archive

The betrayal in Arc III does not arrive as a villain reveal. It arrives as an explanation. Someone the party had reason to trust has been carrying a curated lie, a necessary false memory, or a prophecy fragment shaped by Moebian interference — and they knew, at some level, and said nothing. This scene is that conversation.

---

## Scene Info

- **Arc / Session:** Arc III — Session 3 or 4 (immediately follows or closely follows the Chronoplast)
- **Trigger:** The party has identified the manipulated prophecy and traced it to a specific person in their orbit. The Contradictory Witness, Curator Thess, or a carry-forward party-adjacent NPC is now holding information that doesn't survive the Chronoplast's honest accounting.
- **Scene Type:** `confrontation`
- **Est. Duration:** 5–7 min
- **Tags This Scene Can Change:** `betrayal_resolved`, `wheel_exposure`

---

## Cast

| NPC | Role in This Scene |
|-----|--------------------|
| The Contradictory Witness (or Curator Thess) | The one who knew. They have been waiting for this conversation since the approach and they are not going to lie now that it is here. |
| Iriane Quell | Present or nearby. She discovered the manipulation before the party did and waited to see whether they would find it themselves. |

---

## Opening Narration

> The archive has the quality of a room where documents have been selected rather than collected. Every record present is present because someone decided it should be, and the records that are absent — the absent ones are the shape of whatever was decided not to survive. The Contradictory Witness is sitting in the only chair, which is not a coincidence, and they are not trying to leave. They have a different problem. They are trying to figure out how much explaining they will still be allowed to do before the party stops listening.

---

## NPC Opening Line

> **The Contradictory Witness**, before anyone else speaks: *"I would like to say — before anything else — that what I carried was not invented. Every piece of it was real at the time it was recorded. The portion I knew was shaped afterward, I knew. The portion I didn't know was shaped, I served as faithfully as my original account. I am asking you to hold the difference in mind while we continue, because if you can't, this conversation ends before I can give you what you actually need to know."*

---

## Player Choices

### A — Hear the Full Account Before Judging

> They came here and didn't run. Give them the room.

**NPC Response (Witness):**

> They begin from the point of original memory and walk forward to the point of interference. They name the Wheel operative who approached them. They describe what was changed, why the change was plausible, and what they received in exchange for silence — which is something the party will find either comprehensible or unforgivable, depending on who they've lost to this point in the campaign. At the end: *"What I held back is in the third document case. I didn't destroy it. I didn't know whether I'd have the courage to give it to the right people. Apparently I do."*

**Outcome:** The full account is on record. The Wheel operative named is a lead into the Wheel's endgame infrastructure. The party has what they need for Arc IV. The Witness's fate depends on party response, but they have not lied about anything the party can verify.

**Tags:** `betrayal_resolved: absorbed`, `wheel_exposure: broken`

---

### B — Name What Was Lost Because of the Manipulation

> You do not want the explanation. You want acknowledgment. Name the specific cost — a person, a choice, a wound that happened because the false sequence was believed.

**NPC Response (Witness):**

> They do not deflect. *"I know."* Two seconds of silence. *"I knew when it happened. I told myself the outcome was likely even without the manipulation — that naming it wouldn't have changed the decision. I do not believe that anymore."* They look directly at whoever named the cost. *"If you want to use what I know for what comes next, I am offering it. If you need to put me somewhere where I can't make more decisions like this, I understand that too."*

**Outcome:** The party gets the acknowledgment. The wound is named. What they do with the Witness after this moment is a party decision with Arc IV consequences — a broken ally is different from an absorbed one is different from a severed one. The emotion in the room is real and the Witness has offered both options honestly.

**Tags:** `betrayal_resolved: absorbed` or `severed` (party decides), `wheel_exposure: broken`

---

### C — Turn the Witness Into a Weapon Against the Wheel

> They have names, dates, and procedures. Use them to expose the Wheel's operation before it can be buried again.

**NPC Response (Witness):**

> *"I will testify to everything I know. I will name the operative. I will provide the document. I will do this in the full knowledge that I am not separable from the account — that my complicity is part of the evidence."* A beat. *"I survived the choosing. I'm not trying to survive the consequence."*

**Outcome:** The Wheel's infrastructure is formally exposed in a way that is difficult to deny by any neutral observer. Iriane's records and the Witness's testimony together constitute the most complete account of Moebian-adjacent Wheel interference that has ever been assembled. The Wheel hardens immediately — Bale does not appear in Arc IV; someone harder does.

**Tags:** `betrayal_resolved: weaponized`, `wheel_exposure: broken`

---

### D — Ask Why They Didn't Come to the Party Sooner

> Not accusation. Genuine question. What kept them silent through Arc I and II?

**NPC Response (Witness):**

> A long silence. *"Because the party you were then would have used the information to move faster and break things that needed more time to break correctly. And because I was afraid."* They hold the second reason in the air rather than retreating from it. *"Fear of what you'd do with me. Fear that I'd chosen incorrectly and would have to live with the party knowing that. Fear that the Wheel was right and that telling you sooner only produced more suffering in a different shape."* Another pause. *"All three were true at different points."*

**Outcome:** The most honest answer, which also implicates the party's own prior behavior as a factor in the silence. This does not excuse the Witness, but it complicates the moral arithmetic of the betrayal in a way that makes Arc IV's alliance decisions more fraught. The party now shares partial responsibility for an information environment that produced the lie.

**Tags:** `betrayal_resolved: absorbed`, `wheel_exposure: named`

---

## If No Clear Choice Is Made

> If the party refuses to engage — takes the document, avoids the conversation, or silences the Witness before the account is given — they get the operational intelligence but not the relational resolution. Default tag for the unspoken betrayal: `betrayal_resolved: unresolved`. It surfaces in Arc IV through the Witness's behavior, not through any scene the party controls.

**Default Tag:** `betrayal_resolved: unresolved`

## GM Notes

- The Contradictory Witness is not the villain of Arc III. They are an ordinary person who made a decision under pressure and then lived inside it for too long. The betrayal should feel like that — comprehensible, costly, and human.
- Iriane Quell knew before the party did and stayed quiet. If the party notices this and asks why she didn't say something sooner, she says: *"Because you were carrying enough. I was wrong. That is also on record."* Let that land without turning it into another antagonism.
- The GM should have decided by this scene what specific thing was manipulated and why it mattered. The most useful targets: the sequence of who attacked first in a key Arc I conflict (generating a wrong guilt), a prophecy fragment that told the party someone was safe when they were not, or a future-vision that was manufactured to send the party north in a specific posture.
- Do not make the Witness a villain in retrospect. Being a person who did a comprehensible wrong thing under pressure and is now offering everything they have to correct it is a more difficult moral category than villain, and Arc III is exactly the right place for it.
