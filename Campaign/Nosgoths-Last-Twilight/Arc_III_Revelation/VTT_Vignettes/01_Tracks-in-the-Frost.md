# 01 — Tracks in the Frost

The contradiction from Arc II does not wait for a convenient moment. This scene fires the moment the party enters time-scarred territory and encounters matter they expected to find ahead of them already behind them, or memory that the rest of the group insists hasn't happened yet. The ground here has stopped agreeing with itself, and the party's first task is not to panic.

---

## Scene Info

- **Arc / Session:** Arc III — Session 1
- **Trigger:** The party enters a Moebian ruin, time-scarred pass, or dead observatory on the approach to the Chronoplast. The Arc II contradiction arrives in concrete physical form and demands interpretation.
- **Scene Type:** `revelation`
- **Est. Duration:** 4–6 min
- **Tags This Scene Can Change:** `chronoplast_access`, `betrayal_resolved`

---

## Cast

| NPC | Role in This Scene |
|-----|--------------------|
| Iriane Quell | Present, exhausted, and the most qualified person here to explain what is wrong with the site temporally. She has been here before — she thinks. |
| A Wheel priest or mourner | Not hostile. Present at the settlement or shelter adjacent to the ruin, watching the party arrive with calm that feels purchased. |

---

## Opening Narration

> The road to the Chronoplast runs through ruins where clocks move but shadows do not. Ice covers stonework laid in a style that is centuries wrong for this location, and the tracks in the frost ahead of the party were made by boots that match no member of the group — except that when someone examines the formation, the stride length is exact. You have been here before. There is no version of events where that is true. There is also no version where the tracks are mistaken.

---

## NPC Opening Line

> **Iriane Quell**, studying the tracks without touching them: *"The first thing you will want to do is establish which of your memories are reliable. I would counsel strongly against using each other to confirm that."* She pauses. *"Most of my own memories since the approach have a seam in them. Something was placed after the fact. I cannot tell you which ones."*

---

## Player Choices

### A — Focus on the Arc II Contradiction Immediately

> The contradiction is why you are here. Surface it now, in full, in front of Iriane. Let her tell you what it means.

**NPC Response (Iriane):**

> She listens to the full account without interrupting. Then: *"If both versions were experienced directly and neither is fabricated, the Chronoplast's influence extends to the recording stage, not just the recall stage. That means memory here is an active medium, not a passive one."* She writes something down. *"Which of the two versions did you act on afterward? Because the one you chose to act on is probably the one that is more real — not because it is truer, but because you made it load-bearing."*

**Outcome:** Iriane provides the party's first analytical framework for navigating Arc III: acted-on memory carries more evidential weight than raw recollection. The contradiction remains unresolved but is now workable. `chronoplast_access` improves from impossible to possible.

**Tags:** `chronoplast_access: partial`

---

### B — Investigate the Tracks Before Doing Anything Else

> Someone walked this path already. Find out who, when, and whether they survived.

**NPC Response (Iriane):**

> *"That is a reasonable instinct."* She falls into step beside whoever is leading the track investigation. *"I should mention that I found tracks matching my own boots the last time I was here. They were two hours ahead of me. I stopped following them after the first bend."* She says this as a warning, not a story.

**Outcome:** The party discovers the tracks are their own — specifically, they match three of the party members' boot sizes and walking paces from the approach road. Ahead of them in time-sequence, by somewhere between two hours and two years. The question of whether following the tracks is useful or suicidal is left open. Iriane's note about stopping at the first bend suggests there is an answer behind the bend she chose not to know.

**Tags:** `chronoplast_access: partial`

---

### C — Trust Nothing From This Site and Treat All Observed Data as Contaminated

> You know this place manipulates. The only safe response is to treat everything here as unreliable until you're outside it.

**NPC Response (Iriane):**

> *"That is internally consistent. The problem is that even your decision to distrust everything is data the site can read and respond to."* A pause. *"Full skepticism is a kind of compliance with sequenced environments. The Chronoplast can work with absolute uncertainty as easily as belief."*

**Outcome:** The party operates correctly but slowly. They do not make early errors. They also do not gather early intelligence. The Wheel priest's calm becomes more visible because the party is paying attention to nothing they trust — and the priest is notably the one stable thing in the site. This choice feeds Wheel exposure risk.

**Tags:** `chronoplast_access: partial`

---

### D — Ask the Wheel Priest What They Are Doing Here

> The calm person in a place where nothing is calm is always the most important person in the room.

**NPC Response (Wheel priest):**

> *"We come where the turning is visible."* Warm smile. *"The machine ahead shows futures people were not designed to carry. We offer relief from that weight. You will want us before you leave this place, even if you don't want to want us."* He does not follow. He does not need to.

**Outcome:** The Wheel has announced its presence and its function openly. The party now knows the cult is working the approach path and offering doctrinal surrender to people who have already seen too many futures. `wheel_exposure` cannot be triggered yet — the Wheel has done nothing visible to expose — but the party is now informed rather than surprised in Session 2.

**Tags:** `betrayal_resolved: unresolved` *(the priest's calm suggests they know something about someone in the party)*

---

## If No Clear Choice Is Made

> If the party moves into the ruin without engaging the contradiction or the tracks, the site moves without them: Iriane walks into the ruin ahead, the tracks multiply, and the contradiction surfaces during the first shelter stop in the form of one party member insisting an event happened that two others flatly deny. Default to Choice A's outcome, applied under tension rather than in controlled dialogue.

**Default Tag:** `chronoplast_access: partial`

## GM Notes

- The tracks in the frost should physically match specific party members but not all of them. The GM should decide before the scene which three — and use those three to seed which party members are most chronologically entangled with the Chronoplast.
- Iriane's seam-memory line should land as the scene's most disturbing moment. She is not alarmed. That is the alarming part.
- The Wheel priest should feel like a relief structure, not a cult. Friendliness, no coercion, no dark implication beyond the factual accuracy of "you will want us before you leave." Let the party underestimate what that means.
- Do not resolve the contradiction in this scene. The scene's job is to make the contradiction legible and give the party a language for navigating it. Resolution is Session 3.
