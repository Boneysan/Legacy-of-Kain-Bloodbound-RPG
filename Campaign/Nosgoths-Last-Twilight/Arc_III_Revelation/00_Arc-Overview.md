# Arc III: Revelation

## Arc Purpose

Arc III turns the campaign from territorial struggle into interpretive warfare. The Chronoplast, the Cult of the Wheel, and the Chronal Shard force the party to decide which truths are usable and which are traps. The arc should begin because Arc II produced an impossible contradiction: the public northern record says Hylden pressure or party breach logic drew first blood at the Resonance Pillar, while the corrected record shows a Sarafan containment volley under Scout-Confessor Maelin Rook's authority hit the Accord working ring first. By arc end, the party should have either committed to one operative truth the Heart can use or accepted the structural weakness that comes from refusing to do so.

## Level Band

- Recommended levels: 11-15
- Target length: 5-7 sessions

## Core Locations

- The Chronoplast
- Time-scarred ruins or archive chambers linked to Moebius
- Deep Spectral crossings
- Hidden cult sites where inevitability is preached as mercy
- Political ground under Kain's shadow, whether or not he appears directly

## Active Factions

- Cult of the Wheel
- Crimson Fracture, primarily through the Ash-Glass Procession and allied operatives it leaves behind
- Kain and those acting in his wake
- Pale Accord chapters pursuing time knowledge
- Remaining factions trying to weaponize prophecy fragments

## Session Beats

1. The party enter a zone where time and memory no longer agree.
2. They arrive carrying the corrected northern breach record from the Resonance Pillar, and it must be tested against the Chronoplast.
3. The Cult of the Wheel offer surrender disguised as wisdom.
4. The Chronal Shard reveals at least two mutually exclusive futures, one of them Reaver-shaped and wrong in a way the Wheel notices.
5. A betrayal resolves around the reversed Resonance Pillar first-blood sequence in a way that clarifies one arc while complicating the endgame.
6. The party leave knowing what the Heart can do, and either carrying one operative truth into Arc IV or paying for the lack of one.

## Questions To Force

- Is freedom still freedom if purchased through devastation?
- Which memory is the party treating as proof because it is emotionally convenient, especially if it preserves the false northern first-blood account?
- What does each character fear more: losing choice, or being responsible for choice?

## Milestone Triggers

- The party recover, destroy, or lose the Chronal Shard.
- The Wheel's agenda is exposed in a way the party can no longer ignore.
- The campaign's final coalition lines begin to harden.
- The party commit to one operative truth, or explicitly enter Arc IV without that anchor.

## Carry Forward

- Which future each PC believes is real enough to act on.
- What Kain now believes about the party.
- Which faction has begun preparing for the endgame openly rather than covertly.
- Whether the corrected northern breach record is absorbed, weaponized, contained, or denied.
- Whether the Kain contract enters Arc IV as Open, At Risk, or Closed.
- Who carries the Last Witness role into Convergence.
