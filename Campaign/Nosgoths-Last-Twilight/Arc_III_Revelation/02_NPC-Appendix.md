# Arc III NPC Appendix

Use these as the recurring cast for Revelation. Everyone in this appendix should be interpreted through uncertainty, contradiction, or incomplete memory.

## Iriane Quell

- Role: Pale Accord chronologer and Chronoplast specialist
- Public face: Exhausted genius who can still describe the machine more clearly than anyone else alive
- Hidden truth: Iriane no longer trusts her own memory of why she first sought the Chronoplast
- Wants from the party: Protection, access, and witnesses who can tell her when she has contradicted herself again
- Best use: The expert who can explain structure without being able to promise meaning
- If lost: Leave behind notebooks full of correct equations and deeply suspect conclusions

## Vicar Oren Bale

- Role: Cult of the Wheel officiant
- Public face: Gentle counselor preaching mercy through inevitability
- Hidden truth: He genuinely believes surrender spares people from worse tyrannies, including the party's potential one
- Wants from the party: Acceptance, delay, and one moment of honest despair he can turn into compliance
- Best use: Make the Wheel's philosophy emotionally credible, not merely sinister
- If lost: Keep his teachings alive in communities he already influenced

## Silk-Without-Moon

- Role: Crimson Fracture operative and Chronoplast-side rival or adjunct to the Ash-Glass Procession
- Public face: Elegant predator who speaks of freedom as if civilization were a skin disease
- Hidden truth: Silk wants collapse not only because it believes in it, but because it no longer remembers how to live outside crisis; it sees Reth's Procession as useful mass conversion, but cruder than its own style
- Wants from the party: One proof that boundaries can be broken and one ally willing to call that liberation
- Best use: The most seductive false answer in Arc III, giving the Fracture both a roaming field front and a polished ideological knife
- If lost: Let Procession converts inherit the rhetoric but lose the charisma

## Curator Thess

- Role: Moebian remnant, archivist, or guardian of manipulated records
- Public face: Precise keeper of ordered history
- Hidden truth: Thess has spent years preserving lies because the lies were useful long after their author died
- Wants from the party: Protection from destruction long enough to determine which version of history survives
- Best use: The bureaucrat of prophecy fraud
- If lost: The records remain, but become harder to interpret and easier to misuse

## Black Marshal Sevran

- Role: Kain-aligned intermediary
- Public face: Rare messenger who speaks like every sentence has already been weighed for consequence
- Hidden truth: Sevran does not know Kain's full design either, only the direction of his pressure
- Wants from the party: Judgment without self-pity, movement without paralysis
- Best use: Put Kain's philosophy onstage without overusing Kain himself
- If lost: Reserve Kain's next intervention for a truly pivotal scene

## The Contradictory Witness

- Role: Flexible betrayal or memory-resolution figure
- Public face: Ally, refugee, archivist, or guide who should be trustworthy by every normal standard
- Hidden truth: They are carrying a curated prophecy, false memory, or necessary lie
- Wants from the party: Forgiveness, silence, or the chance to explain before being judged
- Best use: Personalize the arc's central question of whether truth and usefulness are still allies
- If lost: Their records or spectral echo should still force resolution
