# Arc III Session Prep

Use this file as a rolling prep sheet for Sessions 1-5 of Revelation.

## Arc III Defaults

- Core fragment: The Chronal Shard
- Core pressure: prophecy manipulation, incompatible truths, temporal fallout, Wheel coercion
- Core factions: Cult of the Wheel, Crimson Fracture, Pale Accord, Kain's orbit, Moebian remnants
- Kain usage: testing the party's judgment rather than rewarding their loyalty
- Recurring cast: see 02_NPC-Appendix.md
- Detailed encounter planning: see 03_Encounter-Guide.md
- Entry requirement: begin with a concrete contradiction carried out of Arc II, not a generic lead
- Fracture continuity: the Ash-Glass Procession should arrive here as a remembered threat, a pursuing convert-network, or a fresh wound-site congregation rather than a brand-new concept.

## Session 1

- Purpose: Bring the party into a time-scarred environment where certainty fails immediately.
- Opening image: The road to the Chronoplast runs through ruins where clocks move but shadows do not, and the party can see tracks in the frost that were made tomorrow.
- Primary scenes:
	- The party enter a zone where maps, memory, and sequence stop agreeing, either through a ruined Moebian site, a time-scarred pass, or a dead observatory tied to the Chronoplast.
	- Bring the Arc II contradiction onstage immediately: a witness recalling two incompatible breach outcomes, a written report dated after its author's death, or a wound that only appears in one sequence of memory.
	- Early scenes should make ordinary planning fail: allies remember different versions of the last camp, distances shift, and cause seems to follow effect.
	- The party gather fragments of who has been here before them: Wheel agents, Accord seekers, Fracture operatives, or one of Kain's intermediaries.
	- A path to the Chronoplast emerges only after the party accept that information here must be weighed, not trusted.
	- Someone or something in the opening site should confirm that the contradiction is real, but not in the form the party assumed.
	- End the session when the party understand they are already inside manipulated history rather than merely approaching it.
- Faction moves:
	- Cult of the Wheel: Begin with subtle control, not open attack. They want the party uncertain, tired, and receptive to inevitability.
	- Pale Accord: Competing scholars or agents want chronal evidence secured before it can be contaminated further.
	- Crimson Fracture: Ash-Glass Procession operatives are present to exploit rupture, panic, and realm instability rather than hold territory.
	- Kain's orbit: Use an intermediary, omen, or impossible message to show the party are being measured.
- Relic pressure:
	- The Chronal Shard is not yet visible, but its influence already produces false continuity, contradictory recollection, and glimpses of near futures.
	- One scene should make the party doubt a fact they previously treated as fixed.
	- Someone in the group should remember an event that the others insist has not happened yet.
- Mandatory NPCs:
	- One Accord chronologer or archivist who is brilliant, exhausted, and already half lost to chronology.
	- One Wheel priest, mortician, or counselor who presents surrender as wisdom rather than surrender.
	- One messenger or trace tied to Kain, Moebius, or both.
- Encounter slot:
	- Preferred structure: unstable terrain and interpretation pressure before direct combat.
	- Best use: a time-loop skirmish, paradox ambush, or defensive retreat through a site that repeats portions of itself.
	- If combat happens, let enemies benefit from sequence disruption rather than raw power alone.
- Spectral intrusion:
	- The Spectral shows overlapping timelines as architecture layered in the same space.
	- Dead versions of still-living NPCs may appear briefly and react as if prior scenes already occurred.
	- One PC catches their own reflection arriving late to a conversation they have not had yet.
- End-of-session fallout:
	- The party gain access to the approach route, archive key, or paradox clue needed to reach the Chronoplast.
	- Wheel Compliance Clock should advance if the party let despair rhetoric settle unchallenged in a vulnerable settlement or camp.
	- The party should now distrust at least one memory they were relying on.
- Prep notes:
	- Write down one false memory to seed early and one true memory the party will wrongly doubt.
	- Keep the first session disorienting, not incoherent.
	- Avoid using temporal weirdness to erase consequence; it should sharpen consequence, not nullify it.

## Session 2

- Purpose: Let the Cult of the Wheel make its strongest ideological offer before violence becomes the easier answer.
- Opening image: In a settlement that should have been abandoned, mourners sit in perfect calm while masked officiants speak of relief, order, and the mercy of accepting one's place in the turning.
- Primary scenes:
	- The party enter a Wheel-influenced refuge, shrine, or waystation where fear has been transformed into obedience.
	- The Cult of the Wheel make a case rather than a threat: history is pain, prophecy is burden, and resistance is merely feeding another tyrant.
	- The party investigate whether this peace is chosen, coerced, or already post-human.
	- One NPC the party might otherwise trust sincerely believes surrender is the least harmful answer.
	- The session ends when the party reject, accept, or partially bargain with a philosophy that will follow them into the endgame.
- Faction moves:
	- Cult of the Wheel: Offer hospitality, absolution, burial of secrets, and release from impossible decisions.
	- Pale Accord: Oppose the cult's doctrinal certainty, but may underestimate its emotional appeal.
	- Crimson Fracture: The Ash-Glass Procession uses the cult's passivity as proof that civilization is another prison.
	- Kain's orbit: Keep distant. If Kain appears here at all, it should be only as a contradiction to inevitability.
- Relic pressure:
	- The Chronal Shard's approach makes manipulated prophecy easier to detect but harder to dismiss.
	- One Wheel ritual or sermon should reference a future scene the party have not reached yet.
	- The party should discover that some predictions are not foresight but manufactured compliance.
- Mandatory NPCs:
	- A Wheel officiant who is calm, intelligent, and more dangerous for being sincere.
	- A defector, mourner, or refugee who has found comfort in submission.
	- One ally who is tempted by the cult's logic for understandable reasons.
- Encounter slot:
	- Preferred structure: social confrontation with the risk of sudden collapse into violence or revelation.
	- Best use: expose a compromise, rescue someone marked for ritual quieting, or stop a burial rite that is also memory suppression.
	- If combat occurs, make it morally uncomfortable rather than tactically routine.
- Spectral intrusion:
	- The dead here are unusually calm, but that calm should feel purchased.
	- A masked officiant may have no consistent face in the Spectral.
	- One confession spoken aloud causes the room to replay a different version of the same grief.
- End-of-session fallout:
	- The party gain proof that the Wheel's agenda is active, persuasive, and embedded in relief structures rather than only in hidden cult cells.
	- Wheel Compliance Clock should likely advance to 1 or 2 depending on how much influence the cult retains.
	- The party leave with a sharper ideological split about whether certainty is always a trap.
- Prep notes:
	- Let the cult's argument contain real emotional truth.
	- Avoid cartoon villainy. The danger is that surrender sounds reasonable.
	- Seed one future phrase, image, or prophecy that returns in Session 5.

## Session 3

- Purpose: Put the Chronal Shard in reach and reveal multiple outcomes that cannot all be true.
- Opening image: The Chronoplast hangs in hidden ruin like a crystal engine built to wound sequence itself, its mirrors turning on futures no one has earned yet.
- Primary scenes:
	- The party gain entry to the Chronoplast through paradox key, Accord mathematics, Kain's clue, or a risk they cannot fully quantify.
	- They witness the Chronal Shard indirectly first, through reflected outcomes and incompatible scene fragments.
	- Each PC sees at least two plausible futures: one desired, one feared, and neither safely provable.
	- The party identify where a major prophecy, memory, or campaign event was manipulated by an external hand.
	- The session ends with the Shard in reach and trust inside the group under severe strain.
- Faction moves:
	- Cult of the Wheel: Attempt to seize interpretive control, not merely the artifact.
	- Pale Accord: Want exhaustive observation before contact.
	- Crimson Fracture: Seek proof that collapse of boundary and chronology is liberation, ideally in forms the Ash-Glass Procession can preach beyond the Chronoplast.
	- Kain or Kain's proxy: Push the party toward judgment rather than certainty.
- Relic pressure:
	- First direct contact requires a DR 3 Will or Focus test.
	- Failure inflicts 1 Corruption and one false memory accepted as true until challenged in play.
	- Deliberate use costs 2 Corruption and risks paradox fallout or lost time.
	- The Shard can reveal two or more mutually exclusive outcomes of a major campaign decision and identify manipulated prophecy.
- Mandatory NPCs:
	- An Accord operator who can explain the machine but not the meaning.
	- One Fracture operative or zealot who reads instability as invitation.
	- One figure tied to Moebius or a lost timeline.
- Encounter slot:
	- Preferred structure: relic contact under unstable reality.
	- Use mirror selves, paradox guardians, collapsing sequences, or a contested claim while the Chronoplast destabilizes.
	- If combat is light, the real encounter should be interpretive and divisive.
- Spectral intrusion:
	- Material and Spectral layers stop separating cleanly.
	- Dead companions from futures that never happened may appear as if they expect to be remembered.
	- A door, bridge, or stair should exist only in one timeline unless the party make it real.
- End-of-session fallout:
	- The party obtain, partially contact, or lose immediate control of the Chronal Shard.
	- At least one player-facing truth becomes suspect.
	- Everyone leaves with a different account of what the machine showed.
- Prep notes:
	- Decide in advance which futures are lies, which are possible, and which are true but incomplete.
	- Do not over-explain. Let contradiction do work.
	- Give the party a reason to keep moving even after certainty collapses.

## Session 4

- Purpose: Resolve a betrayal, contradiction, or false memory in a way that hardens endgame lines.
- Opening image: The same corridor is encountered twice in one hour, but one version still contains an ally and the other contains the proof that ally already betrayed them.
- Primary scenes:
	- The consequences of Session 3's visions break into the present through accusation, contradiction, or revealed manipulation.
	- A betrayal resolves, but not necessarily in the direction the party expected; a liar may become an ally, a prophet may be exposed as curated, or a trusted companion may prove to have hidden the right truth for the wrong reason.
	- The party test which memories and prophecies can survive corroboration.
	- Kain, a Kainite intermediary, or an anti-Moebian actor may intervene briefly to force choice over paralysis.
	- The session ends when one endgame line hardens publicly or inside the party.
- Faction moves:
	- Cult of the Wheel: Exploit contradiction to push despair and compliance.
	- Crimson Fracture: Use broken trust as proof that structure itself is fraud.
	- Pale Accord: Try to preserve evidence even as interpretation becomes politically dangerous.
	- Kain's orbit: Demand that the party act without perfect proof.
- Relic pressure:
	- The Chronal Shard now exposes inconsistencies in witness, prophecy, and motive.
	- Use of the artifact should deepen mistrust even while clarifying manipulation.
	- One apparently impossible contradiction must be resolved this session.
- Mandatory NPCs:
	- The betrayer, false witness, or manipulated ally
	- One person who still believes in inevitability
	- One person who now refuses all prophecy entirely
- Encounter slot:
	- Preferred structure: confrontation under fractured memory.
	- Best use: fight through contradictory terrain states, protect a witness across unstable chronology, or interrupt a rite built on false remembrance.
	- If combat is avoided, the confrontation must still cost a relationship, alliance, or belief.
- Spectral intrusion:
	- Spectral echoes may support different versions of the same event.
	- One place or person should visibly carry two histories at once.
	- A PC may hear themselves deny a choice they have not made yet.
- End-of-session fallout:
	- The party know more clearly who manipulated what, but certainty about the future remains impossible.
	- Endgame alliances begin hardening.
	- At least one bond inside the party or with a major NPC is permanently altered.
- Prep notes:
	- Make the betrayal specific, not abstract.
	- Avoid making everyone equally wrong; some truths must stand.
	- Use Kain sparingly, but if he appears, let him change the temperature of the arc.

## Session 5

- Purpose: Exit the arc with a shared understanding of what the Heart can do, even if the party disagree on what should be done.
- Opening image: The Chronoplast quiets only after the party choose what to carry away from it, and the silence is more alarming than the machine's noise ever was.
- Primary scenes:
	- The party consolidate what the Chronal Shard has taught them about the Heart, prophecy, and manipulated history.
	- They must decide whether to keep, hide, share, or partially destroy the Shard and what version of its truth enters the wider campaign.
	- Final negotiations, chases, or limited confrontation resolve who leaves with leverage.
	- The party identify the cost of entering the endgame: what must now be protected, what must now be confronted, and what cannot be unknowable any longer.
	- The session ends pointing unmistakably toward Convergence, not merely further travel.
- Faction moves:
	- Cult of the Wheel: Try one last time to bury, own, or interpret the Shard into harmlessness.
	- Crimson Fracture: Seek to exploit the revelation that boundaries and futures are mutable.
	- Pale Accord: Want a record, not just a result.
	- Kain's orbit: Judge whether the party have become actors rather than reactants.
- Relic pressure:
	- The Chronal Shard's main value now is strategic: it clarifies that the Heart can break, remake, or redirect the world's metaphysical order.
	- Any direct use should carry narrative strain, Corruption, and memory cost.
	- The party should leave knowing enough to act, but not enough to feel safe.
- Mandatory NPCs:
	- One surviving faction representative from the arc's main ideological poles
	- One witness or archivist who can carry the truth onward
	- One figure the party now cannot fully trust but cannot ignore
- Encounter slot:
	- Preferred structure: final choice under pressure rather than a large final battle.
	- Use limited combat, pursuit, or timed extraction if needed, but keep the climax interpretive and strategic.
	- If battle happens, it should hinge on who controls meaning, not only the artifact.
- Spectral intrusion:
	- Close the arc with an omen of layered futures collapsing toward one narrowing path.
	- A dead or unrealized figure should acknowledge the party's choice as if history has started noticing them back.
	- End on a time-tinged sign pointing toward Convergence.
- End-of-session fallout:
	- Arc III ends with the Heart's true scale understood enough to divide the party from their enemies in clear philosophical lines.
	- Update the tracker for Wheel influence, Fracture escalation, and any Kain-aligned or Accord-aligned obligations.
	- The party move into Arc IV carrying not just relics, but positions.
- Prep notes:
	- Make disagreement inside the party playable rather than destructive.
	- End on commitment, not just revelation.
	- The arc's final answer should be "now decide," not "now you know everything."

## Compact Templates

### Chronoplast Claim Assault

- Use in Session 3 or 4 when the Crimson Fracture tries to turn Chronoplast instability into a preachable revelation.
- Baseline enemy force for a level 11 party with TV 44: 1 Fracture Void-Touched (TV 12), 2 Fracture Dimension-Walkers (TV 18 total), and 2 Fracture Zealots (TV 10 total), for total TV 40.
- For levels 13-15, add 1 more Dimension-Walker for total TV 49, or add one active dimensional seam that deals 1 Spectral damage at the start of each turn to non-phased creatures standing on exposed catwalks.
- Terrain: mirrored galleries, collapsing sequence-bridges, and one contradiction archive that can be sealed with a DR 4 Focus or Glyphcasting check to deny the Fracture an easy proof-text.
- Tactics: the Void-Touched enters after the Zealots and first Walker are already contesting space, uses Merge Protocol or Commanding Roar to widen pressure, and lets the Walkers harry anyone trying to carry a witness, key, or shard reading out of the room.
- Win condition: preserve one usable contradiction, witness, or archive key while preventing the Fracture from escaping with a clean "the future is already broken" spectacle.
- Complication twist: every round the party leave the contradiction archive uncontrolled, choose one consequence: a false memory spreads to an ally, a witness begins recalling the wrong version of events, or a Procession convert gains rhetoric that should matter in Arc IV.
