# Arc II NPC Appendix

Use these as the default recurring cast for Ascension. If Arc I outcomes removed or changed one, swap role first and name second.

## Marshal Kaev Turel

- Role: Turelim claimant to Dumah's Fortress
- Public face: Disciplined fortress warlord arguing that command must be visible, feared, and stable
- Hidden truth: He believes legitimacy is mostly something people recognize after enough decisive victories
- Wants from the party: Public alignment, military endorsement, or at least refusal to back his rivals
- Best use: The strongest claimant who may also be the most dangerous long-term ruler
- If lost: Let one of his captains inherit his methods without his restraint

## Steward Vaul Drath

- Role: Dumahim steward, regent, or claimant by continuity
- Public face: Reserved inheritor of broken authority, speaking for memory, endurance, and insult not yet repaid
- Hidden truth: He needs the fortress validated as much as he needs to rule it; without the walls, he is mostly grievance and ritual
- Wants from the party: Recognition that inheritance and endurance still matter in Nosgoth
- Best use: The claim of legitimacy through continuity rather than conquest
- If lost: Make the Dumahim cause more embittered and less governable

## Ledger-Master Corin Vey

- Role: Outlands Guild factor assigned to fortress supply reality
- Public face: Calm negotiator who knows where food, arrows, and mercenaries actually come from
- Hidden truth: He can profit from either stability or siege, provided he picks the winner in time
- Wants from the party: Predictability, debt, and access to whichever roads become valuable after the ruling
- Best use: Translate abstract power into immediate material cost
- If lost: Replace with the Guilds acting through ledgers, smugglers, and disappearing stockpiles instead of a face

## Archivist Sen Vael

- Role: Pale Accord observer attached to the fortress vaults
- Public face: Research-minded mediator pretending historical truth can still settle a succession crisis
- Hidden truth: Sen cares more about the Iron Echo's oath logic than the fate of the claimant houses
- Wants from the party: Access to sealed chambers, witness at contact, and protection from simpler minds
- Best use: The scholar who keeps accidentally making everything politically worse
- If lost: Leave behind annotated command records that still drive the plot

## Scout-Confessor Maelin Rook

- Role: Sarafan field operative monitoring the fortress conflict
- Public face: Watchful observer claiming only to gather proof of corruption and instability
- Hidden truth: She is ready to recommend preemptive cleansing the moment the fortress looks weak enough to break
- Wants from the party: Proof that one side is irredeemable, or enough disorder to justify intervention
- Best use: Keep Sarafan pressure active without making every scene a raid
- If lost: Replace her with a harsher strike commander in Session 4 or 5

## Canon-Examiner Ser Jorun Bale

- Role: Sarafan procedural confessor attached to the legalist wing of the order
- Public face: Severe, articulate, and committed to record, chain of proof, and containment before open purgation
- Hidden truth: He still believes law can save the Sarafan from becoming what their enemies say they already are
- Wants from the party: Usable testimony, artifact access under supervision, and any proof that the Hollow faction is manufacturing necessity through panic
- Best use: Make the Sarafan split playable instead of theoretical; he is dangerous, but not identical to the hardliners
- If lost: Let his records survive and become leverage for whoever wants to divide the order later

## Sister Nerys Vale

- Role: Warden observer following fragment imbalance into the fortress and the north
- Public face: Measured, unseduced by victory, and willing to let a room hate her if it makes them hear the cost of what they are asking for
- Hidden truth: She already suspects the Heart can temporarily hold the Hylden wound shut, but only by teaching Nosgoth to depend on another catastrophic instrument
- Wants from the party: A choice they can defend when power is useful but not innocent
- Best use: Put Balance onstage before the Hall of Equilibrium and give Arc II a living conscience that is not passive or decorative
- If lost: Make her absence itself a warning that no one impartial remains in the room

## Ash-Glass Reth

- Role: Field apostle of the Ash-Glass Procession, the Crimson Fracture's roaming breach-cult front
- Public face: Half-broken survivor who speaks of the breach as if it were the first honest thing they have ever seen and now leads others to the same revelation
- Hidden truth: Reth is both victim and evangelist; they have been transformed by instability enough that they can no longer imagine restoration without contempt, and Elder Seripha's blessing has turned that trauma into mission
- Wants from the party: One admission that the old world deserves less loyalty than the wound opening through it, and one public act the Procession can retell as proof
- Best use: Give the Fracture a recurring field face before Arc III turns it into a larger ideological force
- If lost: Leave behind Procession converts, graffiti, and guided survivors that still carry the same seduction

## Toma Icewake

- Role: Frontier guide for the northern transition
- Public face: Bitter scout who knows where maps stop working
- Hidden truth: Toma has already seen Hylden possession and no longer trusts ordinary perception near the Gate
- Wants from the party: To survive the north with enough of his mind intact to leave it again
- Best use: The human cost of moving from clan war into breach horror
- If lost: Make the north immediately less navigable and more lethal

## The Glass Voice

- Role: Hylden negotiator, projection, or possessed mouthpiece
- Public face: Coldly articulate envoy offering solutions in the language of function rather than morality
- Hidden truth: It is not lying about everything, which is what makes it dangerous
- Wants from the party: Use of the Rift Ember, a compromised bargain, or one PC willing to be reasonable
- Best use: Give Hylden Rationalism a face before the breach climax
- If lost: Let its logic persist through devices, notes, and second-order consequences
