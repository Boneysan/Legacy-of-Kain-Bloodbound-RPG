# Arc II: Ascension

## Arc Purpose

Arc II widens the campaign from cult horror into open political struggle. The party confront claims of legitimacy at Dumah's Fortress, then carry that outcome north into the Hylden crisis around the Rift Ember. The key structural rule for this arc is that the fortress problem should not feel solved simply because the party has moved on; its debts, rivalries, and strategic consequences must remain alive while the northern crisis worsens.

## Level Band

- Recommended levels: 6-10
- Target length: 6-8 sessions

## Core Locations

- Dumah's Fortress and surrounding wastes
- Clan-held approach roads and siege routes
- Uschtenheim or allied hunter outposts if pressure from mortals is needed
- Hylden Gate frontier
- Resonance Pillar and breach sites

## Active Factions

- Vampire Clans, especially Turelim and Dumahim claimants
- Sarafan procedural confessors and Hollow Sarafan hardliners
- Hylden Remnants
- Pale Accord field researchers
- Outlands Guild brokers moving weapons, maps, and rumors
- Crimson Fracture scouts, defectors, or saboteurs exploiting realm instability, especially the Ash-Glass Procession led in the field by Ash-Glass Reth
- A Warden or Balance-minded observer tracking what fragment use is doing to the wider order

## Session Beats

1. Arrival at a contested fortress and public demand to take a side.
2. Duel, infiltration, or siege sequence that determines access to the Iron Echo.
3. Repercussions from the fortress claim spill into transport, shelter, and troop movement.
4. Northern expedition where glyph-static, cold, possession, and early Fracture rhetoric replace straightforward warfare.
5. First major breach event involving the Rift Ember and the question of whether fragment logic can reinforce or replace a failing seal.
6. Decision to seal, redirect, exploit, or lose the breach at a strategic cost, followed by one impossible contradiction that points toward the Chronoplast.

## Questions To Force

- Can power in Nosgoth be legitimate if it is inherited through blood and terror?
- Which enemy is worse in practice: the crusader, the clan tyrant, or the rational invader?
- What price is the party willing to pay to stop the Hylden without becoming useful to them?
- If the Heart can hold reality together for a moment, who gets to decide what it is holding together for?

## Milestone Triggers

- The party settle the fate of Dumah's Fortress for now.
- The Iron Echo changes hands or is sealed out of immediate faction use.
- The northern breach outcome changes the strategic map going into Arc III.
- Arc II ends with a witness, record, or contradiction that only the Chronoplast can adjudicate.

## Carry Forward

- Which clan or commander now owes the party a debt or swears revenge.
- Whether the Hylden have identified a PC as a viable vessel or negotiator.
- What faction learned the most from the Rift Ember.
- Which Sarafan bloc now sees the party as useful and which sees them as unforgivable.
- Whether the Ash-Glass Procession gained a convert, survivor, or proof text from the breach.
