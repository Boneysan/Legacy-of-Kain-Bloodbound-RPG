# Arc II: Ascension Choice Guide

Use this document to track the arc's major narrative decisions. This guide focuses on legitimacy, command, breach management, and the costs of turning fragment logic into strategy.

## Session 1: Standing At Dumah's Fortress

### Choose a public position

- Shelter under a claimant: gains immediate cover and access, but ties the party to that faction's legitimacy.
- Claim independent standing: preserves autonomy, but makes neutrality look like arrogance or weakness.
- Refuse the game and operate outside protection: keeps freedom of movement, but forces the party to survive without formal backing.
- Carry forward: sets the party's reputation inside the fortress and determines who expects loyalty, gratitude, or betrayal.

## Session 2: How To Test The Claimants

### Choose the method of intervention

- Formal challenge: makes the conflict public and legible, but binds the party to fortress law and visible consequences.
- Covert infiltration: uncovers hidden truths, but risks looking dishonorable or manipulative if exposed.
- Sabotage exposure or investigation: centers proof over spectacle, but may arrive too late to prevent violence.
- Carry forward: shapes which claimant gains momentum, how legitimacy is argued, and whether the party are seen as honorable, useful, or dangerous.

## Session 3: The Iron Echo Verdict

### Decide whether legitimacy is granted, exposed, or refused

- Grant legitimacy: stabilizes command quickly, but may empower a ruler who is effective without being just.
- Expose the true claim: clarifies the moral record, but can deepen civil fracture if the rightful answer is politically weak.
- Refuse legitimacy altogether: blocks easy consolidation, but risks leaving a vacuum the strong will fill anyway.
- Carry forward: determines the fortress's immediate political future and what kind of order the Iron Echo is remembered for imposing.

### Decide the Iron Echo's status

- Use it openly: resolves the crisis decisively, but normalizes fragment intervention in political succession.
- Seal or hide it: limits future abuse, but leaves the underlying command problem morally unresolved.
- Permit partial claim or stewardship: shares burden, but guarantees later disputes over custody and meaning.
- Carry forward: decides whether the fortress leaves Arc II feeling settled, merely paused, or actively unstable.

## Session 4: What Travels North

### Decide who comes north, who stays behind, and what cannot be carried further

- Bring allies, prisoners, envoys, or claim-marked survivors: gains leverage and continuity, but imports fortress conflict into the northern crisis.
- Leave unstable baggage behind: improves mobility, but risks abandonment, revolt, or hostile reinterpretation of the party's choices.
- Carry forward: determines whether the northern front begins as an expedition, an escort mission, or a moving political disaster.

## Session 5: The Rift Ember Question

### Use enemy tools, destroy them, or attempt a third path

- Use Hylden tools: may be the most effective answer, but carries moral contamination and long-term metaphysical cost.
- Destroy them: denies the enemy leverage, but may also destroy the only practical means of emergency stabilization.
- Attempt a third path: preserves principle and creativity, but relies on incomplete knowledge under crisis pressure.
- Carry forward: frames whether the party treat the Hylden crisis as a technical problem, a moral boundary, or an impossible compromise.

## Session 6: Resolve The Breach

### Seal, redirect, weaponize, or survive failure

- Seal the breach: buys stability, but may strengthen the Wheel, require ugly substitutes, or leave the deeper system untouched.
- Redirect the breach: preserves tactical leverage, but shifts danger onto another place, people, or future front.
- Weaponize the breach: can break enemies and structures quickly, but risks proving the Fracture and Hylden arguments right.
- Survive failure: accepts an imperfect outcome to preserve lives or future action, but leaves the north scarred and unresolved.
- Carry forward: creates the contradiction that drives Arc III and establishes whether the party are seen as saviors, opportunists, or engineers of a worse disaster.
