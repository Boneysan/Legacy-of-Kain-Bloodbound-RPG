# 03 — What Command Sounds Like

The Iron Echo does not award authority. It locates it — or the absence of it. The fragment has been waiting in the fortress for someone with enough unbroken oath to enter the chamber cleanly, and it has been a long time since anyone here qualified. This scene is the first contact, and the price is not physical.

---

## Scene Info

- **Arc / Session:** Arc II — Session 3
- **Trigger:** The party reaches the Iron Echo chamber. Access has been forced, brokered, or granted under claimant escort. The fragment is present and has already begun answering — not the party, but the room: old commands echoing, old refusals ringing.
- **Scene Type:** `revelation`
- **Est. Duration:** 5–7 min
- **Tags This Scene Can Change:** `iron_echo_status`, `fortress_claim`, `sarafan_bloc`

---

## Cast

| NPC | Role in This Scene |
|-----|--------------------|
| Archivist Sen Vael | In the room. He is the only one present who has read what the fragment is supposed to do. He is more frightened than he looks. |
| The arriving or contested claimant | Present at the threshold, not inside the chamber. Whatever happens in here will be used politically the moment the party exits. |
| Sister Nerys Vale | Positioned at the chamber's edge, unannounced. She is not here to stop anything. She is here to record the cost. |

---

## Opening Narration

> The chamber of the Iron Echo does not look like a treasury. It looks like a court of judgment built for generals — a room where commands were expected to be obeyed and where every scar in the stone preserves the memory of one given too early, too late, or to soldiers who were already dead. The fragment sits at the center on a martial dais — not luminous, not theatrical — simply present in the manner of things that have waited long enough to have stopped being surprised by anything that approaches. Old echo-marks line the walls in registers the party cannot read. One of them is still bleeding, very slowly, from a source that has been dry for a century.

---

## NPC Opening Line

> **Archivist Sen Vael**, reading from margin notes he has been holding since before the party arrived: *"The fragment was last formally addressed under Dumah's command authority. Every subsequent contact attempt failed the recognition threshold. The chamber's theory of legitimacy is not bloodline alone — it is unbroken martial oath. Which means—"* He looks at the party. *"One of you may be a better candidate than either of the people waiting outside to use the result."*

---

## Player Choices

### A — Approach the Fragment as a Claimant's Representative

> You are here in someone's name. Present the claim formally to the Echo and let it render a judgment.

**NPC Response (the fragment):**

> The chamber rings once, low and long, as if a bell the size of the room were struck from inside. The echo-marks on the wall shift — two grow dim, one brightens slightly. Not the answer anyone wanted. Not a refusal either. The fragment is identifying which claim is *less broken*, which is not the same as endorsing one.

**Outcome:** The Iron Echo renders a partial validation. The claimant's case is strengthened, but the fragment's judgment is legible to everyone in the room and cannot be edited. Whatever Sen Vael records becomes the only honest account of what it said. The claimant outside takes the result and reshapes it into a stronger version of themselves than the fragment actually produced.

**Tags:** `iron_echo_status: used`, `fortress_claim: party_backed`

---

### B — Approach the Fragment in the Party's Own Name

> No one else's authority. Present yourselves.

**NPC Response (the fragment):**

> Silence lasting about four seconds longer than feels comfortable. Then a series of resonant pulses — not echo-marks brightening, but new marks appearing, in spaces that were blank. The fragment is registering an oath-structure it does not have a prior record of. PCs with martial oaths, blood-debts, or command authority in their backgrounds receive tailored recognition: the weight of an acknowledged obligation, the discomfort of a pending one.

**Outcome:** The Echo does not validate a claimant. It registers the party. Sen Vael's notes are full of annotations he cannot entirely explain. The fortress politics become complicated: whoever controls the party now has more fragment legitimacy than either claimant. Sister Nerys Vale takes a single note and closes her journal.

**Tags:** `iron_echo_status: used`, `fortress_claim: neutral_brokered`

---

### C — Refuse Contact; Observe and Document Instead

> You are not claiming anything. Let it be witnessed, not used.

**NPC Response (Sen Vael):**

> *"That is — arguably the most defensible position anyone has taken in this chamber in a century."* He is writing very quickly. *"The problem is it also means neither claimant can use today and neither can be denied. Which means this continues until someone less careful breaks the standoff."*

**Outcome:** The fragment is preserved, intact, and politically inert. The standoff extends. The fortress remains contested, which means the party carries less factional debt north — but also less factional support. The Pale Accord's documentation is clean and gives Sen Vael considerable interpretive authority over what the fragment actually said.

**Tags:** `iron_echo_status: sealed`, `fortress_claim: contested`

---

### D — Use the Echo to Expose the Oathbreaker in the Room

> Someone in this chamber broke a sworn martial obligation. The fragment knows it. Make it say so.

**NPC Response (the fragment):**

> The chamber does not hesitate. One echo-mark, long dormant, illuminates in deep copper. It is pointing at someone specific. Whether that is a claimant, an archivist, or a party member depends on what the GM has established about broken oaths in prior sessions. Whatever the answer, the room knows it now.

**Outcome:** One person in or adjacent to the chamber has been publicly named by the fortress's own memory. That person's position changes immediately and permanently. The party has done what the fragment was designed to do — they have forced honest accounting. The political aftermath is significant and fast-moving.

**Tags:** `iron_echo_status: used`, `fortress_claim: contested` *(the oathbreaker's claim collapses or their standing cracks)*

---

## If No Clear Choice Is Made

> If the party reaches the chamber and hesitates without progressing, the fragment begins answering ambient oath-residue in the room on its own — and the first answer it gives is about whoever among those present has the most unresolved obligation. The GM selects the target. Default to Choice B's mechanical outcome applied to whoever that is.

**Default Tag:** `iron_echo_status: used`

## GM Notes

- Sister Nerys Vale does not participate in this scene. She observes. If someone asks her what the fragment said, she answers with the literal account — which is almost certainly more complicated than either claimant's version.
- The oathbreaker in Choice D should have been seeded in prior sessions. Best targets: a fortress retainer who served multiple administrations, the claimant whose claim is weakest, or a party member with an unresolved blood-debt from Arc I.
- The fragment's judgment is not a verdict that compels obedience. It is public memory given voice. What factions do with that memory is the rest of Arc II.
- Archivist Sen Vael will not falsify his notes. He can, however, keep them private — for a price.
