# Arc II Session Prep

Use this file as a rolling prep sheet for Sessions 1-6 of Ascension.

## Arc II Defaults

- Core fragments: The Iron Echo and the Rift Ember
- Core pressure: legitimacy, siege logic, clan claims, Hylden escalation, breach management, and the first signs that balance itself is being strained
- Core factions: Vampire Clans, Sarafan Order, Hylden Remnants, Pale Accord, Outlands Guilds, Crimson Fracture
- Kain usage: distant strategic pressure or one intervention that reframes the northern crisis
- Recurring cast: see 02_NPC-Appendix.md
- Detailed encounter planning: see 03_Encounter-Guide.md
- Fracture front: use the Ash-Glass Procession as the recurring Fracture face, with Ash-Glass Reth converting survivors and carrying Elder Seripha's doctrine from the fortress roads into the northern breach.

## Session 1

- Purpose: Bring the party to Dumah's Fortress and force public positioning before private planning becomes possible.
- Opening image: Dumah's Fortress rises from the eastern wastes as a gray mountain of dead authority, its gate banners torn away but its walls still commanding obedience from everyone who approaches.
- Primary scenes:
	- The party arrive with Arc I consequences attached: relic rumors, faction scrutiny, and at least one pursuer or petition following them.
	- Before entry is granted, rival claimants, envoys, or commanders demand to know under whose leave the party approach the fortress.
	- A public audience, challenge rite, or contested parley establishes that neutrality is read as weakness here.
	- The party learn three competing versions of who has the best claim to the fortress and why each version is self-serving.
	- The session ends with the party either taking provisional shelter under one claimant, claiming independent standing, or making enough enemies that they must operate without protection.
- Faction moves:
	- Vampire Clans: Turelim and Dumahim agents want the party legitimizing their side, even if only by accepting hospitality.
	- Outlands Guilds: A Guild broker offers practical access, smuggling, or intelligence in exchange for future leverage around fortress supply lines.
	- Sarafan Order: Scouts or envoys are watching for proof that the fortress can be destabilized, cleansed, or politically exploited.
	- Sarafan Order: Make the split visible immediately. One confessor wants evidence and controlled seizure; one Hollow hardliner wants public collapse as justification for extermination.
	- Pale Accord: Observers want access to old vaults and ward chambers beneath the fortress, and may back whichever claimant grants them research rights.
- Relic pressure:
	- The Iron Echo is not yet exposed, but the fortress already resonates with its pressure through old challenge laws, command rituals, and oathstones.
	- PCs prone to pride, martial honor, or unresolved blood-debts should feel the place encouraging formal response over compromise.
	- One gate, hall, or armory should only respond to rank, oath, or lineage recognition.
	- A subtle imbalance should show itself here too: a hall holding too many echoes, a ward that punishes the wrong oath, or a spectral response that feels heavier than the immediate politics justify.
- Mandatory NPCs:
	- A Turelim claimant: disciplined, forceful, and convinced only strength can hold Nosgoth together.
	- A Dumahim claimant or steward: stoic, wounded by history, and invoking inheritance rather than charisma.
	- A Guild factor: someone who knows the supply reality beneath the public rhetoric.
	- One fortress retainer or thrall who has survived multiple regime shifts and knows where bodies, vows, and keys are buried.
	- One Warden observer, pilgrim-judge, or Balance-minded mediator who is tolerated only because nobody wants to be seen expelling them publicly.
- Encounter slot:
	- Preferred structure: social brinkmanship with the possibility of a formal duel or ambush.
	- Best use: a gate challenge, assassination attempt during parley, or sabotage blamed on the wrong faction.
	- If combat erupts, keep it limited and political rather than total war.
- Spectral intrusion:
	- The fortress halls appear crowded in the Spectral with soldiers still standing post long after death.
	- One command spoken aloud causes spectral troops to turn as if awaiting the rightful voice.
	- A PC sees Dumah's court at full strength for a blink, then only dust and ruin again.
- End-of-session fallout:
	- The party are now publicly associated with a claimant, a faction, or a dangerous refusal.
	- Clan War Clock should advance if negotiations collapse into honor violence or sabotage.
	- The path to the Iron Echo is clarified through rumor, rite, or secured access.
- Prep notes:
	- Decide before play which claimant is strong, which is legitimate, and which is actually governable. These should not automatically be the same person.
	- Make hospitality politically binding.
	- Carry at least one Arc I faction consequence into the opening scene.

## Session 2

- Purpose: Test claims to the fortress through challenge, espionage, sabotage, or open violence.
- Opening image: Inside the fortress, every corridor feels like a corridor to a duel, and every meal is eaten as if poison, insult, and recruitment might arrive in the same cup.
- Primary scenes:
	- The party are asked to prove loyalty, utility, or neutrality through a specific task.
	- Competing routes open: formal challenge, covert infiltration, sabotage exposure, or investigation of an old oath violation tied to the Iron Echo.
	- The party learn who is staging violence and who is merely benefiting from it.
	- A private chamber, crypt vault, or command hall reveals that fortress legitimacy is tied to more than inheritance.
	- The session ends with the claimant crisis sharpened into a contest the party cannot avoid influencing.
- Faction moves:
	- Turelim side: pushes for decisive action, purges, and visible strength.
	- Dumahim side: emphasizes memory, continuity, and offense taken at illegitimate command.
	- Sarafan agents: try to fuel internal collapse so the fortress weakens before any crusade begins.
	- Hollow Sarafan agents: prefer sabotage that can later be described as proof no undead authority can stand.
	- Guilds: sell information to multiple sides unless stopped.
- Relic pressure:
	- The Iron Echo starts answering challenges indirectly through echoed commands, ringing steel, and compulsions toward formal answer.
	- PCs failing social restraint should feel pushed toward duel logic.
	- One piece of evidence should only make sense if someone broke a sworn martial obligation.
	- Leave one small sign that the fortress conflict is not closed by a victor alone: a spectral regiment obeying no living commander, or a ward that accepts the ruling but not the moral claim behind it.
- Mandatory NPCs:
	- The two primary claimants or their seconds.
	- A witness or archivist who knows the old command law.
	- One saboteur, spy, or falsely accused retainer.
- Encounter slot:
	- Preferred structure: choose one signature test.
	- Options: duel by proxy, infiltration of sealed barracks, ambush in a gallery, or interruption of a blood-oath ceremony.
	- If combat occurs, make victory change political standing immediately.
- Spectral intrusion:
	- Old commanders appear behind current claimants, endorsing or rejecting them silently.
	- A shattered throne room momentarily fills with spectral banners and the sound of marching columns.
- End-of-session fallout:
	- One claimant gains momentum, one loses face, and the party are now implicated.
	- Clan War Clock likely advances if blood is publicly spilled or accusations become irreversible.
	- Access to the Iron Echo becomes attainable in Session 3.
- Prep notes:
	- Build at least one path that rewards intelligence over brute force.
	- Let insult matter mechanically or socially.
	- Make it possible for the party to back no one and still shape the outcome.

## Session 3

- Purpose: Resolve access to the Iron Echo and show what martial legitimacy costs once the fragment starts answering blood and command.
- Opening image: The chamber of the Iron Echo does not look like a treasury; it looks like a courtroom built for generals, where every scar in the stone remembers a command obeyed too late.
- Primary scenes:
	- The party reach the Iron Echo chamber through oath, force, or treachery.
	- Access to the fragment requires a formal recognition scene: command claim, oath declaration, blood payment, or trial of worth.
	- The fragment reveals broken martial vows and unanswered claims inside the fortress crisis.
	- A decisive confrontation forces the party to determine whether legitimacy can be awarded, exposed, or refused.
	- The session ends with the fortress's immediate future settled enough to move the campaign north.
- Faction moves:
	- Clan claimants want the Echo to validate them.
	- Sarafan operatives want the fortress weakened regardless of who holds it.
	- Pale Accord wants to study the fragment's oath-binding function.
	- Guilds want to know which road becomes safe and profitable after the ruling.
	- A Warden voice should note that the Echo can impose order without producing justice, and that this distinction will matter later.
- Relic pressure:
	- First meaningful contact demands a DR 3 Will or Fury test.
	- Failure inflicts 1 Corruption and a compulsion toward challenge, command, or violent answer to insult.
	- The fragment can expose oathbreakers, bind temporary truces, or awaken dormant fortress wards.
- Mandatory NPCs:
	- Winning or losing claimant
	- A credible witness to the old command structure
	- One person the party do not want to give power to, but cannot easily exclude
- Encounter slot:
	- Preferred structure: relic contact under martial crisis.
	- Use a chamber defense, command trial gone violent, or ward activation sequence.
	- If combat is avoided, the scene should still end with a binding ruling or public humiliation.
- Spectral intrusion:
	- The fortress dead visibly respond to command legitimacy.
	- A broken oath becomes visible as chain, wound, or banner tear in the Spectral.
	- Someone the party trusted may look false under the Echo's judgment.
- End-of-session fallout:
	- The Iron Echo is used, sealed, hidden, or partially claimed.
	- Dumah's Fortress has a current ruler, steward, or unresolved power vacuum.
	- The party gain a debt, feud, or reputation that follows them north.
	- Clan War Clock may advance again if the ruling solves nothing cleanly.
	- One fortress consequence remains active by design: a rival escapes, a truce is oath-bound but unstable, or the rightful command remains morally contested.
- Prep notes:
	- Decide how much order the Echo actually creates versus reveals.
	- Let the fragment answer values as much as bloodline.
	- Keep the northward hook visible before the session ends.

## Session 4

- Purpose: Transition north and convert political victory or failure into a logistical and military problem.
- Opening image: The eastern wastes harden into northern ice and old war scars, where maps stop agreeing with the ground and every supply fire feels visible from too far away.
- Primary scenes:
	- The party depart the fortress with allies, prisoners, envoys, or stolen authority attached.
	- Travel scenes establish the cost of what was decided at Dumah's Fortress: escort burdens, raids, political messages, and supply strain.
	- Evidence of Hylden activity escalates from rumor to material proof through glyph-static, twisted bodies, burned geometry, and at least one Fracture survivor or document treating the instability as revelation rather than disaster.
	- The party decide who comes north, who stays behind, and what cannot be carried further.
	- The session ends with the frontier crisis unmistakable and immediate.
- Faction moves:
	- Fortress survivors reposition around the party's choice.
	- Sarafan scouts and hunters track either the relic aftermath or the breach rumors.
	- Pale Accord researchers push north aggressively once the Rift Ember is confirmed.
	- Hylden agents begin acting openly enough to be undeniable.
	- The Ash-Glass Procession scavenges the same instability for doctrine, recruits, and proof that the wall between realms should not survive.
- Relic pressure:
	- The Iron Echo's fallout continues socially even if the fragment is left behind.
	- The first signs of the Rift Ember appear as instability in distance, wards, and geometry.
	- PCs should feel the tonal shift from oath-pressure to breach-pressure.
	- One scene should make clear that fragment logic might hold a breach shut for a moment even while making the long-term wound worse.
- Mandatory NPCs:
	- One Arc II ally from the fortress who is unsuited to the north but insists on coming anyway
	- One frontier guide or scout
	- One victim or witness of Hylden possession
	- One Procession-marked defector, scavenger, or sermon fragment showing that somebody has already started to call collapse freedom and that Reth is organizing the survivors
- Encounter slot:
	- Preferred structure: travel hazard plus contact encounter.
	- Best use: attack by corrupted scouts, a frontier outpost already compromised, or a rescue under glyph-static conditions.
	- Keep the fight as a sign of escalation, not the climax.
- Spectral intrusion:
	- Distance feels wrong and dead paths overlap living roads.
	- A camp seen from afar may prove to be ruins when reached.
- End-of-session fallout:
	- Hylden Breach Clock should advance to at least 1.
	- The party reach the frontier knowing Arc II is no longer mainly about clan politics.
	- A clear route to the Rift Ember or Resonance Pillar is established.
- Prep notes:
	- Make the north feel alien before the Hylden themselves fully appear.
	- Carry one unresolved fortress consequence into the expedition.
	- Keep the party short on certainty, not necessarily on resources alone.

## Session 5

- Purpose: Put the party in contact with the Rift Ember and make Hylden logic both useful and abhorrent.
- Opening image: The Resonance Pillar hums through the ice like a machine dreaming, while the Rift Ember burns with cold light that makes nearby angles look invented seconds ago.
- Primary scenes:
	- The party reach the breach site and discover that the crisis is part military front, part engineering problem, part metaphysical wound.
	- Hylden logic presents itself through devices, possessed intermediaries, or a negotiator who speaks with unbearable clarity.
	- The party learn what the Rift Ember can do: open, power, stabilize, or sabotage a breach.
	- They must decide whether to use enemy tools, destroy them, or attempt a third path with incomplete knowledge.
	- The session ends with a breach event beginning or already underway.
- Faction moves:
	- Hylden Remnants: seek activation, leverage, or a living vessel.
	- Pale Accord: want controlled study and emergency containment.
	- Sarafan forces: let one bloc prefer destruction at any cost while another argues that containment and evidence must precede cleansing.
	- Any clan allies: may want the power redirected for territorial advantage.
	- The Ash-Glass Procession: seek proof that the breach should be widened, not healed, because fracture is the first honest condition the world has shown them.
- Relic pressure:
	- First contact requires a DR 3 Focus or Soul test.
	- Failure inflicts 1 Corruption and spatial distortion, lost time, or destabilized memory.
	- Deliberate use costs 2-3 Corruption when powering or altering the site.
	- The Rift Ember should make even good solutions feel mechanically dangerous.
	- Make explicit that the fragment can temporarily reinforce a failing seal, replace one under emergency conditions, or widen it into a true opening depending on who drives it.
- Mandatory NPCs:
	- A Hylden voice, projection, or possessed negotiator
	- A Pale Accord operator or Glyphwright
	- One commander or zealot who wants the simplest possible answer
	- One Ash-Glass Procession voice who interprets the same evidence as liberation, whether Reth personally or a convert speaking in their rhetoric
- Encounter slot:
	- Preferred structure: boss pressure plus site management.
	- Use Hylden Shock Troopers, possession risk, collapsing geometry, or pylon overload as simultaneous objectives.
	- Let player choices about the site matter as much as damage dealt.
- Spectral intrusion:
	- The Spectral behaves badly here; paths fork, distances tear, and some entities vanish at the edge of Hylden logic.
	- One PC may see themselves arriving seconds before they do.
- End-of-session fallout:
	- The breach outcome becomes immediate, not hypothetical.
	- Hylden Breach Clock likely advances again.
	- The party now understand why the north cannot be solved through brute force alone.
- Prep notes:
	- Decide what the Hylden argument gets right.
	- Put at least one useful but morally corrosive option on the table.
	- Make the site readable to Glyphwrights and terrifying to everyone else.

## Session 6

- Purpose: Resolve the breach outcome and establish the new strategic map going into Revelation.
- Opening image: Snow, glyph-fire, and torn reality share the same horizon while every surviving faction decides whether this is a battlefield, a laboratory, or the beginning of the end.
- Primary scenes:
	- The party execute their chosen answer to the breach: seal, redirect, weaponize, or survive failure.
	- Allies and enemies converge for one final northern crisis sequence.
	- The Rift Ember's cost becomes undeniable through Corruption, casualties, or lasting instability.
	- Once the immediate crisis resolves, the party learn who benefited, who was broken, and which larger questions now point toward the Chronoplast and Arc III.
	- The transition clue must be concrete: a soldier remembered alive in two incompatible outcomes, a battlefield record dated to tomorrow, or a witness carrying a prophecy in Moebius's voice that no one present could have heard.
	- The session ends with the north changed and the campaign's next truth-seeking arc clearly signaled.
- Faction moves:
	- Hylden Remnants either gain a foothold, suffer a setback, or successfully mark the party as future instruments.
	- Pale Accord claims data, salvage, or blame.
	- Sarafan forces interpret the outcome through purity and heresy.
	- Clan or Guild observers reposition according to the new strategic map.
	- Ash-Glass Procession survivors leave with doctrine strengthened if the breach felt more honest than the old world.
- Relic pressure:
	- The Rift Ember is now sealed, moved, exploited, or left in a dangerous partial state.
	- If used directly by the party, it should leave ongoing narrative strain.
	- Its aftermath should alter travel, wards, and trust going forward.
	- State clearly which happened: the breach was reinforced temporarily, redirected, worsened, or converted into a fragile substitute seal.
- Mandatory NPCs:
	- Surviving Hylden representative or aftermath trace
	- Surviving Accord operator
	- One witness who can report the northern truth to the rest of Nosgoth
	- One time-scarred witness, impossible record, or paradox wound that forces the party toward the Chronoplast rather than merely suggesting it
- Encounter slot:
	- Preferred structure: layered end-of-arc crisis.
	- Use moving objectives, unstable terrain, reinforcement timing, and a choice between tactical victory and strategic cost.
	- If combat is brief, let the real climax be the choice of how the breach is resolved.
- Spectral intrusion:
	- Reality should feel thin and post-traumatic after the breach.
	- The dead and the not-yet-dead may briefly occupy the same scene.
	- End on an omen that points toward time, paradox, or a manipulated future.
- End-of-session fallout:
	- Arc II ends with the fortress question settled enough to stand and the northern question settled enough to scar.
	- The party enter Arc III with at least one new enemy, one new ideological problem, and one reason to seek answers instead of territory.
	- Update the tracker for clan standings, Hylden escalation, and any surviving Sarafan or Accord obligations.
	- The Chronoplast hook is no longer abstract. The party now possess or pursue evidence that ordinary history cannot contain.
- Prep notes:
	- Make the breach outcome matter on the map and in conversation.
	- Tie the next arc hook to consequence, not just mystery.
	- Do not let every faction interpret the result the same way.

## Compact Templates

### Fortress Gate Purge

- Use in Session 1 or 2 when Sarafan pressure turns a claim dispute into a public purge test.
- Baseline enemy force for a level 6 party with TV 24: 1 Sarafan Inquisitor (TV 8), 4 Sarafan Footsoldiers (TV 12 total), and 1 Sarafan Outrider (TV 5), for total TV 25.
- For levels 8-10, add a second Sarafan Inquisitor for total TV 33, or replace the Outrider with a veteran fortress retainer if the scene should feel more political than mobile.
- Terrain: gate tunnel, murder-slit gallery, oath banners that grant half cover to whoever controls the portcullis crank, and a winch chain that can be cut with a DR 3 Blood or Fury check to drop the inner gate.
- Tactics: Footsoldiers lock the threshold and exploit Formation Fighting, the Outrider marks escape routes and chases corrupted-looking PCs, and the Inquisitor uses Judgement Strike on the party member who looks most supernatural or most publicly defiant.
- Win condition: hold the claimant, witness, or command sigil for 3 rounds, or escort them through the inner gate before the Sarafan can brand the whole fortress faction as irredeemable.
- Complication twist: if Ser Jorun Bale is present, the legalist wing demands custody while hardliners attempt an unsanctioned kill, making Sarafan Schism matter inside the same encounter.

### Rift-Line Emergency

- Use in Session 5 or 6 when the northern front stops being abstract and the Rift Ember must be defended, redirected, or shut down under fire.
- Baseline enemy force for a level 8 party with TV 32: 1 Hylden Rift-Priest (TV 11) and 2 Hylden Shock Troopers (TV 18 total), for total TV 29.
- For levels 9-10, add 1 Hylden War-Stalker (TV 7) for total TV 36, or let Dimensional Tether complete and keep the summoned Grunts as a time-pressure reward for the GM rather than adding them to the opening budget.
- Terrain: broken pylon ring, unstable ice shelves, and a central breach node that can be stabilized with DR 4 Glyphcasting or Focus checks, sabotaged with Corruption, or physically shattered for immediate backlash.
- Tactics: the Rift-Priest stays behind the Troopers, opens with Corruption Pulse, then uses Rift Tear to scatter the party away from the node while the Troopers punish anyone who closes on the backline.
- Win condition: either destroy the Priest before a third full round of channeling, or complete 3 successful stabilization actions at the breach node while surviving the Trooper screen.
- Complication twist: if Ash-Glass Procession converts are nearby, every obvious failure at the node becomes instant Fracture propaganda and should be logged in the Fracture Merge Clock fallout.
