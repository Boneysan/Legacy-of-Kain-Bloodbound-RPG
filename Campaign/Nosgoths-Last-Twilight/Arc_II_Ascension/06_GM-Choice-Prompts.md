# Arc II: Ascension GM Choice Prompts

Use this document when a fortress or breach decision lands and you need a clear answer to what scene to run next.

## Session 1: Standing At Dumah's Fortress

### If the party shelter under a claimant

- Run a protected audience, formal meal, or guard-duty scene where every courtesy is political debt.
- Make rival claimants react as if the party already picked a side.
- Call in that shelter later as repayment, endorsement, or demanded fragment access.

### If the party claim independent standing

- Run checkpoint resistance, restricted access, or a negotiation where no one grants safe ground for free.
- Make every rumor sharper because neutrality is treated as concealed ambition.
- Let later hardship be worsened by the fact that the party owe no one and no one owes them.

### If the party operate without protection

- Run survival pressure inside the fortress: denied quarters, hostile patrols, and opportunistic attacks.
- Make freedom of movement feel earned rather than granted.
- Bring back their outsider status later when they need shelter in the north and old factions remember the slight.

## Session 2: How To Test The Claimants

### If the party choose formal challenge

- Run duel logic, oath-bound combat, or a public confrontation the whole fortress can witness.
- Let victory settle the room while leaving deeper rot unresolved.
- Use surviving witnesses later to remember exactly who won honor and who merely endured humiliation.

### If the party choose covert infiltration

- Run archives, sealed barracks, or sabotage-recovery under threat of discovery.
- Reward insight, but stain the party's standing if the method becomes public.
- Let defeated rivals later call the entire verdict fraud.

### If the party choose investigation or exposure

- Run a proof-driven scene that corners a liar, oathbreaker, or saboteur.
- Make the political situation clearer and the emotional situation more dangerous.
- Let the evidence itself become a later target for theft, reinterpretation, or destruction.

## Session 3: The Iron Echo Verdict

### If the party grant legitimacy

- Run coronation defense, crackdown, or immediate retaliation from the losing side.
- Give the fortress a chain of command before the party move north.
- Let the new ruler later send troops, tribute, or hunters based on how they were elevated.

### If the party expose the true claim

- Run a morally clarifying and politically destabilizing scene where truth outruns order.
- Make at least one powerful faction accept the facts and reject the consequence.
- Use the exposed truth later as proof that justice and governability were never the same answer.

### If the party refuse legitimacy altogether

- Run riot control, evacuation, or a scramble to stop the vacuum from becoming massacre.
- Advance feud pressure because no one can now pretend the fortress is settled.
- Keep the fortress alive later as a wound, not background.

### If the party use the Iron Echo openly

- Run a fragment-centered ruling scene where command itself becomes the weapon.
- Make everyone present learn that relic logic can settle succession by force of memory.
- Let later claimants invoke that precedent whenever they are desperate.

### If the party seal, hide, or steward the Iron Echo

- Run vault defense, tense council, or convoy protection instead of public resolution.
- Make the crisis quieter now and more brittle later.
- Use the key, vault, or steward later as the center of raids and legal claims.

## Session 4: What Travels North

### If the party bring envoys, prisoners, or marked survivors north

- Run escort pressure and divided-loyalty travel before the Hylden front even fully appears.
- Make the expedition feel politically crowded from the start.
- Turn one carried burden later into leverage during a breach negotiation or panic.

### If the party leave unstable burdens behind

- Run a cleaner departure followed by immediate news that the fortress story is changing without them.
- Make local actors seize testimony, titles, or narrative control.
- Bring one abandoned figure back later as martyr, mouthpiece, or broken witness.

## Session 5: The Rift Ember Question

### If the party use enemy tools

- Run device activation, possession defense, or a scene where success feels visibly contaminated.
- Make necessity persuasive and terrifying at the same time.
- Let allies later question whether the party resisted the Hylden or merely learned to administer them better.

### If the party destroy enemy tools

- Run demolition, unstable withdrawal, or a harder emergency seal attempt with fewer options.
- Preserve moral clarity while worsening immediate danger.
- Return later to the destroyed site through salvage attempts, regret, or accusations of waste.

### If the party attempt a third path

- Run improvised lattice repair, partial sealing, or experimental containment under severe pressure.
- Reward creativity with uncertainty rather than safety.
- Make the prototype answer later worth stealing, refining, or weaponizing.

## Session 6: Resolve The Breach

### If the party seal the breach

- Run hold-the-line defense while the seal is completed.
- Make the party look like saviors only if the price remains survivable and visible.
- Let the aftermath still show strain, refugees, and doubt about what was strengthened to make this possible.

### If the party redirect the breach

- Run evacuation, convoy defense, or battle around the place now inheriting the danger.
- Reframe the party immediately as practical and morally compromised.
- Bring the scar back later as a reason Arc III must confront contradictory outcomes.

### If the party weaponize the breach

- Run catastrophic battlefield play where victory and atrocity become hard to separate.
- Give hostile factions instant rhetorical ammunition.
- Let later survivors treat the party as the proof that fracture and desperation rule the age.

### If the party survive failure

- Run extraction under collapse, rescue under impossible conditions, or a retreat made meaningful by who is saved.
- Make the north changed and the victory partial at best.
- Use the unresolved contradiction born here as the concrete engine that drives the Chronoplast arc.
