# Nosgoth's Last Twilight Skill Check Recommendations

Use this document to identify where explicit skill checks would most improve GM usability across all four arcs.

This is not a rewrite of the campaign's existing structure. It is a module-readiness pass focused on scenes that already imply skill use but do not yet formalize it.

## Design Rule

- Add most new checks in vignette files, session-prep scene bullets, and GM choice prompts rather than only in combat files.
- Use DR 2 for first-pass clues, suspicion, and reading a room.
- Use DR 3 for layered interpretation, political leverage, and pressure scenes.
- Use DR 4 only for endgame engineering, metaphysical judgment, or scenes where the party are trying to secure decisive control.
- Let success change position, evidence quality, route access, or faction posture.
- Let failure cost time, trust, clarity, or leverage rather than hard-stopping the campaign.
- Prefer current PHB skill language from [../player's_handbook/06_Skills.md](../player's_handbook/06_Skills.md): Observation, Insight, Persuasion, Intimidation, Stealth, Infiltration, Lore, Forbidden Knowledge, Blood Sense, Concentration, Glyphcasting, Ritualism, Craft, Wilderness, and Tactics.

## Arc I: Awakening

### 1. Caravan Argument

- Files: [Arc_I_Awakening/VTT_Vignettes/01_Caravan-Argument.md](Arc_I_Awakening/VTT_Vignettes/01_Caravan-Argument.md), [Arc_I_Awakening/01_Session-Prep.md](Arc_I_Awakening/01_Session-Prep.md)
- Add: Observation or Insight at DR 2 to read Serit correctly; Blood Sense at DR 2-3 if a relic-sensitive PC probes the satchel.
- Success: confirm the satchel has been active since the second waymarker, catch that Serit's urgency is tied to a specific threat, and make Choice D feel like earned disclosure rather than blind pressure.
- Failure: the party still move forward, but they read the scene as generic urgency and lose early leverage with Serit.

### 2. False Refuge

- Files: [Arc_I_Awakening/VTT_Vignettes/02_False-Refuge.md](Arc_I_Awakening/VTT_Vignettes/02_False-Refuge.md), [Arc_I_Awakening/01_Session-Prep.md](Arc_I_Awakening/01_Session-Prep.md)
- Add: Observation or Insight at DR 2 to catch Halden's rehearsed rider story; Infiltration or Stealth at DR 2 to scout the exterior or hidden access points before the ambush shifts into motion.
- Success: identify that the story was prepared, find the side entrance and recent tracks, or force the cult cell into a noisier approach.
- Failure: the ambush still happens, but the cult act from a cleaner starting position and the party enter Session 2 with less confidence in what they saw.

### 3. Session 2 Investigation Layer

- Files: [Arc_I_Awakening/01_Session-Prep.md](Arc_I_Awakening/01_Session-Prep.md), [Encounters/Arc_I_Awakening/02_The-Healers-Smile.md](Encounters/Arc_I_Awakening/02_The-Healers-Smile.md)
- Add: Observation, Lore, Insight, and Infiltration checks across the refuge ward, side chapel, and records-route investigation.
- Suggested DRs: DR 2 for finding clues, DR 3 for proving compromise or exposing the right suspect.
- Success: identify one compromised site, one compromised person, and one route below Avernus before the reveal turns violent.
- Failure: the party still reach the same answer-space, but with weaker proof, more collateral panic, or a stronger chance of accusing the wrong person first.

### 4. Blood-Well Descent

- Files: [Arc_I_Awakening/01_Session-Prep.md](Arc_I_Awakening/01_Session-Prep.md)
- Add: Blood Sense, Observation, Lore, and Infiltration checks while moving through lower passages and blood-channel architecture.
- Suggested DRs: DR 2 to pick up directional resonance and route pressure; DR 3 to separate older site history from newer cult profanation.
- Success: determine which route is oldest, which route the cult used recently, and where the chamber's true pressure is strongest.
- Failure: the party still descend, but lose time, invite worse positioning, or misread what caused the site to become active.

### 5. Fragment Custody and Exit Story

- Files: [Arc_I_Awakening/VTT_Vignettes/05_Fragment-Custody.md](Arc_I_Awakening/VTT_Vignettes/05_Fragment-Custody.md), [Arc_I_Awakening/06_GM-Choice-Prompts.md](Arc_I_Awakening/06_GM-Choice-Prompts.md)
- Add: Insight and Tactics at DR 2-3 to read faction motives; Persuasion or Intimidation at DR 2-3 to control what witnesses, Accord agents, or Guild contacts will carry out of Avernus.
- Success: let the party understand who is bluffing, who is positioning for later custody, and who can still be persuaded into a controlled public story.
- Failure: Arc II opens with more misinformation, shakier witness support, or a faction believing the party are easier to isolate than they really are.

### 6. First Blood-Memory

- Files: [Arc_I_Awakening/VTT_Vignettes/04_First-Blood-Memory.md](Arc_I_Awakening/VTT_Vignettes/04_First-Blood-Memory.md)
- Add: Blood Sense, Forbidden Knowledge, Insight, and Concentration checks to formalize the private recognition event.
- Suggested DRs: DR 2 to recognize that the fragment is identifying rather than possessing, DR 3 to interpret the ancestral name, chain, or oath-symbol correctly, and DR 3 Concentration if the PC is trying to stay outwardly composed.
- Success: the player learns something actionable about lineage, oath, or buried inheritance and can choose secrecy, disclosure, or pursuit from an informed position.
- Failure: the vision still lands, but it remains more uncanny than interpretable and is easier for later NPCs to categorize before the player does.

### 7. Session 6 Social Consolidation

- Files: [Arc_I_Awakening/01_Session-Prep.md](Arc_I_Awakening/01_Session-Prep.md)
- Add: Insight, Persuasion, Intimidation, and Tactics checks during the arc-closing consolidation scene where the party settle debts, choose a public story, and decide who travels with them.
- Suggested DRs: DR 2 to read which offer is sincere, which threat is a bluff, and which witness is unstable; DR 3 to lock in a workable departure agreement or controlled account.
- Success: Arc II opens with a cleaner ally-hunter-debt-secret configuration and a named Last Witness chosen with intent rather than habit.
- Failure: the transition still works, but the party leave Avernus with looser promises, shakier witness control, or an avoidable faction misunderstanding already in motion.

## Arc II: Ascension

### 1. Fortress Arrival and Claimant Read

- Files: [Arc_II_Ascension/01_Session-Prep.md](Arc_II_Ascension/01_Session-Prep.md), [Arc_II_Ascension/03_Encounter-Guide.md](Arc_II_Ascension/03_Encounter-Guide.md), [Arc_II_Ascension/06_GM-Choice-Prompts.md](Arc_II_Ascension/06_GM-Choice-Prompts.md)
- Add: Insight, Observation, and Tactics at DR 2-3 when the party choose which claimant, gate, or neutral posture to approach first.
- Success: identify which claimant is politically exposed, which is militarily better placed, and which apparent neutral is actually brokering access.
- Failure: the party still enter fortress politics, but take on debt or hostility without understanding the real terms.

### 2. Claimant's Test Routes

- Files: [Arc_II_Ascension/01_Session-Prep.md](Arc_II_Ascension/01_Session-Prep.md), [Arc_II_Ascension/04_Choice-Guide.md](Arc_II_Ascension/04_Choice-Guide.md), [Arc_II_Ascension/06_GM-Choice-Prompts.md](Arc_II_Ascension/06_GM-Choice-Prompts.md), [Encounters/Arc_II_Ascension/05_Claimants-Test.md](Encounters/Arc_II_Ascension/05_Claimants-Test.md)
- Add: route-specific checks instead of only route labels.
- Formal challenge: Persuasion or Intimidation at DR 2 before the duel to frame what the duel proves publicly.
- Covert infiltration: Infiltration or Stealth at DR 2-3 to reach sealed records or barracks without triggering public dishonor.
- Investigation or exposure: Observation, Lore, or Insight at DR 2-3 to prove who altered an oath, hid evidence, or sabotaged a claimant.
- Success: the party shape public standing with evidence or ritual legitimacy instead of just surviving the route.
- Failure: the route still resolves, but the public answer is weaker or easier to spin.

### 3. Iron Echo Interpretation

- Files: [Arc_II_Ascension/01_Session-Prep.md](Arc_II_Ascension/01_Session-Prep.md), [Arc_II_Ascension/03_Encounter-Guide.md](Arc_II_Ascension/03_Encounter-Guide.md)
- Add: Lore, Blood Sense, and Forbidden Knowledge checks around the Echo's judgment, not only the existing contact test.
- Suggested DRs: DR 2 to understand what kind of oath or bloodline the chamber responds to; DR 3 to explain why the chamber's recognition mattered.
- Success: the party can name whether the Echo judged command integrity, inherited claim, martial continuity, or refusal of false legitimacy.
- Failure: the chamber's answer can still stand, but it will feel arbitrary to the table and easier for rivals to dismiss.

### 4. Northern Route and Hylden Contact Read

- Files: [Arc_II_Ascension/01_Session-Prep.md](Arc_II_Ascension/01_Session-Prep.md), [Arc_II_Ascension/03_Encounter-Guide.md](Arc_II_Ascension/03_Encounter-Guide.md)
- Add: Observation, Wilderness, Blood Sense, and Forbidden Knowledge at DR 2-3 while the party travel north and begin encountering Hylden traces.
- Success: distinguish rumor from active escalation, predict where the breach pressure is strongest, and understand whether the next scene is contact, ambush, or environmental crisis.
- Failure: the north still works as a pressure front, but the party meet it reactively rather than intelligently.

### 5. Rift Ember Negotiation and Breach Logic

- Files: [Arc_II_Ascension/01_Session-Prep.md](Arc_II_Ascension/01_Session-Prep.md), [Encounters/Arc_II_Ascension/04_Rift-Line-Emergency.md](Encounters/Arc_II_Ascension/04_Rift-Line-Emergency.md), [Encounters/Arc_II_Ascension/03_Breach-on-the-Northern-Ice.md](Encounters/Arc_II_Ascension/03_Breach-on-the-Northern-Ice.md)
- Add: Insight and Forbidden Knowledge checks before or during negotiation with Hylden logic, alongside the already-authored technical Glyphcasting, Focus, and Craft work.
- Suggested DRs: DR 3 to tell when Hylden claims are incomplete but not false; DR 4 only when the party are trying to read or rewrite the whole breach logic in real time.
- Success: the party understand what redirecting the node actually means before they commit to it.
- Failure: technical actions still exist, but the political and philosophical consequences land more as surprise than earned consequence.

### 6. Session 6 Impossible Record Handoff

- Files: [Arc_II_Ascension/01_Session-Prep.md](Arc_II_Ascension/01_Session-Prep.md)
- Add: Observation, Lore, Insight, and Persuasion checks when the breach resolves into an impossible witness, paradox wound, or tomorrow-dated record that points to the Chronoplast.
- Suggested DRs: DR 2 to confirm that the record really is impossible by ordinary historical standards; DR 3 to determine who should carry it, who should not see it yet, and whether its meaning should be stated publicly or protected as coalition-only evidence.
- Success: Arc III begins with a sharper, more intentional contradiction and a more credible Last Witness or proof-bearer.
- Failure: the party still gain the hook, but the evidence is easier to dispute, misfile, or politicize before it reaches the Chronoplast.

## Arc III: Revelation

### 1. Tracks in the Frost and Time-Scar Read

- Files: [Arc_III_Revelation/01_Session-Prep.md](Arc_III_Revelation/01_Session-Prep.md), [Arc_III_Revelation/VTT_Vignettes/01_Tracks-in-the-Frost.md](Arc_III_Revelation/VTT_Vignettes/01_Tracks-in-the-Frost.md)
- Add: Observation, Lore, and Insight at DR 2-3 when the party confront impossible tracks, contradictory sequence, and the first proof that chronology is compromised.
- Success: establish what is impossible, what is planted, and what the party can still trust enough to act on.
- Failure: the scene still unsettles them, but the GM has less mechanical guidance for who remains grounded and who becomes vulnerable to despair or manipulation.

### 2. The Wheel's Offer

- Files: [Arc_III_Revelation/01_Session-Prep.md](Arc_III_Revelation/01_Session-Prep.md), [Arc_III_Revelation/VTT_Vignettes/02_The-Wheel-Offers-Peace.md](Arc_III_Revelation/VTT_Vignettes/02_The-Wheel-Offers-Peace.md), [Arc_III_Revelation/03_Encounter-Guide.md](Arc_III_Revelation/03_Encounter-Guide.md)
- Add: Insight, Concentration, and Persuasion checks to make ideological resistance or temptation mechanically legible.
- Suggested DRs: DR 2 to catch what Bale is really offering; DR 3 to resist or counter his emotional logic cleanly.
- Success: the party distinguish peace from surrender, identify whether the room's calm is chosen or enforced, and manage a tempted ally without pure GM fiat.
- Failure: the Wheel's appeal remains live, and later betrayal, hesitation, or partial compliance feels mechanically earned.

### 3. Into the Chronoplast

- Files: [Arc_III_Revelation/01_Session-Prep.md](Arc_III_Revelation/01_Session-Prep.md), [Arc_III_Revelation/VTT_Vignettes/03_Into-the-Chronoplast.md](Arc_III_Revelation/VTT_Vignettes/03_Into-the-Chronoplast.md)
- Add: Lore, Forbidden Knowledge, and Observation checks before direct Shard contact.
- Suggested DRs: DR 2 to parse Moebian or Accord access logic; DR 3 to understand which contradiction matters and which is bait.
- Success: the party can enter with a framework for what the machine is doing and what kind of proof object matters.
- Failure: the Chronoplast still opens, but with more confusion and less ability to distinguish outcome from manipulation.

### 4. Witness, Record, and Betrayal Scenes

- Files: [Arc_III_Revelation/03_Encounter-Guide.md](Arc_III_Revelation/03_Encounter-Guide.md), [Arc_III_Revelation/VTT_Vignettes/04_The-Betrayal-Named.md](Arc_III_Revelation/VTT_Vignettes/04_The-Betrayal-Named.md), [Arc_III_Revelation/06_GM-Choice-Prompts.md](Arc_III_Revelation/06_GM-Choice-Prompts.md)

- Suggested DRs: DR 2 for reading a liar or frightened witness, DR 3 for holding coalition coherence while exposing the truth.
- Success: the party can keep a witness functional, prove the contradiction, or contain the fallout of naming the betrayal.
- Failure: the truth still emerges, but through accusation, panic, or uncontrolled escalation.

### 5. Session 5 Exit and Last Witness Stability

- Files: [Arc_III_Revelation/04_Choice-Guide.md](Arc_III_Revelation/04_Choice-Guide.md), [Arc_III_Revelation/VTT_Vignettes/05_Which-Future-Is-Real.md](Arc_III_Revelation/VTT_Vignettes/05_Which-Future-Is-Real.md)

### 6. Curator Thess Pursuit And Archive Navigation

- Files: [Arc_III_Revelation/01_Session-Prep.md](Arc_III_Revelation/01_Session-Prep.md)
- Add: Observation, Infiltration, Lore, and Glyphcasting checks around the pursuit-template spaces: narrowing mirror-stacks, purge lanes, and seal-glyph doors.
- Suggested DRs: DR 2 to read which route Thess is likely to take or which confirming detail is about to be lost; DR 3 to hold the proof intact while moving; DR 3-4 if the party try to preserve evidence and catch Thess simultaneously.
- Success: the party preserve cleaner confirming details and choose whether the priority is the witness, the record case, or the archive route itself.
- Failure: the pursuit still resolves, but some evidence is degraded, reframed, or physically separated from the people best able to interpret it.

## Campaign-Level Additions

### 1. Relic Dossiers As Recurring Check Prompts

- Files: [Relics/Sanguine-Knot.md](Relics/Sanguine-Knot.md), [Relics/Chronal-Shard.md](Relics/Chronal-Shard.md), [Relics/Black-Fulcrum.md](Relics/Black-Fulcrum.md)
- Add: a short recurring skill note to each relic file clarifying which skill should read proximity, meaning, and fallout.
- Suggested pattern:
- Blood Sense or Observation at DR 2 to notice fragment proximity, environmental shift, or personalized answer.
- Forbidden Knowledge or Lore at DR 3 to interpret what the fragment is revealing beyond first impression.
- Concentration or Insight at DR 2-3 when the real challenge is staying coherent under pressure rather than decoding a symbol.
- Benefit: relic scenes become mechanically consistent across arcs instead of relying entirely on GM instinct.

### 2. Faction Clock And Standing Read Scenes

- Files: [Faction-Tracker/Faction-Clocks.md](Faction-Tracker/Faction-Clocks.md), [Faction-Tracker/Faction-Standing.md](Faction-Tracker/Faction-Standing.md)
- Add: optional between-session or end-of-arc checks for the party to predict who moves next.
- Suggested skills: Insight, Observation, and Tactics at DR 2-3 after major public fallout, fragment transfer, or battlefield outcome.
- Success: the party correctly identify which faction will escalate first, which wing of a split faction is actually speaking, and who notices the hidden cost before everyone else does.
- Failure: the clocks still advance normally, but the next arc opens with more surprise pressure and less player ownership of the political map.
- Benefit: faction escalation becomes something the party can read and prepare for, not only something the GM updates backstage.

## Recommended Order Of Implementation

1. Arc I Session 1-2: Caravan Argument, False Refuge, and Session 2 investigation.
2. Arc II Session 2-3: Claimant's Test and Iron Echo interpretation.
3. Arc III Session 2-3: The Wheel's Offer and Into the Chronoplast.
4. Arc IV Session 1-3: Heath coalition, Hall legitimacy, and Fulcrum contact.

These are the scenes where explicit skill checks will produce the biggest gain in table-facing clarity with the least structural rewrite.
