# The Sanguine Knot

## Site

Blood-Well beneath Avernus Cathedral

## Associated Pressure

- Pillar pressure: States / Death
- Arc: Awakening

## First Impression

The fragment throbs like a buried heart and answers blood before language. It reveals lineage, oath-chains, stolen inheritance, and blood-rites hidden under polite identities.

## Site Logic

- The Sanguine Knot is not the Blood-Well itself.
- The Blood-Well is the older Janos-linked blood site beneath Avernus where blood-magic was first codified.
- The Knot is the Heart-fragment now lodged in that older wound and making blood-memory answer in the present campaign.
- The Cult of Hash'ak'gik did not create either layer, but it occupies the cathedral above and tries to profane every contact with the fragment.

## Primary Claimants

- Cult of Hash'ak'gik
- Sangromancers
- Sarafan inquisitors and confessors
- Any PC carrying blood-debts or hidden ancestry
- Balance-minded observers who recognize that blood truth can still be used unjustly

## Contact Cost

- Base test: DR 2-3 Will or Soul
- Failure: 1 Corruption and one involuntary vision of betrayal or blood inheritance
- Deliberate use: 1-2 Corruption depending on scale

## Practical Uses

- Expose false identities and blood-sworn obligations
- Identify ritual weak points in cult chambers or blood wards
- Reveal whether an oath was freely sworn, coerced, or inherited
- Force early confrontation between truth, mercy, and public shame

## Regional Fallout

- Dead relatives or betrayed patrons appear in Spectral overlap
- Oaths become harder to deny publicly
- Blood magic becomes easier and more dangerous nearby
- Sarafan legalists gain new evidence while hardliners gain new excuses
- Early Balance pressure appears when revelation is used to humiliate, dominate, or sanctify vengeance rather than clarify judgment

## GM Use

- When players blame Avernus for everything, split the answer: the cult explains the corruption on the approach, the Blood-Well explains the blood-memory beneath, and the Sanguine Knot explains why old truths are surfacing now.
