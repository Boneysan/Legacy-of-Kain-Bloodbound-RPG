# Arc IV: Convergence

## Arc Purpose

Arc IV resolves the campaign at mythic scale. Every surviving oath, grievance, and doctrine should arrive at the Pillar Heath with an army, rite, or ultimatum attached. This arc should feel like the judgment promised by earlier fragments, not merely the largest battle in the campaign.

## Level Band

- Recommended levels: 16-20
- Target length: 5-8 sessions

## Core Locations

- Pillar Heath
- Hall of Equilibrium
- Final Heart assembly site
- Battlefield zones where Material and Spectral overlap destabilize tactics
- Any surviving faction stronghold pulled into the final coalition map

## Active Factions

- Every surviving major faction
- Legendary NPCs as rare intervention points
- Any surviving cult or claimant from prior arcs who still has standing to matter
- At least one Balance-minded witness or Warden voice carrying forward what earlier fragment use has already cost

## Session Beats

1. Final alliance offers, betrayals, and irreconcilable demands.
2. The Black Fulcrum becomes accessible and no side can remain hidden.
3. The Hylden Gate and the Wheel are both forced into the same final equation.
4. A major battle, rite, or multi-front crisis at the Pillar Heath.
5. The party decide what the complete Heart is for.
6. End-state execution and immediate world consequences.

## Questions To Force

- What does saving Nosgoth mean in a world this damaged?
- Which outcome can the party defend in front of both the dead and the living?
- Which compromise would count as surrender even if it works?
- What is the party willing to preserve if striking at the Wheel worsens the Hylden wound, or stabilizing the wound leaves the Wheel stronger?

## Milestone Triggers

- The Black Fulcrum is claimed, ruined, or sealed.
- The final coalition is chosen by action rather than negotiation.
- Nosgoth enters its post-campaign state.
- The final relationship between the Wheel and the Hylden breach network is decided in practice, not only in rhetoric.

## Carry Forward

- The final state of the Pillars, the Wheel, and the Hylden Gate.
- Which factions survive with leverage.
- Which PCs become legends, fugitives, martyrs, or rulers.
- Whether the party's earlier claims about balance proved true, hypocritical, or incomplete.
