# Arc IV: Convergence Choice Guide

Use this document to track the arc's major narrative decisions. This guide focuses on coalition boundaries, the Black Fulcrum's purpose, and the final state of Nosgoth.

## Session 1: Coalition Boundaries

### Decide who is permitted near the party, the Pillars, and the assembled Heart

- Keep the coalition narrow: improves security and ideological clarity, but costs manpower, legitimacy, and possible specialist support.
- Keep it broad and temporary: maximizes resources, but guarantees betrayal risk and conflicting demands at the worst possible time.
- Exclude a faction entirely: simplifies one front, but often forces that bloc into open sabotage or violence.
- Carry forward: determines the alliance map, who can still be persuaded, and which enemies become unrecoverable before the Hall is reached.

## Session 2: Prove Coalition Legitimacy

### Choose sacrifice, compromise, or refusal on the road to the Hall

- Sacrifice for access: proves seriousness and earns passage, but exacts an immediate cost in lives, position, or principle.
- Compromise to keep the coalition intact: preserves numbers, but weakens moral clarity and may validate the wrong ally.
- Refuse the Hall's demanded terms: preserves integrity, but can cost time, access, or a wavering ally.
- Carry forward: reveals what kind of authority the party are actually building before they touch the Black Fulcrum.

## Session 3: What The Heart Is For

### Commit to an endgame direction at the Black Fulcrum

- Weaken or break the Wheel first: prioritizes freedom from inevitability, but risks worsening the Hylden wound and destabilizing Balance through shock.
- Stabilize the Hylden breach network first: preserves the world materially, but may leave the Wheel stronger and the deeper tyranny intact.
- Impose faction order through the Heart: creates governable structure quickly, but turns salvation into domination.
- Accept a catastrophic merge or fracture: rejects the old system entirely, but invites world-scale predation and collapse.
- Attempt a burdened compromise: preserves multiple goods imperfectly, but leaves the next age dependent on stewardship, vigilance, and visible sacrifice.
- Carry forward: this is the campaign's central choice and should be treated as the answer all later consequences flow from.

## Session 4: Keep The Chosen Ending Executable

### Decide what to protect when every front is collapsing

- Prioritize chamber control: keeps the rite or Fulcrum logic intact, but may sacrifice battlefield position or survivors.
- Prioritize battlefield control: preserves the wider coalition and the chance to hold the line, but may let the core rite fail.
- Prioritize survivor extraction or witness survival: protects the human meaning of victory, but can cost strategic dominance.
- Carry forward: determines who still has standing to shape the ending and how history will remember the price paid to secure it.

## Session 5: Final Execution

### Carry the final choice through to completion

- Seal, break, redirect, or remake the Heart's effect in line with the Session 3 commitment.
- Accept visible personal and political cost, or attempt to conceal the cost and risk a false-balance ending.
- Carry forward: determines the final state of the Pillars, the Wheel, the Hylden wound, and every surviving faction's place in the new order.
