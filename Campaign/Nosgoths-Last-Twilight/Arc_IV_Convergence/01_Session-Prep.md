# Arc IV Session Prep

Use this file as a rolling prep sheet for Sessions 1-5 of Convergence.

## Arc IV Defaults

- Core fragment: The Black Fulcrum
- Core pressure: coalition choice, Pillar fate, end-state consequences, irreversible sacrifice, and the final trade between anti-Wheel action and Hylden stability
- Core factions: every surviving major faction
- Kain usage: only in moments worthy of changing the campaign's final shape
- Recurring cast: see 02_NPC-Appendix.md
- Detailed encounter planning: see 04_Encounter-Guide.md
- GM tool: use 03_Endgame-Adjudication-Matrix.md once Session 3 fixes the party's real priority and again in Session 5 when final fallout is narrated.

## Session 1

- Purpose: Gather the surviving fronts and make every alliance conditional, costly, and temporary.
- Opening image: Across the Pillar Heath, campfires, siege sigils, funeral lanterns, and war banners burn under the same dead sky while every road in Nosgoth seems to end at the cracked stones.
- Primary scenes:
	- The party arrive at or near the Pillar Heath with everything from prior arcs now converging in visible form.
	- Final envoys, rivals, prophets, and survivors seek audience before battle or rite makes negotiation impossible.
	- The party must decide who is permitted near them, near the Pillars, and near the assembled Heart components.
	- Early scenes should make clear that no surviving faction is coming to witness; all are coming to shape the result.
	- The session ends when temporary alliance lines are drawn and at least one old relationship breaks under endgame pressure.
- Faction moves:
	- Every major surviving faction states its demand in actionable form.
	- Sarafan forces demand destruction or purification.
	- Clan survivors demand continuity, sovereignty, or repayment.
	- Pale Accord seek controlled restoration or recorded intervention.
	- Wheel loyalists seek burial, surrender, or paralysis.
	- Fracture agents want instability maximized.
	- Hylden interests want the final balance broken in their favor.
	- Balance-minded figures warn that every proposal is preserving one catastrophe by risking another.
- Relic pressure:
	- The Black Fulcrum is not yet directly accessible, but the assembled fragments begin reacting to one another.
	- Every prior fragment decision should echo here through dreams, omen, resonance, or faction leverage.
	- The party should feel that the campaign itself is being weighed.
	- Make explicit before Session 3 that the endgame is not only who rules Nosgoth, but whether the Heart is spent against the Wheel, spent stabilizing breach logic, or forced into a ruinous compromise between the two.
- Mandatory NPCs:
	- Kain or a clear Kain-aligned proxy
	- One faction representative the party trust least
	- One faction representative the party trust most
	- One survivor from Arc I or II to remind the table what this cost on the ground
	- One Balance-minded voice from earlier arcs or their successor, so the judgment frame arrives as continuity rather than surprise
- Encounter slot:
	- Preferred structure: social brinkmanship with limited violence at the edges.
	- Best use: assassination attempt, duel challenge, sabotage of parley space, or ritual theft attempt.
	- If combat breaks out, it should alter coalition math immediately.
- Spectral intrusion:
	- The dead of prior arcs gather invisibly around living negotiations.
	- The Pillars cast spectral shadows of what they once were and what they may become.
	- A party member may see which envoy dies first if no one intervenes.
- End-of-session fallout:
	- The party have a provisional coalition, a declared enemy bloc, or both.
	- At least one faction is now locked out of persuasion and moves to force.
	- The final road to the Hall of Equilibrium is identified or contested.
- Prep notes:
	- List every surviving faction before play and decide what each wants in one sentence.
	- Make every alliance cost something tangible.
	- Let history walk into the room through people, not exposition.

## Session 2

- Purpose: Open the road to the Hall of Equilibrium and make the final coalition visible.
- Opening image: The road to the Hall shifts across the Pillar Heath like a wound closing and reopening, forcing armies and supplicants alike to follow balance rather than formation.
- Primary scenes:
	- The party and their chosen allies attempt to reach the Hall of Equilibrium while enemy factions test their coalition's cohesion.
	- The route itself enforces judgment through changing terrain, mirrored dilemmas, and impossible shortcuts that only work for the wrong reasons.
	- The Hall teaches through contradiction: every oath answered by counter-oath, every certainty cut by its cost.
	- The session should force the party to prove coalition legitimacy through sacrifice, compromise, or refusal.
	- End when the Black Fulcrum becomes attainable and all hidden agendas are effectively exposed.
- Faction moves:
	- Allies ask for guarantees they cannot all receive.
	- Enemies attack supply, trust, or route rather than just bodies.
	- Kain's orbit pushes toward action without sentimentality.
	- Wheel and Fracture elements both try to weaponize hesitation, but toward opposite ends.
	- Sarafan legalists and Hollow hardliners should no longer be treated as interchangeable; one may accept constrained catastrophe while the other demands annihilation.
- Relic pressure:
	- The Black Fulcrum starts reacting to prior Corruption, bargains, and fragment use.
	- PCs may experience direct judgment scenes tied to earlier choices.
	- One faction should fail the Hall's logic and respond with open force.
	- The Hall should also force the party to face which previous "necessary" choice made the Hylden question worse and which made the Wheel harder to wound.
- Mandatory NPCs:
	- A Warden, Balance-minded scholar, or Hall guide if one still survives
	- One enemy envoy forced into proximity by the route
	- One ally whose commitment wavers under pressure
- Encounter slot:
	- Preferred structure: moving objective across hostile metaphysical terrain.
	- Best use: ambush during passage, defense of a ritual stop, or protection of someone needed to open the Hall.
	- If battle occurs, terrain and principle should matter more than raw attrition.
- Spectral intrusion:
	- The Hall shows vows as architecture and hypocrisy as cracks.
	- The dead argue silently beside the living at every major choice point.
	- One path exists only for those willing to admit what they have done.
- End-of-session fallout:
	- The final coalition is now visible to everyone.
	- One ally may be lost, exposed, or morally compromised on the road.
	- The Black Fulcrum is now in reach, and no side can pretend ignorance anymore.
- Prep notes:
	- Treat the Hall as a judge, not a dungeon.
	- Force at least one coalition-breaking conversation.
	- Make movement through the Heath feel like a test of worldview.

## Session 3

- Purpose: Put the Black Fulcrum in reach and force a decision under battlefield and metaphysical pressure.
- Opening image: The Black Fulcrum hangs within the Hall like a verdict made stone, and every fragment the party have touched begins answering it at once.
- Primary scenes:
	- The party gain access to the Black Fulcrum chamber while the battle for approach control continues around them.
	- First contact begins as judgment: the Fulcrum weighs every compromise, alliance, and corruption the campaign accumulated.
	- Each PC should receive a severe personal revelation tied to the true cost of the path they favored.
	- The chamber becomes a contested decision space where time, soul, and faction pressure collapse together.
	- End when the party commit to an endgame direction, even if the final act is not yet complete.
- Faction moves:
	- Allies try to steer the choice toward their interests.
	- Enemies push for interruption, desecration, or premature activation.
	- Kain, if present, should force clarity rather than provide comfort.
	- Wheel, Fracture, Hylden, Sarafan, and clan agendas should all become mutually legible here.
	- A Balance voice should challenge the party's preferred solution using evidence from the campaign, not abstract philosophy.
- Relic pressure:
	- First direct contact requires a DR 4 Will or Soul test.
	- Failure inflicts 1-2 Corruption and a severe revelation about self, party, or campaign cost.
	- Deliberate use costs 2-3 Corruption and causes immediate instability if mishandled.
	- The Fulcrum can complete, seal, break, or redirect the assembled Heart.
	- Make the three endgame pressures explicit in the chamber: anti-Wheel violence, Hylden-seal stabilization, and factional domination. The party should understand they cannot maximize all three.
- Mandatory NPCs:
	- Kain or the clearest opposing claimant to Kain's logic
	- One ally whose support is conditional on the party choosing correctly by their standards
	- One surviving ideological antagonist
- Encounter slot:
	- Preferred structure: relic decision under layered crisis.
	- Use chamber defense, rival intervention, collapsing route control, or simultaneous rite disruption.
	- If combat is minimal, intensify judgment and consequence instead.
- Spectral intrusion:
	- The chamber shows the party their dead, their undone futures, and the versions of themselves that would choose differently.
	- Material and Spectral reality should no longer remain tactically separate.
	- The Pillars themselves may react to different voiced outcomes.
- End-of-session fallout:
	- The party choose what the Heart is for.
	- Some allies immediately become enemies and some enemies may accept the chosen logic for pragmatic reasons.
	- The final battle or rite in Session 4 is now inevitable.
	- State clearly which side of the final equation the choice favors: weakening the Wheel, stabilizing the breach network, preserving faction order, or accepting a catastrophic split between them.
- Prep notes:
	- Make the choice narrower, not broader. By this point, the party should be choosing between terrible answers, not abstract possibilities.
	- Ensure each PC has a stake in the judgment.
	- Do not let the Fulcrum be just another artifact; it is the campaign's reckoning.
	- Once the party commit, mark their primary answer on the endgame adjudication matrix before running Session 4 at full scale.

## Session 4

- Purpose: Resolve the main battle, rite, or layered crisis that decides who still has standing to shape Nosgoth.
- Opening image: The Pillar Heath becomes battlefield, altar, and graveyard at once, with armies colliding under flaring monoliths and spectral weather tearing at every banner.
- Primary scenes:
	- The party execute the chosen rite, defense, destruction, or redirection while the surviving factions commit to force.
	- Multiple fronts should matter: chamber control, battlefield control, survivor extraction, and interruption of enemy objectives.
	- The old political order and the cosmic order should feel equally unstable.
	- Legendary figures, if used, appear only at decisive moments.
	- End when only one viable path to the world's immediate future remains open.
- Faction moves:
	- Sarafan push to purge.
	- Sarafan legalists may push for containment and lawful custody even while hardliners push extermination.
	- Clans push to preserve or claim.
	- Hylden push to rupture balance.
	- Wheel loyalists push to restore inevitability.
	- Fracture push to merge boundaries beyond recall.
	- Accord push to stabilize or record, sometimes at terrible cost.
- Relic pressure:
	- Every prior fragment should now be manifesting through the battle: oath pressure, blood revelations, breach instability, contradictory futures, and balance collapse.
	- The Black Fulcrum amplifies whatever answer the party attempt to impose.
	- Mishandling should have visible, immediate regional consequences.
	- Show the price of the chosen answer in both metaphysical theaters: what happens to the Wheel's hold, and what happens to the Hylden wound.
- Mandatory NPCs:
	- Any surviving major antagonist who earned endgame presence
	- Any ally the party would genuinely risk the ending for
	- One witness whose survival determines how the world remembers this
- Encounter slot:
	- Preferred structure: layered crisis, not a single boss arena.
	- Use moving fronts, endangered pillars, ritual sequences, and shifting terrain.
	- If using a main boss or legendary threat, keep that figure embedded in the larger crisis rather than isolated from it.
- Spectral intrusion:
	- The dead of Nosgoth should feel present in judgment, not just as scenery.
	- Spectral and Material layers cross freely enough to reshape tactics scene by scene.
	- One battlefield area should become a memory of an older war before returning to the present.
- End-of-session fallout:
	- The world's next state is effectively decided.
	- Factions are broken, crowned, annihilated, vindicated, or left maimed.
	- Session 5 becomes consequence rather than uncertainty.
	- The party now know whether Balance was restored, imposed, faked, or merely invoked by the victors.
- Prep notes:
	- Track multiple objectives visibly.
	- Let hard choices cost real NPCs, territory, or future options.
	- Keep the focus on what the party are deciding, not only what they are defeating.

## Session 5

- Purpose: Execute the final choice and show immediate consequences in the world, the factions, and the party.
- Opening image: After the rite or catastrophe, Nosgoth answers immediately: the sky changes, the Pillars flare or fail, the dead react, and every surviving witness understands that the old world is gone in some essential way.
- Primary scenes:
	- The party carry the final choice through to completion: sealing, breaking, redirecting, or remaking the Heart's effect.
	- Immediate outcomes ripple across the Pillar Heath, surviving armies, and any connected sites the GM wants to cut to briefly.
	- Each major surviving faction receives a consequence consistent with the party's choice.
	- The party confront what they have become in the process: rulers, fugitives, martyrs, stewards, or unresolved anomalies.
	- The session closes with one final image of Nosgoth's new state.
- Faction moves:
	- Survivors either submit, flee, regroup, or swear continuation of the conflict under altered terms.
	- Kain's final stance should be clear if he survived into the ending.
	- If the Wheel, Hylden, or Fracture survive in force, their future shape must be legible.
- Relic pressure:
	- The Black Fulcrum's effect is now active, spent, broken, or transformed.
	- Corruption, dreams, or soul-scars from direct use should remain part of the ending.
	- The fragment consequences should not disappear simply because the campaign is ending.
- Mandatory NPCs:
	- Any surviving figure central to the chosen ending
	- One witness of the common people, not just powers and monsters
	- One dead or unrealized presence in the final spectral coda
- Encounter slot:
	- Combat is optional here.
	- If used, it should be a final pursuit, mercy killing, escape, or sacrifice scene rather than a full battle.
	- The climax is consequence, not another health pool.
- Spectral intrusion:
	- The dead, the denied, and the not-yet-born all feel close at the end.
	- Close with a spectral acknowledgment of what was saved, lost, or merely postponed.
	- Let one image linger that confirms the choice had metaphysical weight.
- End-of-session fallout:
	- Establish the final state of the Pillars, the Wheel, the Hylden Gate, and the dominant powers of Nosgoth.
	- Record which factions survive with leverage.
	- Record what each PC becomes in the setting's memory.
	- Record whether Balance was restored, imposed, faked, or merely invoked by the victors.
	- End with consequence, not tidy closure.
- Prep notes:
	- Pay off every major choice you can without turning the ending into a ledger.
	- Give the players time to inhabit the aftermath.
	- Leave one thread unresolved only if that unresolved thread says something meaningful about Nosgoth.
	- Use the endgame adjudication matrix to keep Session 5 specific: pick one core outcome, one coalition modifier, and one pressure modifier rather than improvising every consequence from scratch.

## Compact Templates

### Pillar Heath Seal-Breaker Push

- Use in Session 4 when one battlefield front must decide whether the party's chosen end-state can even be executed.
- Baseline enemy force for a level 16 party with TV 64: 1 Hylden Dimension Lord (TV 18), 1 Hylden Rift-Priest (TV 11), 2 Hylden Shock Troopers (TV 18 total), and 4 Hylden-Possessed Grunts (TV 12 total), for total TV 59.
- For levels 18-20, add 1 Hylden War-Stalker (TV 7) or 1 Wraith of the Abyss (TV 9) depending on whether you want the front to feel more military or more metaphysically ruptured.
- Terrain: cracked Pillar trench, two rift pylons feeding the assault, and a contested high ground where a witness, Hall guide, or rite operator must survive long enough to complete the party's answer.
- Tactics: the Dimension Lord uses the Troopers and Grunts to pin the line while the Rift-Priest contaminates the zone, opens movement breaks, and threatens possession transfers if ignored. The Lord's job is not only to kill PCs, but to force them off the operator, witness, or Fulcrum support task.
- Win condition: hold the operator zone for 4 rounds, destroy both pylons, or kill the Rift-Priest before the Lord can convert battlefield pressure into a permanent breach gain.
- Complication twist: if the party already chose to wound the Wheel first, increase visible instability each round. If they chose stabilization first, increase enemy reinforcements and factional sabotage instead. The same template should feel different depending on the adjudication-matrix row they already committed to.
