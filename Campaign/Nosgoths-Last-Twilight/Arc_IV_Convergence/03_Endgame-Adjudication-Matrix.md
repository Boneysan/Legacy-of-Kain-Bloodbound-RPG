# Arc IV Endgame Adjudication Matrix

Use this sheet when the party reach the Black Fulcrum and you need a fast ruling on what their choice does to the Wheel, the Hylden wound, the Pillars, and the surviving powers of Nosgoth.

## How To Use

1. Identify the party's primary answer at the Fulcrum. If they argue for multiple goals, choose the one they are most willing to sacrifice for.
2. Apply the matching row from the core matrix.
3. Add one coalition modifier for the bloc that most shaped the rite or battle outcome.
4. Add one pressure modifier from the clocks or prior fragment history if the campaign has clearly earned it.
5. Use the final row result to narrate Session 5 fallout and record the post-campaign state.

## Coalition Modifier

Apply one modifier for the bloc that contributed the most decisive force, rite logic, or surviving leverage.

| Dominant Bloc | Modifier |
|:---|:---|
| Kain or Kain-aligned actors | The result is harder to narrate as surrender. Even failures feel chosen. Add one surviving anti-fate symbol, ally, or doctrine to the ending. |
| Wardens or Balance-minded coalition | The result gains moral clarity but loses political comfort. Add one explicit judgment against the victors' hypocrisy, even if the ending is broadly beneficial. |
| Pale Accord | The result is better documented and slightly more stable in the short term, but dangerous knowledge survives in records, devices, and witnesses. |
| Legalist Sarafan | Custody, quarantine, and doctrine survive more intact than the party may prefer. The ending looks more governable and more punitive at once. |
| Hollow Sarafan | Purge logic stains the ending. Even a strategically sound outcome is remembered through fire, extermination, and terror. |
| Vampire clan coalition | Continuity and territorial order recover faster, but the ending leaves hierarchy, blood-debt, and domination more legitimate than before. |
| Hylden-backed solution | The breach side of the equation becomes more efficient, but trust collapses across every non-Hylden bloc and the long-term metaphysical cost rises. |
| Wheel loyalist influence | Civil collapse slows briefly, but freedom of action narrows. The ending feels safer to institutions and worse to the soul of the world. |
| Crimson Fracture influence | Survivor conversion, feral doctrine, and merge rhetoric spread even if the party technically avoided a full Fracture ending. |

## Pressure Modifier

Apply one modifier if a clock or repeated campaign pattern clearly matters at the finale.

| Pressure State | Modifier |
|:---|:---|
| Balance Reckoning Clock at 3-4 | The Hall's judgment hardens. Any outcome claimed as Balance must also show what cost the victors accept personally, or observers name it false balance. |
| Hylden Breach Clock at 3-4 | Any anti-Wheel answer is harder to contain. Increase visible regional instability, refugee fallout, and the need for emergency seal-work. |
| Wheel Compliance Clock at 3-4 | Civil populations, mourners, and institutions are more likely to accept surrender, burial, or managed truth even after the party's victory. |
| Fracture Merge Clock at 3-4 | Even non-Fracture endings leave behind merge cults, breach pilgrims, and predators who call the crisis revelation. |
| Sarafan Schism Clock at 3-4 | Sarafan fallout splits into legal aftermath and retaliatory purges rather than a single clean reaction. |
| Repeated fragment use to solve crises by force | The ending inherits legitimacy problems. Allies ask whether the party saved Nosgoth or merely taught it to depend on another catastrophic instrument. |

## Balance Verdict Shortcuts

Use one of these labels in Session 5 and in your final campaign summary.

- Restored Balance: the party accepted real limits, bore visible cost, and did not merely hand catastrophe to a preferred faction.
- Imposed Balance: the world is stabilized, but under coercive terms that many survivors will call domination.
- False Balance: the victors used the language of equilibrium to justify appetite, fear, or factional control.
- Witnessed Balance: the party did not solve the contradiction so much as keep Nosgoth from breaking completely, leaving the burden to the next age.
