# Arc IV: Convergence GM Choice Prompts

Use this document when the endgame turns on a decision and you want a direct answer to what confrontation, fallout, or pressure to run next.

## Session 1: Coalition Boundaries

### If the party keep the coalition narrow

- Run elite infiltration, shortage pressure, or a defense scene where every missing ally is felt.
- Give the march to the Hall ideological clarity and practical fragility.
- Let excluded blocs become later spoilers because they know they are being written out of the verdict.

### If the party keep the coalition broad

- Run multi-front negotiation, sabotage, or violence caused by too many agendas in one camp.
- Make the party stronger on paper and less coherent in motion.
- Leave more surviving witnesses later to contest what the ending meant.

### If the party exclude a faction entirely

- Run last offers, revenge sabotage, or immediate hostile action from that bloc.
- Make exclusion feel like an irreversible political act rather than a clean sorting choice.
- Bring that bloc back later as the force most committed to spoiling final execution or the aftermath.

## Session 2: Prove Coalition Legitimacy

### If the party choose sacrifice

- Run rear-guard, ordeal, or blood-bought passage to the Hall.
- Make the cost visible enough that Wardens and survivors must acknowledge it.
- Let martyrs, debts, and testimony from survivors shape the aftermath.

### If the party choose compromise

- Run negotiation under arms, alternate route control, or a scene where access is bought dirty.
- Preserve numbers and legitimacy with one bloc while corroding them with another.
- Bring the compromise back later as proof that the party won passage but not innocence.

### If the party choose refusal

- Run access denial, time-pressure movement, or a coalition-breaking argument on the road.
- Preserve integrity and cost momentum immediately.
- Use the refusal later as proof either of principle or of endangering the world for purity.

## Session 3: What The Heart Is For

### If the party commit to breaking the Wheel first

- Run split-field play where one front protects the Fulcrum and another contains the immediate metaphysical backlash.
- Make freedom the declared priority even as the sky answers with danger.
- Let later fallout show unrest among the dead and a world freer but less governed.

### If the party commit to stabilizing the breach first

- Run defensive crisis play around seals, operators, and anything that keeps the wound from widening.
- Make material survival the declared priority even if it feels like surrender to deeper tyranny.
- Bring back anti-Wheel grievance later from anyone who thinks the real work was postponed.

### If the party commit to imposed faction order

- Run suppression, custody, and battlefield discipline where the ending already looks like rule rather than healing.
- Reveal immediately which allies wanted stewardship and which wanted domination.
- Let the next age inherit insurgency, resentment, and legitimacy crises.

### If the party commit to merge or fracture

- Run mass panic, predator emergence, and battlefield geometry choosing victims in real time.
- Make collapse feel liberating only to those who were always ready to prey on it.
- Leave behind pilgrims, predators, and catastrophe-faith movements in the aftermath.

### If the party attempt a burdened compromise

- Run multi-objective crisis play: hold the chamber, preserve key seals, and keep witnesses alive.
- Make stewardship rather than triumph the core tone of the ending.
- Use the surviving strain later as the cost of choosing a world that still needs guardians.

## Session 4: Keep The Chosen Ending Executable

### If the party prioritize chamber control

- Run close defense and ritual interruption with the rest of the battlefield going visibly wrong.
- Keep the chosen answer executable at the cost of making abandonment visible.
- Let those left outside the chamber become later accusers, martyrs, or proof of what victory cost.

### If the party prioritize battlefield control

- Run command fights, rescue lanes, and shifting fronts while the Fulcrum window narrows.
- Preserve more people and more legitimacy while risking the core rite.
- Let survivors later say the party saved the world around the choice but nearly lost the choice itself.

### If the party prioritize witness or survivor extraction

- Run rescue-through-catastrophe with history itself depending on who gets out alive.
- Make the party choose living meaning over optimal control.
- Turn saved witnesses later into rulers, accusers, historians, or the only people who can defend the verdict.

## Session 5: Final Execution

### If the party openly accept visible cost

- Run the finish as sacrificial completion under direct assault.
- Make the ending legible through what is lost, not merely what is won.
- Let history remember the verdict as tragic but defensible.

### If the party try to conceal the price

- Run interruption, witness survival, or a final push by those who recognize false balance immediately.
- Make the ending feel orderly for one breath and unstable underneath it.
- Bring the hidden cost back later through tribunals, heresies, and the collapse of claimed legitimacy.
