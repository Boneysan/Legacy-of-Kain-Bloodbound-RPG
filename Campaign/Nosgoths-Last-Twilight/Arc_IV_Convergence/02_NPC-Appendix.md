# Arc IV NPC Appendix

Use these as the recurring cast for Convergence. Replace any dead or absent figure with the faction's next-hardest surviving voice, but preserve the role the scene needs.

## Kain

- Role: Legendary claimant to the future of Nosgoth
- Public face: Scion of Balance, wielder of the purified Soul Reaver, and the one figure every faction must account for whether they trust him or not
- Hidden truth: He still does not control the outcome, and that is part of what makes him dangerous
- Wants from the party: A choice strong enough to matter and clear enough to survive history
- Best use: Rare intervention, never constant patronage
- If absent: His absence must still feel like deliberate pressure, not missing content

## Warden Asha Merel

- Role: Balance-minded mediator or Hall guide
- Public face: Severe, lucid, and committed to a form of balance that does not flatter anyone present
- Hidden truth: She already believes some factions should not survive the ending as intact powers
- Wants from the party: An answer that can be defended in principle, not just in victory
- Best use: Give the Hall and the Black Fulcrum a human voice of judgment
- If lost: Let the Hall itself take over her function more harshly, or promote Sister Nerys Vale's warnings into Asha's surviving records so the judgment voice still feels continuous

## Grand Castellan Garric Sol

- Role: Sarafan endgame commander
- Public face: Purifier willing to reduce the Heath to ash if that is what it takes to deny corruption victory
- Hidden truth: He fears half-measures more than monsters
- Wants from the party: Submission, proof, or justification to destroy what they built
- Best use: Personify the logic of cleansing at catastrophic scale
- If lost: The Sarafan become more chaotic but not less dangerous

## Canon-Examiner Ser Jorun Bale

- Role: Sarafan legalist endgame counterweight
- Public face: Coldly procedural examiner who still believes custody, proof, and constrained authority matter even at the end of the world
- Hidden truth: He knows his faction may already be past saving, and is trying to preserve law from the wreck of Sarafan identity rather than the other way around
- Wants from the party: A solution that can be justified without extermination becoming the only language left in Nosgoth
- Best use: Keep the Sarafan split alive in the finale so Garric Sol is a factional answer, not the whole order
- If lost: Let his ledgers, warrants, or testimony survive as the last legal indictment of Sarafan collapse

## Matriarch Veila Turel

- Role: Clan sovereign or coalition claimant
- Public face: Aristocratic vampire authority arguing that continuity, not purity, is how worlds survive collapse
- Hidden truth: She is fully willing to call domination stewardship if it wins the field
- Wants from the party: Recognition that vampire order is preferable to metaphysical ruin
- Best use: Give the clans an endgame voice more strategic than merely monstrous
- If lost: Replace her with whichever surviving clan power best represents continuity through rule

## The Glass Voice Reforged

- Role: Hylden endgame representative
- Public face: Rational, alien, and contemptuous of every solution built on reverence
- Hidden truth: Even Hylden strategy now depends on forces it cannot fully control
- Wants from the party: One break in balance large enough to matter forever
- Best use: Keep Hylden logic alive in the endgame whether or not the original negotiator survived Arc II, especially when the party must choose between striking the Wheel and stabilizing the breach network
- If lost: Let devices, projections, or possessed agents carry its agenda

## Sepulcher-Mother Elain

- Role: Wheel loyalist or endgame funerary authority
- Public face: Calm prophet of surrender who speaks of endings as restoration of order
- Hidden truth: She knows the Wheel has always fed on obedience, and chooses that truth anyway
- Wants from the party: Hesitation, burial, and rejection of free-willed catastrophe
- Best use: Put inevitability on the field as a live option, not just a background doctrine
- If lost: Keep her followers present as mournful enforcers of inevitability

## The Last Witness

- Role: Common-person perspective on the ending
- Public face: Survivor, refugee, soldier, healer, or child who has no right to decide the world and still must live in it
- Hidden truth: Their memory of the ending may be what future Nosgoth treats as truth
- Wants from the party: Something survivable, even if not something pure
- Best use: Prevent the ending from becoming only a conversation among powers
- If lost: Replace with another witness quickly; the ending needs eyes from below

## Ash-Glass Reth

- Role: Crimson Fracture endgame temptation and surviving field leader of the Ash-Glass Procession
- Public face: Scarred apostle of instability arguing that fracture at least ends the lie of imposed order, now followed by breach-marked converts gathered from every prior wound-site
- Hidden truth: Reth has become unable to distinguish liberation from trauma, which is precisely why their rhetoric remains dangerous; by the finale, the Procession itself is proof of how many people catastrophe has made recruitable
- Wants from the party: One admission that the merged wound is preferable to another age of managed ruin
- Best use: Ensure the Fracture reaches the ending as a living temptation rather than a background slogan
- If lost: Let their doctrine survive in followers, slogans, and the breach-scarred terrain itself
