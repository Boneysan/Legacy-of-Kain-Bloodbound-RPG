# 02 — The Hall Tests the Coalition

The Hall of Equilibrium does not make decisions. It asks whether the people claiming to make them are honest about what they are claiming. Warden Asha Merel has been authorized to admit one coalition for formal proceedings — one unified group with one account of what the Heart is for. This scene is the Hall's assessment. It is not a hearing. It is a test of whether the party's coalition is real enough to be accountable for.

## Opening Narration

> The Hall of Equilibrium was built before the Pillars fell, and the design predates them — which means it was built around the assumption that balance was real and required tending, not around the hope that it might be recovered. The geometry insists on it. Every angle of sightline converges on the center of the floor, and every person in the room knows they are being observed from every position simultaneously. Warden Asha Merel is standing at the center. She has the quality of someone who arrived before anyone else and will remain when everyone else has gone. When the party enters, she does not announce the procedure. She begins.

---

## NPC Opening Line

> **Warden Asha Merel:** *"The Hall's question is one question, and it is the same for every coalition: Can you state — clearly, together, without revision — what you intend to do with the Heart of Nosgoth, what you will do with the factions you are not including, and who among you is prepared to answer if the account turns out to be wrong?"*

> She looks at the party. *"I will hear it from whoever speaks first."*

---

## Decision Pressure

- Choice A should feel like stepping into authorship and liability at the same time.
- Choice B should feel principled until someone asks who pays if the principle fails.
- Choice C should feel humble and dangerous: incomplete honesty is still honesty, but it buys only provisional trust.
- Choice D should feel like choosing to ground the coalition in lived consequence rather than procedural elegance.

---

## Player Choices

### A — State the Account Clearly and Name the Responsible Party

> The party delivers a full, direct answer: what they intend, who they've excluded and why, and who within the party accepts accountability if the outcome doesn't hold.

**NPC Response (Merel):**

> She listens to the entire statement without interruption. Then: *"Thank you. The Hall notes the statement and the named accountability."* She looks at the rest of the party. *"Does anyone present wish to revise or dissent from the account as given?"*

> If no one dissents: *"Then you are recognized as a coalition with standing. You will be challenged. Your standing does not protect you from challenge — it means your challenger has to meet the same standard of account."*

> If someone dissents: *"Then we continue until you share an account, or until you ask me to recognize two separate coalitions, which the Hall can also do. That is a harder position to hold in the proceedings that follow."*

**Outcome:** Full recognition with named accountability. The faction that was named as excluded will challenge formally — but on the Hall's ground, where their own account can be examined. `black_fulcrum_status` moves to `active`.

**Tags:** `black_fulcrum_status: active`, `coalition_shape: recognized`

---

### B — Present the Account But Refuse to Name a Responsible Person

> The coalition is presented collectively. No individual is designated as the accountable party.

**NPC Response (Merel):**

> A quiet pause. *"The Hall can recognize collective accountability. It has before. It is harder to adjudicate after the fact, and it gives opposing coalitions a procedural argument that your accountability is diffuse."* She looks at the group. *"I am not recommending against it. I am informing you of what it means when someone asks who answers for the decision."*

> If the party holds to collective accountability, she recognizes them — with a notation. The Black Fulcrum's examination in Vignette 04 will probe specifically at this joint, testing whether collective accountability can hold under the pressure of a single catastrophic hypothetical.

**Outcome:** Recognized with collective accountability. Harder Vignette 04. The Last Witness in the gallery makes a note.

**Tags:** `black_fulcrum_status: active-collective`, `coalition_shape: recognized`

---

### C — Ask Merel Whether the Hall Has a Preference

> The party isn't refusing to answer — they want to know whether the procedure has a preferred form.

**NPC Response (Merel):**

> *"The Hall prefers honesty over form. If you have not decided who answers, say you have not decided. That is a true account. I can recognize a coalition that has not resolved all its internal questions. I cannot recognize a coalition that gives me an account they do not believe."*

> If the party then gives an honest answer — even an incomplete one — she accepts it. *"That is enough. Partial accounts with acknowledged gaps are recognized under the Hall's longer tradition. You will be asked to complete the account before the final proceedings."*

**Outcome:** Partial recognition, requiring completion at Vignette 04's Fulcrum examination. Buys time. The Last Witness approaches the party after the session.

**Tags:** `black_fulcrum_status: partial`, `coalition_shape: conditionally-recognized`

---

### D — Ask the Last Witness to Speak Before the Party Gives Its Account

> The party spots the civilian observer in the gallery and calls them as a witness before giving their own statement. This is unusual but not prohibited.

**NPC Response (Merel):**

> A pause. *"That is not standard procedure."* She looks at the gallery. *"However, the Witness arrived with valid standing, and the Hall's tradition permits civilian testimony when it precedes the principal account. If the Witness consents."*

> **The Last Witness**, if called, gives a brief account from a ground-level perspective — what they have seen the party do, what they believe the party is trying to build, and what they need from the outcome. The account is factual and unembellished and it is the most human thing that will be said in the Hall today.

> After the Witness speaks, the party's own account is expected. But the Witness's testimony becomes part of the formal record, which means any opposing coalition that challenges the party's account now has to address the Witness's account directly. It is harder to use procedural argument against a person who does not use procedural argument.

**Outcome:** The Last Witness is bound into the coalition's formal record. Vignette 04 includes them as a participant in the Fulcrum examination, which changes its emotional register significantly.

**Tags:** `black_fulcrum_status: witness-anchored`, `coalition_shape: witness-anchored-recognition`

---

## If No Clear Choice Is Made

> If the party cannot give a coherent account and does not request partial recognition, the Hall does not recognize the coalition. Merel notes the impasse and schedules a secondary session. `coalition_shape` is recorded as `unrecognized`. This is not a campaign ender — but every faction in Vignettes 03 and 04 now operates knowing the Hall hasn't backed the party, which shifts negotiating leverage.

**Default Tag:** `coalition_shape: unrecognized`, `black_fulcrum_status: dormant`

## GM Notes

- Merel is neutral but not indifferent. She has already told the party (in Vignette 01) that she believes some factions should not survive as intact powers. She is using the Hall's procedure to shape which accounts become official — and she is good at it.
- The Last Witness's testimony, if called, should be specific to what the party has actually done in the campaign. The GM should brief this moment: what did ordinary people experience because of the party's choices in Arcs I–III? Not judgment — witness. Accurate, plain, weighted.
- If `betrayal_resolved` is still `unresolved` from Arc III, Merel will ask about it directly when reviewing the coalition's internal standing: *"You have an unresolved breach in your prior relationships. Is that breach present in your coalition's current operation, or has it been contained?"* The party's answer changes how the Hall handles their coalition.
- The Hall chamber should feel ancient and indifferent in the most neutral sense — not hostile, not warm. The geometry serves accountability, not comfort. The party should feel the weight of the room without feeling it is working against them.
