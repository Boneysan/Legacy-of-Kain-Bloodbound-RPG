# 03 — What Reth Wants

By the time Ash-Glass Reth arrives with this question, the Procession is not small anymore. Every structure that failed in Arcs I and II recruited for it. The question Reth is asking is not whether the party will join — they know the answer to that — but whether the party will admit what they are trying to prevent, and whether what they're trying to prevent is actually distinguishable from what Reth is already doing in the parts of Nosgoth the party hasn't reached.

## Opening Narration

> Reth chose the meeting point: the edge of the Heath where the Ash-Glass Procession's attendants set up three days ago and have not moved. The camp has the quality of somewhere people arrived in flight and stayed until it became structure. There are people here who were not making a political choice when the Procession found them — they were making the only available choice, which is what Reth will tell the party, and Reth is not wrong about that part. The Procession's three attendants maintain a comfortable distance without being instructed to. Reth has the manner of someone who has not been uncertain about anything for long enough that they have forgotten what certainty felt like from the outside.

---

## NPC Opening Line

> **Ash-Glass Reth:** *"I want to show you something before you give me your formal answer."* They indicate the camp without pointing at any individual. *"These are people who were in Sarafan lock-up, Wheel service, or clan debt when the current emergency broke open the structures that held them. They did not come to the Procession because of a philosophy. They came because the Procession was moving and the alternative was standing still in a place that was falling."* A pause. *"What I want to know is what you are offering them that I'm not."*

---

## Player Choices

### A — We're Offering Them a Future That Has a Structure

> The party's coalition represents a future with law, balance, or negotiated coexistence — not chaos.

**NPC Response (Reth):**

> *"I know you believe that. I believed it too, about four hundred people ago, when I was describing a future rather than building it."* They look at the party with something that is not quite contempt and not quite pity. *"The structures you're describing — who decided they were the right ones? Who was in the room? Who wrote the law? It wasn't these people."*

> If Silk-Without-Moon is present, she quietly: *"The point is not whether the future has structure, Reth. The point is whether the structure is a crypt."*

> Reth, without looking at her: *"Or a faction."*

**Outcome:** The party has made the case for structure without making the case for their specific structure. Reth does not join — but the party's challenge has named the accountability gap in their own coalition that they will have to address in Vignette 04. `wheel_final_state` is not changed here — but the gap is on record.

**Tags:** `coalition_shape: structure-challenged`

---

### B — We're Offering Them a Seat in the Decision

> The coalition intends to include survivor voices — including Procession members — in the deliberation about what happens to the Heart.

**NPC Response (Reth):**

> A long pause. *"Show me the mechanism."*

> If the party can describe how — formally or informally — the people in this camp get a voice in the outcome: Reth is not converted, but they offer a conditional alignment. They will not dismantle the Procession. They will not attend the Hall. But they will not move against the party's coalition in the final proceedings, and they will direct Procession resources away from interference with the convergence while it is live.

> If the party cannot describe the mechanism — if the "seat" is theoretical — Reth says: *"Then what you're describing is not a seat. It's a promise. I've had promises."*

**Outcome:** Reth is not converted, but they offer conditional alignment — the Procession will not move against the party's coalition in the final proceedings and will direct Procession resources away from interference while it is live. **This choice requires the party to name the specific mechanism at the table.** If no mechanism can be described, the party has not truly chosen this branch; move to Choice D or accept the default outcome.

**Tags:** `coalition_shape: procession-neutral`

---

### C — Ask Reth What They Would Do With the Heart

> Not a debate. A genuine question. The parties' positions are already known. Ask what's under it.

**NPC Response (Reth):**

> They are quiet for a moment. This is not a question they have been asked. *"I would put it somewhere no single coalition could reach it. Encode the access conditions in the Hall's public record — so its activation requires demonstrated, verifiable consent from the factions currently excluded from this conversation."* They study the party. *"I know that isn't what you want. I'm answering honestly."*

> A beat. *"The version of that answer I gave two years ago was different. The version I would have given before the Procession was different again. I'm not sure if I've gotten more radical or more realistic. I genuinely cannot tell anymore."*

**Outcome:** This exchange does not produce an alliance, but it produces an honest account of what Reth actually wants — which is different from what `fracture-collapse` implies. The party now has the option, in the Hall proceedings, of citing Reth's stated position as a legitimate policy option rather than an extremist stance. This shifts the Wheel's endgame calculation. `wheel_final_state` is updated to `contested` rather than `default`.

**Tags:** `wheel_final_state: contested`, `hylden_gate_resolution: reth-adjacent` (if Reth's stated position happens to align with incomplete gate activation)

---

### D — Tell Reth Directly What the Party Believes the Procession Has Cost

> Not an argument about the philosophy. A statement about the specific losses the party has traced back to Procession activity.

**NPC Response (Reth):**

> They listen to the entire statement. Then: *"I know."* Flat, without deflection. *"Not those specific instances — but I know the cost. I started keeping the account when we were forty people. I stopped when it became — not manageable, but theoretical. Which is when I understood I had become something I wouldn't have recognized five years ago."*

> They do not apologize. But: *"I'm not asking you to forget it. I'm asking whether the coalition you're building can do what it claims to do with that account active, or whether you need to resolve it here before you go into the Hall."*

**Outcome:** Reth has named the party's own unresolved grief as a strategic liability. This is genuinely useful, even though it comes from someone the party may oppose. The party cannot change what happened — but how they respond to this naming determines whether the grief is a fracture or an anchor in Vignettes 04 and 05.

**Tags:** `coalition_shape: grief-named`

## If No Clear Choice Is Made

> If the party refuses to engage substantively and simply rejects the approach, Reth receives the refusal politely and leaves. The Procession moves to active interference posture. `coalition_shape: procession-hostile`. It does not close down Arc IV paths — but two coalition logistics scenes in the background become complications rather than conveniences.

**Default Tag:** `coalition_shape: procession-hostile`

## GM Notes

- Reth is not a villain in this scene. They are a person who has been doing a thing for long enough that they have lost the ability to see it clearly — which is a far more frightening category. The GM's goal is to make the party feel the genuine force of Reth's position while also feeling the cost of what the Procession has become.
- The Silk-Without-Moon exchange, if she is present, should be brief and not resolved. She and Reth have a history of mutual understanding that produced divergent outcomes, and neither of them is comfortable with what the other represents. Let that sit in the room without explaining it.
- Reth's honest answer in Choice C is one of the most important moments in the campaign. "I'm not sure if I've gotten more radical or more realistic" is a sentence about what happens to people who have been living inside catastrophe for too long. It applies, with only slight modification, to every faction in Arc IV. Let in land slowly.
- The party should leave this scene feeling that Reth is dangerous, sincere, and not entirely wrong — which is the correct feeling.
