# Cathedral Road Ambush

- Arc: Awakening
- Recommended levels: 3
- Target TV: 12
- Difficulty: Standard
- Use when: The campaign needs a violent opener on the road to Avernus.

## Location

- Site: Cathedral road outside Avernus
- Terrain: Broken wagons, rain-slick mud, panicked refugees, and a shattered milestone that grants Advantage on one Will save to the first creature that reaches it
- Pressure: The attackers are trying to drag the reliquary, courier, or marked PC off the road and into a hidden access path toward Avernus

## Encounter Objective

- Keep the courier, reliquary, or chosen witness from being dragged into the ditch-path leading toward the cathedral approach tunnels.
- Make the party choose between protecting people, cargo, and information.

## Serit Participation

- If `serit_trust: earned`, Serit gets one warning beat before initiative: she spots the wrong shadow, shoves one PC or the cargo clear, and cancels one cult ambush advantage or first strike.
- If `serit_trust: guarded`, Serit fights competently but does not volunteer information. She protects the cargo lane, not the whole party.
- If `serit_trust: low`, Serit gives no warning, protects only her satchel, and assumes the party will solve the wider road crisis on their own.
- If Choice D from Vignette 01 happened, let Serit body-block one early shadow move or forced drag effect. The point is that honesty bought immediate operational value.

## Watcher Add

- If the party camped at the milestone shrines, add one cult watcher in the fiction even if you do not add another stat block.
- The watcher marks the marked PC, reliquary, or courier and flees once the ambush turns against the cult. Do not run them as a front-line combatant.
- If the watcher escapes, increase later Sarafan visibility and treat the party as described rather than rumored when public fallout begins.

## Choice Pressure By Opening State

- Press on to Avernus: the party start tired, socially uncovered, and already committed to motion. One failed rescue usually means losing either rest, position, or the courier at once.
- Camp at the shrines: the party are better rested but more witnessed. Use frightened pilgrims, shouted names, and recognizability as part of the danger.
- Inspect the reliquary: cult attackers prioritize the sensitive PC or weakest observer rather than the obvious strongest target.
- Question Serit directly: the party understand the cargo is active, so the scene should feel less like random road violence and more like an interception.

## Running Notes

- The Acolytes use Blood-Prayer pressure and refuse to break once fighting starts.
- The Raider attacks harnesses, blocks retreat lanes, and turns the storm and refugees into chaos rather than standing in a fair fight.
- If you want less combat, run the same scene as an attempted drugging, spiritual marking, or snatch-and-run before steel comes out.
- If the party save the people but lose the cargo lane, that should still count as a meaningful win with ugly cost. Do not collapse every outcome into total success or total failure.
