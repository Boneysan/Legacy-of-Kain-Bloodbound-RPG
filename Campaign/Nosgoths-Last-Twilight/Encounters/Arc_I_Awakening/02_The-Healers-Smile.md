# The Healer's Smile

- Arc: Awakening
- Recommended levels: 4-5
- Target TV: 15
- Difficulty: Standard
- Use when: Investigation inside Avernus has to break into open horror.

## Location

- Site: Hospice chapel or relief ward inside the Avernus precinct
- Terrain: Narrow aisles, hanging censers, curtained cots, reliquary rooms, and blood channels hidden under the floorboards
- Pressure: The party must expose the wrongness before it escapes into the surrounding refugee population

## Encounter Objective

- Expose the Vessel before it escapes into the refugee camp or completes a possession rite.
- Keep the scene focused on proof, panic, and compromised care rather than a simple room-clear.

## Vignette Entry Conditions

### Choice A — Accept Sanctuary Fully

- Opening position: the party are indoors, partially disarmed by apparent safety, and the cult controls bed assignments, exits, and timing.
- Cult adjustment: the Vessel starts from initiative advantage in the fiction, with an acolyte already positioned near the reliquary room or infirmary stores.
- Civilian disposition: the refugees read the hospice as safety and initially resist the party causing alarm.

### Choice B — Accept but Post Guards

- Opening position: one party member or ally is isolated on first watch, outside the main emotional warmth of the room.
- Cult adjustment: the Vessel probes through social pressure, false requests for help, and one split-second attempt to isolate the watcher before revealing itself.
- Civilian disposition: fewer civilians are fully fooled, but no one wants open panic unless the party produces proof.

### Choice C — Challenge Halden Publicly

- Opening position: the scene begins half a step from riot. Furniture is moved, people are already moving for exits, and every accusation has witnesses.
- Cult adjustment: the Vessel abandons patience and triggers fear, fleeing civilians, and collateral danger early.
- Civilian disposition: the refugees are frightened witnesses, not neutral scenery. If the party handle this badly, prepare `Purge-at-First-Light` immediately after.

### Choice D — Split the Party

- Opening position: one group has clue advantage while the other is inside the trap. Start with separated information and force the reunion under time pressure.
- Cult adjustment: the Vessel tries to complete its rite before the outside team can rejoin and compare notes.
- Civilian disposition: the room is confused rather than hostile; the first believable voice can still define what this place is.

## Running Notes

- Let the reveal happen after clues, not as a random monster drop.
- The Vessel opens with social misdirection if possible, then shifts into Voice of the Pit and sacrificial survival tactics once cornered.
- Use crypt stairs or side corridors if the party identifies the suspect early and forces a chase instead of a chapel standoff.
- If the party win the fight but fail to establish proof, treat the scene as operational success and political failure. That should matter later.
