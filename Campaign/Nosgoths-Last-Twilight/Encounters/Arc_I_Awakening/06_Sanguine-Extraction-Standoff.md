# Sanguine Extraction Standoff

- Arc: Awakening
- Recommended levels: 5-6
- Target TV: 24 baseline before political fallout
- Difficulty: Challenging
- Use when: The fight over the Sanguine Knot becomes a three-sided custody crisis rather than a simple cult cleanup.

## Location

- Site: Blood-Well chamber exits, adjoining tunnels, and the first viable extraction route back toward the surface
- Terrain: Tight exits, unstable ritual residue, poor sight lines, and prisoners or bystanders who make clean violence politically expensive
- Pressure: The real question is who leaves with the fragment and who gets believed afterward, not who drops the last enemy first

## Encounter Objective

- Decide who controls the Sanguine Knot, the chamber exits, and the public story of what happened below Avernus before the scene collapses into pure bloodshed.

## Framing By Custody State

### Choice A — Party Keeps the Knot

- Objective: hold the bearer and one extraction lane long enough to break contact.
- Hostility map: the cult want contamination and seizure; the Inquisitor treats the party as unlawful possessors first and cult opponents second.
- Success condition: the party leave with the Knot and at least one credible witness or document trail intact.
- Failure condition: the Knot is separated from the bearer, publicly claimed by another faction, or the bearer is isolated under Sarafan custody.

### Choice B — Entrust It to Serit and the Accord

- Objective: protect Serit's exit route and prevent the Accord custody transfer from collapsing into seizure theater.
- Hostility map: the cult want the fragment; the Inquisitor wants to turn Accord custody into grounds for immediate legal intervention.
- Success condition: Serit or the Accord team clear the chamber route with the Knot and the party retain consultation rights in the fiction.
- Failure condition: Serit is cut off, discredited, or forced to abandon the fragment under pressure.

### Choice C — Conceal It or Use a Neutral Holder

- Objective: get the Knot out of sight and leave no certain answer about who now holds it.
- Hostility map: the cult hunt for signs of the hiding place; the Inquisitor acts as a pressure source and search authority more than a simple combatant.
- Success condition: the party exit without the true holder being identified.
- Partial/skip condition: if the concealment plan was exceptionally strong before the scene starts, downgrade this from full battle to tense search, bluff, and one sharp betrayal beat.
- Failure condition: anyone visibly tracks the hiding route, forces the neutral holder into the open, or turns the concealment into public evidence of guilt.

### Choice D — Force a Faction Vote

- Objective: survive a public claim crisis while keeping the chamber from becoming a massacre that decides custody by panic.
- Hostility map: every faction speaks first and reaches for violence second. The Inquisitor is a co-claimant unless directly attacked.
- Success condition: the party leave with a declared custody outcome and enough surviving witnesses that the result cannot be rewritten cleanly.
- Failure condition: the vote collapses into unframed bloodshed and the loudest survivor defines the story.

## Running Notes

- Do not run the Inquisitor as an automatic ally against the cult; they are there to seize narrative and lawful custody.
- The Vessel wants panic and contamination, not honorable battle.
- If the party talks their way out of a three-sided fight, pivot immediately into a ritual standoff or sudden betrayal rather than letting the scene end cleanly.
- Preserve at least one witness whose survival matters more than one more casualty.
- The point of this scene is not body count. It is whether the party leave owning the fragment, the route, and the story all at once. They should rarely get all three cleanly.
