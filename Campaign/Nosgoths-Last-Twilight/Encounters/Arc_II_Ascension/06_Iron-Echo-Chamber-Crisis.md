# Iron Echo Chamber Crisis

- Arc: Ascension
- Recommended levels: 7-9
- Target TV: 26 baseline before claimant or witness complications
- Difficulty: Standard
- Use when: The Iron Echo begins answering and the party must decide whether legitimacy is granted, exposed, refused, or weaponized.

## Location

- Site: The Iron Echo chamber, command dais, and relic access hall beneath Dumah's Fortress
- Terrain: Oathstones, awakened ward lines, visible spectral banners, and a ruling dais that reacts to blood, challenge, and lineage
- Pressure: The fragment is forcing a ruling under unstable political conditions; a clean tactical win that leaves legitimacy unresolved should count as incomplete

## Encounter Objective

- Reach a binding answer about who the Echo recognizes before the chamber is seized, desecrated, or turned into proof that no claimant can actually command what Dumah built.

## Chamber Approach Framing

### Choice A — Claimant's Representative

- Objective shift: the party are trying to secure a ruling for someone else without becoming the visible author of it.
- Political danger: rival retainers and excluded claimants react immediately once the chamber answers.
- Add-on guidance: if you add one political NPC to the scene, make them a claimant champion or witness whose survival determines whether the ruling holds outside the room.

### Choice B — Party's Own Name

- Objective shift: the chamber becomes a legitimacy referendum on the party themselves.
- Political danger: everyone now wants to court, threaten, or delegitimize the party directly.
- Add-on guidance: if you add one political NPC, make them an envoy or confessor who can turn chamber outcome into immediate northbound baggage.

### Choice C — Refuse Contact And Document

- Objective shift: this is a refusal scene, not a coronation. The party are trying to leave with evidence and without granting the chamber a public ruler.
- Political danger: soft exclusion, sabotage, and angry claimants replace clean battlefield logic.
- Add-on guidance: combat may be replaced entirely by departure pressure, denial of route, or a forced escort scene if the room does not tip violent.

### Choice D — Expose The Oathbreaker

- Objective shift: break one person's standing and survive the backlash from honest memory becoming public fact.
- Political danger: the chamber itself becomes proof against selective versions of rule, so every invested faction has reason to suppress or seize the result.
- Add-on guidance: if you add one political NPC, make them the collapsing figure or their most dangerous defender.

## Running Notes

- The undead garrison should feel like command pressure made visible, not random tomb monsters.
- Add claimant champions, rival retainers, or a saboteur only if the scene needs a second political blade, not extra bulk.
- Let the chamber answer blood, oath, and challenge in real time so the terrain itself judges the party's method.
- If combat is avoided, keep the same objective and resolve it through trial, public humiliation, or forced recognition under relic pressure.
- The correct outcome is not always "someone wins the chamber." Sometimes the strongest result is a ruling that destabilizes everyone.
