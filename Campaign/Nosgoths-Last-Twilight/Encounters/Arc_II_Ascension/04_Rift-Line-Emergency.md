# Rift-Line Emergency

- Arc: Ascension
- Recommended levels: 8-10
- Target TV: 29 baseline, 36 for the heavier version
- Difficulty: Standard
- Use when: The Rift Ember must be defended, redirected, or shut down under concentrated Hylden pressure.

## Location

- Site: Broken pylon ring around a live breach node
- Terrain: Unstable ice shelves, central breach core, and a pylon network that can be stabilized with DR 4 Glyphcasting or Focus checks, sabotaged with Corruption, or shattered for immediate backlash
- Pressure: The node is as important as the enemy; the party should feel out of time from the moment initiative starts

## Encounter Objective

- Destroy the Rift-Priest before a third full round of channeling, or complete 3 successful stabilization actions at the breach node while surviving the Trooper screen.

## Hylden Engagement Toggle

- If `hylden_engagement: none`, the party do not have a redirect option. Stabilization DR increases by 1 because the Accord is solving seal geometry without enemy-side data.
- If `hylden_engagement: negotiated`, unlock `redirect the node` as an action: DR 4 Glyphcasting, Focus, or equivalent technical check at a pylon to convert it into a controlled passage anchor.
- If `hylden_engagement: negotiated` because the party asked what the Glass Voice needed, describe breach behavior in door-not-wall terms from the start. The players should understand they are editing traffic, not merely plugging a hole.
- If the party accused the Glass Voice and kept the channel open, Hylden actors target the negotiator or visible technician with precise disruption rather than random aggression.

## State-Conditional Objectives

### Refuse Negotiation

- Objective: survive long enough to stabilize or shatter the pylon ring without access to redirect geometry.
- Cost: higher DRs, harsher Corruption risk, clearer moral boundary.

### Engage The Logic

- Objective: choose in play whether to stabilize or redirect, knowing the second option now exists and will stain the party politically.
- Cost: any visible use of redirect logic becomes live evidence later.

### Ask What It Needs

- Objective: hold the site while building a technically coherent controlled passage under impossible pressure.
- Cost: the scene should feel smarter and more dangerous, not easier.

### Accuse It

- Objective: solve the crisis while facing more exact counterplay from Hylden forces that now respect the party as serious adversaries.
- Cost: the party are read, studied, and specifically opposed rather than merely attacked.

## Running Notes

- The Rift-Priest should open with Corruption Pulse and use Rift Tear to scatter the line.
- The Shock Troopers punish anyone trying to reach the backline or camp on the node.
- Add the War-Stalker for the level 9-10 version or when you want the front to feel more like a field assault than a ritual defense.
- If Ash-Glass Procession converts are nearby, treat visible failure as Fracture propaganda and log it in the tracker.
- If the party earn the redirect option, make it mechanically and narratively concrete. It should never feel like the same encounter with different flavor text.
