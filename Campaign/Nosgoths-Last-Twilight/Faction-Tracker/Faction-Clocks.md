# Faction Clocks

Use these clocks to escalate consequences visibly. Advance a clock when the party helps a faction, ignores a threat, loses control of a fragment, or triggers public fallout.

## Sarafan Purge Clock

- 1: Patrols and denunciations increase.
- 2: Known allies are questioned or detained.
- 3: A sanctuary is marked for cleansing.
- 4: Formal purge action begins in a settlement tied to the party.

## Sarafan Schism Clock

- 1: Legalists and hardliners begin contradicting one another in the field.
- 2: Competing warrants, doctrines, or command claims create operational confusion.
- 3: One Sarafan wing uses the party to discredit the other publicly.
- 4: The order fractures into open conflict over custody, doctrine, or extermination policy.

## Clan War Clock

- 1: Duels and assassinations escalate.
- 2: Fortress supply lines are contested.
- 3: A Truce of Nights is threatened or broken.
- 4: Open clan war reshapes regional travel and shelter.

## Hylden Breach Clock

- 1: Glyph-static and possession rumors spread.
- 2: Devices, pylons, or agents appear in force.
- 3: A real breach opens and stays open long enough to matter.
- 4: The Gate crisis becomes a campaign-defining military front.

## Wheel Compliance Clock

- 1: Funerary rites and despair rhetoric intensify.
- 2: A trusted counselor or priest is revealed as compromised.
- 3: A public call for surrender to inevitability fractures morale.
- 4: The Cult of the Wheel gains enough fear to direct civic behavior openly.

## Hash'ak'gik Corruption Clock

- 1: More charity houses, shrines, or hospices feel wrong.
- 2: One notable civic figure is revealed as compromised.
- 3: Blood-rites begin altering reality locally.
- 4: Possession cascades or mass rite conditions emerge.

## Fracture Merge Clock

- 1: Realm-merge rhetoric spreads among feral cells.
- 2: A breach is used as proof that the old order can be broken.
- 3: Predatory conversions and terror raids increase.
- 4: The Fracture attempts an irreversible merge event.

## Balance Reckoning Clock

- 1: A fragment solution fixes the immediate problem but leaves a visible metaphysical distortion behind.
- 2: Wardens, the Hall, or spectral witnesses begin treating the party as agents of dangerous imbalance rather than reluctant stewards.
- 3: A prior compromise returns as proof that order was preserved through corruption, coercion, or false judgment.
- 4: The campaign's endgame judgment hardens; the Hall of Equilibrium or its living representatives will no longer treat the party as untested claimants.

In Arc I especially, render this as withdrawal of grace rather than visible punishment: closed shrines, withheld rites, and routes that suddenly demand named cost.

## Arc-End Update Cheat Sheet

Use this after each arc-ending session before you close prep for the night. Advance only what the fiction clearly earned; if two clocks could move, prefer the one the table will feel first next session.

### After Arc I: Awakening

- If the Sanguine Knot was touched publicly, or if the party used it to expose or coerce when mercy was still possible, advance Sarafan Purge by 1 and Balance Reckoning by 1.
- If the chamber or refuge network was profaned, advance Hash'ak'gik Corruption by 1.
- If the party exposed cult infiltration without panicking the region, advance Sarafan Schism by 1 instead of Purge if legalists now want custody and proof.
- If the fragment was buried, hidden, or handled discreetly, leave Purge unchanged but worsen Cult of the Wheel posture in the standing table.
- When Balance Reckoning advances in Arc I, show it immediately through withdrawal of grace: Nerys refuses an easy rite, a shrine begins weeping blood, or the safe road now demands confession, time, or surrendered leverage.

### After Arc II: Ascension

- If Dumah's Fortress was settled through bloodshed, humiliation, or unstable oaths, advance Clan War by 1.
- If the Rift Ember was used to hold or redirect the breach, advance Balance Reckoning by 1 and Hylden Breach by 1 unless the stabilization felt exceptionally clean.
- If the Ash-Glass Procession gained converts, survivors, or a convincing proof-text, advance Fracture Merge by 1.
- If Sarafan legalists and hardliners reacted differently to the fortress or breach result, advance Sarafan Schism by 1.

### After Arc III: Revelation

- If Wheel doctrine gained frightened civilians, grieving survivors, or institutional cover, advance Wheel Compliance by 1.
- If the Chronal Shard proved boundary collapse, prophecy fraud, or contradictory survival in ways the Fracture can preach, advance Fracture Merge by 1.
- If the party used the Shard to force action instead of understanding, advance Balance Reckoning by 1.
- If the party leave with a clearer anti-Wheel agenda and the means to act on it, note that on the endgame matrix rather than advancing a clock automatically.

### After Arc IV: Convergence

- Stop treating the clocks only as escalation and use them as aftermath labels.
- Hylden Breach 3-4 means the north remains an active wound in the epilogue, even after victory.
- Wheel Compliance 3-4 means institutions, mourners, and civic authorities still prefer managed surrender over freedom.
- Fracture Merge 3-4 means survivor cults, breach pilgrims, and predatory converts persist in the next age.
- Balance Reckoning 3-4 means the Hall's verdict on the party should appear in the ending, whether or not the world benefits.
- When Arc IV ends, carry the relevant pressure into 03_Endgame-Adjudication-Matrix.md instead of advancing clocks past 4.
