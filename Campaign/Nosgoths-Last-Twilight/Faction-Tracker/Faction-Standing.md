# Faction Standing Tracker

Update this after every major session or whenever the party changes control of a fragment, public alliance, or major battlefield outcome.

## Status Key

- Allied: Will spend real assets and take risks for the party.
- Cooperative: Will bargain honestly enough to do business.
- Wary: Still talking, already preparing a knife.
- Hostile: Active obstruction, denunciation, or local violence.
- War: Kill, capture, or ruin on sight where feasible.

## Fast Standing Adjustment

Use these shortcuts after each arc before rewriting the full table language.

- Move a faction one step toward Allied if the party protected its people, validated its worldview in public, or handed it fragment leverage without immediate humiliation.
- Move a faction one step toward Hostile if the party exposed its lies, denied it a fragment, or made it look weak in front of rivals.
- Move a faction straight to War only when the party killed a top representative, destroyed a defining asset, or publicly chose a mutually exclusive end-state.
- If one wing of a split faction benefits and the other loses, leave the overall standing where it is and update the fault-line text instead.
- If the party solved a crisis in a way common people would call survivable but elites would call intolerable, improve Outlands Guilds or Pale Accord first and worsen ideological factions second.
- If the party solved a crisis through terror, coercion, or catastrophic force, worsen Balance Pressure even when allies approve.

## Internal Fault Lines

Track these separately from simple standing. They determine which version of a faction the party are actually dealing with.

### Sarafan Split

- Legalist wing: represented by examiners, confessors, and seizure doctrine. Dangerous, but still cares about proof, custody, and chain of authority.
- Hollow hardliners: represented by purge commanders and annihilation logic. Treat instability as proof and public terror as policy.
- GM use: whenever the party deal with the Sarafan, decide which wing is speaking and whether the other wing gains from the outcome.

### Balance Pressure

- If the party solve crises by force alone, the Warden view of them worsens even if allies approve.
- If the party preserve order through obviously corrupt means, the Hall's later judgment should harden.
- GM use: log any fragment decision that stabilized a region short-term while distorting the wider order.

### Fracture Recruitment

- The Fracture grows when survivors conclude the old order is a lie and instability is more truthful than restoration.
- The Ash-Glass Procession should be the visible mechanism of that growth: scarred pilgrims, recovered survivors, and guided deserters moving from one wound-site to the next turning collapse into testimony.
- GM use: each catastrophic but revealing scene is a chance for a new Fracture convert, slogan, or local cell.
