# Arc I: Awakening Choice Consequence Guide

Use this document beside 04_Choice-Guide.md when you want to turn a player decision into the next encounter, pressure shift, or recurring complication.

## Defend people, cargo, or information

### Encounter Uses

- If the party defend people, frame the next scene as escort, triage, or rear-guard fighting against cult skirmishers and panic.
- If they defend cargo or information, frame the follow-up as pursuit, interception, or recovery before stolen goods or proof vanish into Avernus.

### Immediate Narrative Changes

- Saving people makes survivors speak for the party immediately, while lost cargo or clues forces Session 2 to open from shortage, doubt, or delayed leverage.
- Saving cargo or information preserves mission momentum, but nearby witnesses treat the party as cold, selective, or compromised.

### Faction Pressure

- Outlands Guilds respond best to preserved cargo and route discipline, while local civilians and practical allies respond best to visible rescue.
- Sarafan legalists lean toward custody if evidence survives, while the cult benefits if proof is lost and only fear remains.

### Later Payoffs

- Rescued witnesses can return as credible voices against cult lies or as people the Sarafan try to seize for testimony.
- Lost cargo or missing proof can reappear later in enemy hands, proving that the ambush was larger and more organized than first assumed.

## Choose speed, secrecy, or safety

### Encounter Uses

- A fast descent supports ambushes in tight corridors, running fights, and a chamber entered before the party can fully understand the field.
- A careful advance or detour supports rescue scenes, stealth routes, or a final chamber already altered by cult preparation.

### Immediate Narrative Changes

- Speed gets the party to the Blood-Well before the rite fully settles, but leaves captives, cargo, or witnesses behind them.
- Caution or detour preserves lives and evidence, but the cult has time to reposition vessels, fortify approaches, or move key actors.

### Faction Pressure

- The Pale Accord values recovered witnesses and intact evidence, even if the party lose surprise.
- Hash'ak'gik gains pressure whenever delay lets its false refuge network stay active for one more scene.

### Later Payoffs

- A rushed descent leaves unfinished danger on the upper levels, letting compromised NPCs or fleeing cultists reappear later in the arc.
- Rescued captives or recovered goods become later proof that the party chose mercy under pressure, which matters when chamber truth is contested.

## Press for truth now or limit contact

### Encounter Uses

- Pushing deeper truth supports spectral bleed, involuntary visions, and chamber scenes where revelation itself becomes the hazard.
- Limiting contact supports extraction, containment, or short defensive encounters while rivals try to define the fragment before the party does.

### Immediate Narrative Changes

- Deep contact can reveal blood-oaths, ancestry, or betrayal at once, forcing an immediate reckoning with whoever is present.
- Limited contact preserves strength and reduces immediate fallout, but leaves the party speaking in uncertainty while others speak with confidence.

### Faction Pressure

- Sarafan legalists and the Pale Accord press hard if the fragment yields names, lineage, or visible proof.
- The cult and the Wheel benefit if the party hesitate, because silence gives room for curated interpretation and surrender rhetoric.

### Later Payoffs

- Truth pulled too early can keep resurfacing through spectral echoes, hostile testimony, or personal shame that refuses to stay buried.
- Questions left unanswered become leverage for rivals who later claim the party saw more than they admitted.

## Take, seal, conceal, or temporarily leave the fragment

### Encounter Uses

- Taking or concealing the Knot supports escape, pursuit, and betrayal-sensitive travel encounters because custody becomes the immediate battlefield.
- Sealing or leaving it supports site-control fights, contested return attempts, or rival expeditions trying to claim the chamber after the party withdraw.

### Immediate Narrative Changes

- Taking it makes the party the obvious center of every surviving claimant's attention before they even clear Avernus.
- Sealing, hiding, or leaving it shifts the next scene toward argument over access, with no one trusting that the matter is settled.

### Faction Pressure

- Taking the fragment sharpens hostility from the Sarafan, the cult, and any ally who expected shared oversight.
- Leaving or sealing it gives the Accord, the cult, or local authorities room to argue they, not the party, now control what happens next.

### Later Payoffs

- A concealed Knot can start answering blood before anyone admits where it is, exposing secrecy through visions, sickness, or oath-memory.
- A sealed or abandoned chamber becomes a later target for cult reinfection, Sarafan seizure, or an Accord salvage mission.

## Decide who controls relic access and chamber truth

### Encounter Uses

- Keeping control inside the party supports perimeter defense, anti-seizure scenes, and pressure encounters built around exhaustion and mistrust.
- Sharing access or ceding narrative control supports hearings, joint rites, or negotiations that can be broken by sabotage, panic, or a planted witness.

### Immediate Narrative Changes

- Internal control lets the party leave with independence, but no neutral voice softens how dangerous they now appear.
- Shared or faction-led control creates an immediate public script for what happened below, whether or not that script is honest.

### Faction Pressure

- Shared access strengthens whichever ally receives it and makes every excluded faction more aggressive.
- Letting a faction define the story hardens rival hostility fast, because chamber truth in Nosgoth is never politically neutral.

### Later Payoffs

- An ally granted access can later call in debt, demand continued oversight, or produce records the party cannot fully control.
- A false or partial chamber narrative becomes harder to dislodge later, even when better evidence emerges.

## Decide who travels with the party

### Encounter Uses

- Bringing survivors, scholars, or compromised witnesses supports escort, protection, and assassination-prevention encounters on the road out of Avernus.
- Leaving people behind supports later rescue, extraction, or retaliation scenes when abandoned witnesses are seized, silenced, or turned.

### Immediate Narrative Changes

- Traveling witnesses make the party slower and more politically visible, but they also keep the party from being the sole voice about the Blood-Well.
- Leaving people behind lightens movement, but it immediately raises questions of abandonment, trust, and who now controls those survivors.

### Faction Pressure

- Sarafan legalists want survivors under doctrine and custody, while the cult wants them lost, corrupted, or publicly discredited.
- Outlands brokers value portable experts, but hate taking on noncombatant burdens without payment or leverage.

### Later Payoffs

- A survivor brought along can later validate the party, break under pressure, or reveal more than intended in front of the wrong audience.
- A witness left behind can return later as a captive, a collaborator, or a ruined soul used to indict the party's priorities.

## Choose what version of the truth enters the world

### Encounter Uses

- Broad truth supports riot control, purge patrols, emergency flight, and scenes where panic is as dangerous as any enemy.
- Selective truth or secrecy supports courier interception, leak hunts, blackmail, and quieter encounters where information itself is the contested object.

### Immediate Narrative Changes

- Full disclosure makes Arc II open under scandal, fear, and immediate moral clarity about Avernus.
- Managed or buried truth opens with rumor, accusation, and multiple factions claiming to know what the party hid.

### Faction Pressure

- Broad truth usually advances Sarafan Purge or Schism pressure because legalists want evidence while hardliners want fire.
- Controlled truth improves short-term leverage with the Accord or Guilds, but worsens trust with anyone already suspicious of manipulation.

### Later Payoffs

- Public truth makes the party harder to quietly erase, but also easier to denounce as corrupters who brought forbidden knowledge into the open.
- Buried truth returns later as a leak, a forged testimony, or an enemy narrative the party can no longer fully correct.
