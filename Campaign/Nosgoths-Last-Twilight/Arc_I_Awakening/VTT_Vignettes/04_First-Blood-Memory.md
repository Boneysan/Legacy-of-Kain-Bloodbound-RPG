# 04 — The First Blood-Memory

The relic does not announce itself. It identifies. One character feels something on the road or near the cargo that is too specific to be coincidence — a name spoken in a dead voice, a warmth in the wrong place, a vision pulling at something inherited rather than learned. This vignette is a solo aside. Run it privately if possible. What the affected PC decides to do with the experience is the choice.

---

## Scene Info

- **Arc / Session:** Arc I — Session 1 (during travel) or Session 2 (near the Blood-Well approaches)
- **Trigger:** A PC with blood magic, spectral sensitivity, inherited lineage ties, or a broken oath is in close proximity to the Sanguine Knot when the fragment stirs. The trigger should happen naturally during travel or rest, not as a sudden interruption.
- **Scene Type:** `revelation`
- **Est. Duration:** 2–4 min (solo or small group)
- **Tags This Scene Can Change:** `serit_trust`, `reliquary_status`

---

## Cast

| NPC | Role in This Scene |
|-----|--------------------|
| A dead voice, a name, a pressure — not an NPC | The relic does not speak as a person. It answers with what is already inside the PC. |
| Serit Vale | Potentially present. If she witnesses this, it changes her from Session 2 forward. |

---

## Opening Narration

> There is no warning. The road comes to a stretch of low ground between two dead stands of trees, and something in the cargo shifts — not audibly, but in the manner of attention, as if something behind the wood and latch turned its regard toward you specifically. Then: a name. Not spoken aloud. Not spoken in your voice. Spoken in the voice of someone who died knowing your face.

---

## GM Opening Prompt

*[Speak this directly to the affected player.]*

> The name is yours. Not the name you use now — something older, or something owed, or the name that appears in a lineage record you have either seen or been told to forget. It is accompanied by a sensation between warmth and iron at the back of the throat. For two seconds, you see the road doubled — the physical road now, and a road layered underneath it that smells of deep water and old blood. Someone is standing on the lower road, and they are watching you.

> *What do you do in the next ten seconds?*

---

## Player Choices

### A — Resist It and Say Nothing

> Push it down. This is not the time or the place, and you don't know these people well enough.

**Outcome:** The vision closes. The doubled road fades. No one saw. But the suppressed recognition sits behind the character's eyes for the rest of the session and the fragment has now registered the refusal. At the next close proximity event, the experience will be harder to contain. No immediate social consequence — but the PC carries the experience forward alone.

**Tags:** `reliquary_status: sealed`

---

### B — Report It Immediately to Serit Vale

> Find her and tell her what just happened. She wanted to know something earlier. Let her.

**NPC Response (Serit):**

> She sets her satchel down carefully and looks at the PC for a long moment. Then: *"Your bloodline, or what you owe someone? Both are possible. The Sanguine Knot doesn't invent — it locates."* She pulls out a journal. *"Tell me the name it used. I need to know whether it matches the site's prior record."* She is performing precision. She does not quite manage it.

**Outcome:** Serit knows. She now begins treating the PC as a likely bloodline carrier and adjusting her Accord reports accordingly. This accelerates her cooperation in Sessions 2–3 but also begins a chain of Accord documentation that labels the PC as a point of interest. `serit_trust` benefits directly.

**Tags:** `serit_trust: earned`, `reliquary_status: examined`

---

### C — Follow the Vision's Pull

> Something on the lower road is watching. Turn toward it.

**Outcome:** The PC who chases the doubled-road experience gets closer to the spectral layer than anyone else has at this point in the campaign. They see the figure more clearly — not its face, but the chain it carries, which is recognizable (an ancestral binding, a broken vow symbol, a House mark, or an oath sigil — the GM should tie this to the PC's background). The vision ends abruptly when another party member touches them or speaks directly to them. The PC knows they can return to that layer — and that the figure knew they would come.

**Tags:** `reliquary_status: examined`

> **GM:** This outcome plants the most seeds for later direct relic contact in Session 4. The chain seen here should reappear in the Blood-Well chamber.

---

### D — Share the Experience With Another PC

> Not Serit. Another player character. The one the affected PC trusts most, or trusts least, or has the most reason to worry about.

**Outcome:** The two PCs now share the most accurate read on what the Sanguine Knot is actually doing — it isn't random, it isn't environmental, it is specifically ancestral and social. What they do with that shared knowledge is the rest of Arc I. The fragment has registered the disclosure and the next pulse will occur to *both* of them simultaneously, which will be harder to hide.

**Tags:** `reliquary_status: examined`

> **GM:** In Session 2, the joint pulse should fire during the NPC investigation — at the worst possible moment for discretion.

---

## If No Clear Choice Is Made

> If the affected player freezes, passes, or the moment gets disrupted: the vision closes on its own. The relic noted the contact and will try again under worse circumstances. Default to Choice A's outcome. The following Session 1 ambush should begin with the fragment's warmth spiking noticeably during the fight — the next pulse arrives bound to violence.

**Default Tag:** `reliquary_status: sealed`

## GM Notes

- Run this during travel or quiet prep time, not during a combat scene. It should feel private and slightly out-of-time.
- The doubled road and the figure are not the relic itself — they are the PC's inherited memory meeting the Knot's resonance field. Do not make it feel like possession or threat. It should feel like recognition from the other side.
- The name the relic uses should be the GM's anchor for that PC's blood-memory arc. Establish what name you'll use before play and note it privately.
- If Serit is visible during Choice C's vision-chase, she notices the PC's thousand-yard focus. She does not interrupt. She writes a note.
- This vignette can fire more than once across Sessions 1–3 for different PCs. Each instance should use a different name, a different ancestral symbol, and a different emotional register.
