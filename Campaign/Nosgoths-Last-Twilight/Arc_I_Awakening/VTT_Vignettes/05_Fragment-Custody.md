# 05 — Who Carries the Knot

The fragment has been secured — or it nearly has been. Before Session 5 closes and before the arc resolves its central custody question through the formal Choice Guide entry, this vignette stages the conversation about *who should hold it going forward.* This is the political moment. Every faction represented in the room has a position, some spoken and some not.

## Opening Narration

> The chamber has stopped answering. Whatever it was doing before — answering names, bleeding against gravity, holding the dead in frame — it has gone quiet, and the Sanguine Knot is sitting in a wrapped bundle or sealed container on the nearest flat surface, and the room has arranged itself around the fact that someone has to decide what comes next. Serit Vale has been the most patient she has been since the night the caravan reached Avernus. That patience is a kind of argument.

---

## NPC Opening Line

> **Serit Vale**, sitting across from the fragment rather than next to it: *"The Accord can provide secure transport, documentation conditions, and research framing that keeps this out of Sarafan inventory lists and Guild manifests. I wish to make that offer formally before anyone else makes a worse one. I am telling you what I want and what I will give in return. I will not pretend otherwise."*

> **Mera Ash**, without looking up: *"She's not wrong. She's also not the only person in this room with a more comfortable arrangement ready."*

---

## Player Choices

### A — The Party Keeps the Sanguine Knot

> We carry it. No one else's arrangement, no one else's framing. The party takes direct custody and moves with it into Arc II.

**NPC Response (Serit Vale):**

> *"Then I need a co-documentation agreement and assurance of research access below Avernus. If you are carrying it, I am walking next to it. I will not document from a distance what I understand better than anyone else in this room."* She is not arguing. She is re-positioning.

**NPC Response (Mera Ash):**

> *"You understand that ownership of that fragment is the kind of rumor that pulls attention. I'll note the decision for my employers and they will adjust their expectations accordingly. We'll still be useful to each other — just differently."*

**Outcome:** The party gains the fragment and inherits its full social and physical weight. Bearer Corruption mechanics activate from Session 6 onward. Serit remains an ally but is now a watcher as much as a collaborator. The Guilds pull slightly back from direct operational support; Mera Ash stays but the Guild position shifts from investment to monitoring.

**Tags:** `reliquary_status: sealed`, `guild_pressure: high`, `public_story: secrecy`

---

### B — Entrust It to Serit Vale and the Pale Accord

> The Accord has the infrastructure. They have the knowledge. Let them take it under documented terms.

**NPC Response (Serit Vale):**

> *"Good."* A pause. Then: *"I mean — thank you. The terms are these: you retain right of consultation. Any finding that directly concerns a bloodline in your group comes to you first. The Accord does not weaponize what the Knot tells us about you."* She looks at each person in the room before she finishes. *"I will hold to that personally. The institution will hold to it as long as it is inconvenient rather than advantageous to break. That is the honest version of the agreement."*

**Outcome:** The Accord takes custody. The party retains access and is listed as field associates rather than possessors. Corruption weight lifts from individual bearers. In Arc II, the Accord's documentation labs become available, but so does Accord political vulnerability — what they hold, others will target.

**Tags:** `reliquary_status: sealed`, `serit_trust: earned`, `guild_pressure: low`

---

### C — Give It to a Neutral Third Party or Conceal It

> The Accord wants it. The Guilds want it moved. The Sarafan will come for it. Bury it again, or find someone uninvested enough to hold it safely.

**NPC Response (Serit Vale):**

> Serit closes her notes. *"There is no neutral third party in Nosgoth for an object of this kind. What you are describing is not neutrality — it is concealment with extra steps. I will record that the fragment was secured and its location was not disclosed to me. That is the most accurate statement I can produce."* She stands up. *"When it calls someone in whatever hole you leave it in, please notify us before the second pulse."*

**NPC Response (Mera Ash):**

> *"Conceal it. Smart. I'll route us accordingly."*

**Outcome:** The fragment is hidden. No faction gets a win. In the short term, pressure drops on all fronts. In Session 6, however, the cover is already developing fractures — the hiding place will have been seen, guessed at, or compromised by at least one watcher. The party enters Arc II without the weight of the Knot, but the Accord is now operating independently and the Guilds are reading the party as unpredictable.

**Tags:** `reliquary_status: sealed`, `serit_trust: guarded`, `public_story: controlled_lie`, `sarafan_attention: 0`

---

### D — Force a Faction Vote Among All Present

> Let everyone speak. Make it a decision made in the open rather than decided by the party alone.

**Outcome:** Serit presents the Accord position. Mera Ash presents the Guild position. If any cult-linked NPC is present, they observe rather than participate but their silence is a kind of vote. The exercise exposes irreconcilable interests faster than any interrogation has. By the end of the round, the party has more information but less tolerance for pretending the factions care about the same outcome. The fragment's custody decision defaults to whoever in the room makes the sharpest case, which is almost always Serit.

This choice only works if Serit has `serit_trust: earned` or `guarded`; if it is `low`, she refuses to participate in a public negotiation and the vote collapses.

**Tags:** `serit_trust: guarded` (or unchanged if already `earned`), `reliquary_status: examined`, `public_story: open_truth`

---

## If No Clear Choice Is Made

> Mera Ash makes a unilateral decision: she physically moves the fragment into a sealed transport container and says "we move it and decide in motion. Deciding here is the only worse option." Default to Choice A's outcome with the party controlling the cargo but without a formal agreement with the Accord.

**Default Tags:** `reliquary_status: sealed`, `guild_pressure: high`

## GM Notes

- This is the most politically loaded scene in Arc I. Save it until the party has reason to trust at least one NPC and reason to distrust at least one other.
- Serit's admission that the Accord "will hold to it as long as it is inconvenient to break" (Choice B) is the most honest the Accord will ever sound. Deliver that line with weight.
- Do not rush to a tidy resolution. The fractured outcome (Choice D collapse, Choice C silent disagreement) is as valid as a clean handover. Arc II should open with at least one person in the party who thinks the wrong decision was made.
- If any PC is a confirmed blood-carrier from Vignette 04, their personal stakes in the custody decision should be named — even if not resolved. The Knot is not just a political object for them.
