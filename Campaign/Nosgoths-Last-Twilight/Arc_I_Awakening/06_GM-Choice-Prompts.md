# Arc I: Awakening GM Choice Prompts

Use this document when a choice has just been made and you want a fast answer to what to run next.

## Session 1: Roadside Priorities

### If the party defend people

- Run an escort, triage, or rear-guard scene before the road fully clears.
- Put a survivor in position to validate the party publicly in Session 2.
- Let the lost cargo, missing courier package, or vanished clue become the next problem.

### If the party defend cargo

- Run a pursuit or extraction scene where frightened witnesses and wounded travelers are left behind.
- Have Mera Ash or another practical ally approve of the decision while civilians recoil from it.
- Bring back one abandoned witness later as an accuser, captive, or compromised survivor.

### If the party defend information

- Run an interrogation, chase, or clue-recovery scene before the cult can erase the trail.
- Make the next session open with sharper suspicion and thinner public goodwill.
- Let preserved proof attract Sarafan scrutiny sooner than expected.

## Session 3: The Descent Below Avernus

### If the party choose speed

- Run a rushed corridor ambush or partial chamber entry before the cult fully stabilizes the site.
- Leave captives, evidence, or fleeing cultists behind them as unresolved pressure.
- Reuse that unfinished danger later as a reinforcements problem or hostile testimony.

### If the party choose secrecy and caution

- Run a stealth approach, locked-route puzzle, or chamber altered by cult preparation.
- Reward them with better evidence, but make the cult more ready when contact begins.
- Let the Accord value what was preserved even while the cult gains time.

### If the party detour for safety or rescue

- Run a rescue under time pressure with one life saved at the cost of surprise.
- Put a grateful witness or survivor in the next scene as moral proof of the party's priorities.
- Make the chamber harder because the cult used that lost time well.

## Session 4: First Contact At The Blood-Well

### If the party press for truth now

- Run a vision-heavy chamber crisis where revelation itself creates the hazard.
- Expose one blood-oath, lineage truth, or betrayal immediately in front of the wrong people.
- Let that truth keep resurfacing later through spectral echoes, shame, or political exploitation.

### If the party limit contact

- Run containment, extraction, or defensive play while rivals argue over what the fragment means.
- Make the party leave with fewer answers and weaker narrative control.
- Use that uncertainty later when enemies claim the party hid what they learned.

## Session 5: Fate Of The Sanguine Knot

### If the party take the fragment

- Run pursuit, contested extraction, or ambush on the route out of Avernus.
- Make every surviving faction treat the party as the immediate center of leverage.
- Let the Knot begin answering inconvenient blood truths before the party can travel far.

### If the party seal the fragment

- Run a site-control, withdrawal, or argument-over-custody scene instead of a clean exit.
- Put the Accord, Sarafan, or surviving cultists in position to claim they now control what happens next.
- Return to the chamber later as a salvage site, purge site, or reinfected shrine.

### If the party conceal the fragment

- Run a quiet exit under mistrust, with one ally already wondering what was hidden.
- Seed visions, sickness, or oath-memory leaks that slowly betray the secret.
- Let later conflict hinge on who guessed the truth first.

### If the party temporarily leave the fragment

- Run a retreat with unstable control of the chamber and no consensus on what counts as safety.
- Make the next scene about access, not closure.
- Use the abandoned site later as a target for seizure, desecration, or false claims.

### If the party keep relic access inside the party

- Run perimeter defense, anti-seizure pressure, or internal trust strain.
- Make the party leave with maximum independence and minimum external credibility.
- Let outside factions later describe them as hoarders, heretics, or unreliable custodians.

### If the party share relic access with an ally

- Run a joint rite, protected study, or negotiation vulnerable to sabotage.
- Strengthen the chosen ally immediately and anger every excluded faction.
- Bring that alliance back later as debt, oversight, or leaked records.

### If the party let a faction define the chamber truth

- Run a hearing, public claim, or managed retreat shaped by that faction's script.
- Make rivals react fast because chamber truth is now political currency.
- Let the party later struggle against a false or partial narrative that already took root.

## Session 6: The Story That Leaves Avernus

### If the party bring survivors or witnesses with them

- Run escort, protection, or assassination-prevention on the road out.
- Make the party slower but harder to politically isolate.
- Let one witness later validate the party or break under pressure in public.

### If the party leave key people behind

- Run a cleaner departure followed by bad news, seizure, or betrayal from Avernus.
- Put immediate doubt on who now controls the surviving testimony.
- Bring one abandoned figure back later as captive, collaborator, or accusation made flesh.

### If the party tell the truth broadly

- Run panic, denunciation, or crackdown scenes as the news spreads faster than control.
- Advance Sarafan pressure and make Arc II open under scandal.
- Let public knowledge protect the party from quiet erasure while exposing them to louder enemies.

### If the party manage or bury the truth

- Run courier interception, leak hunts, and rumor warfare instead of open scandal.
- Give the party short-term leverage and long-term mistrust.
- Reveal the hidden version later through a leak, forged record, or enemy testimony.
